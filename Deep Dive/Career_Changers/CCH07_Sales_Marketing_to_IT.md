# Career Changer Guide: Sales / Marketing → IT

**`CCH07`** · _Sales / Marketing / Account Management_ **→** _Sales Engineering, MarTech & CRM Administration_ · _6–12 months to first IT job_

> **Who this is for:** You've spent years understanding customer problems, translating solutions into value propositions, and closing deals. You speak product language and understand how technical features translate to business outcomes. You know sales processes, pipelines, and how to communicate with technical teams to drive sales. This guide shows you how to transition these client communication and product expertise skills into IT roles where your ability to bridge sales and technical worlds—a rare combination—is genuinely premium talent: Sales Engineering and CRM/MarTech specialist roles where you make 1.5–2x salesperson salaries.

---

## Your Starting Position

### What your current career gives you

Sales and marketing professionals bring a rare perspective to IT: understanding of customer problems and business outcomes. Most IT people are technically brilliant but can't articulate why customers should care. You can. You've spent years translating technical features into business value, understanding customer pain points, and closing deals. You understand sales processes, you can think about ROI and business impact, you know how to present to executives, and you're comfortable with technical discussions despite not being purely technical. Your ability to communicate clearly, to handle objections, and to understand what customers actually need (not just what they say they need) is genuinely valuable in IT. Additionally, if you have experience with Salesforce, HubSpot, marketing automation platforms, or analytics tools, you already have hands-on technical product knowledge.

### Transferable skills inventory

| Skill from Sales / Marketing | How it maps to IT |
|---|---|
| Understanding customer pain points and problems | Requirements gathering, user-centered system design, solution architecture |
| Translating technical features into business outcomes | Product management, sales engineering, solution positioning |
| Presentation and public speaking skills | Pre-sales demonstrations, conference speaking, technical training |
| Pipeline and deal management | Project management, process management, IT service delivery |
| Client communication and relationship building | Stakeholder management, account management, customer success in IT |
| Product knowledge and learning speed | Learning IT products (Salesforce, HubSpot, cloud platforms) quickly |
| Data interpretation and analytics | Data-driven decision making, analytics, reporting |
| Marketing campaign planning | Go-to-market strategy for IT products, product marketing |
| Problem-solving for customer challenges | Solution architecture, business analysis, technical troubleshooting |

### What you'll need to build from scratch

### Honest difficulty assessment

**Rating: Moderate**

This transition is moderately difficult because your sales background is very different from pure IT support or infrastructure. However, Sales Engineer and CRM Administrator roles are specifically designed for people with your background: you know sales, now you add technical knowledge. Your learning curve is shorter than most career-changers because you understand business language and customer communication already. The main challenge: you need to learn actual technical skills (specific product platforms, APIs, data architecture). You can't succeed on communication skills alone—you must genuinely learn the product and technical concepts.

## Why IT Employers Value Sales/Marketing Backgrounds

Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack, Marketo, and other enterprise software companies explicitly hire sales/marketing professionals as Sales Engineers and Solutions Consultants because they understand the customer. A 2023 LinkedIn analysis found that Sales Engineer job postings prefer "sales background" more than pure technical background. Companies know that brilliant technologists who can't communicate value are less useful than good communicators with solid technical skills.

Real examples: Priya Sharma, a former SaaS sales director, transitioned into Salesforce as a Solutions Engineer; her sales background enabled her to sell technical solutions to customer stakeholders—a skill that took pure developers years to develop. In South Africa, Salesforce partners and consulting firms actively hire sales/marketing professionals into Sales Engineer roles.

Sales professionals transition most successfully into: (1) Sales Engineer / Pre-Sales Consultant roles (highest pay, uses both sales and technical skills), (2) CRM Administrator / Salesforce Administrator roles (learning specific platforms), (3) Solutions Architect (designing technical solutions), (4) MarTech Specialist (marketing automation and data platforms).

## Recommended Career Target

### Primary target: Sales Engineer / Pre-Sales Consultant

**Why this role fits your background:**

Sales Engineer is the natural evolution of your career—you maintain your sales communication skills and client-facing strengths while adding technical depth. You're not learning to code; you're learning to understand and demonstrate technical products to sales and customer audiences. This role values everything you already do (understand customers, communicate value) and asks you to add product/technical knowledge. It's a 1.5–2x salary increase over sales roles, it uses your existing strengths, and it's the easiest IT transition for sales professionals.

**Salary at entry level:**
- USD: $70,000–$105,000/year
- ZAR: R45,000–R67,000/month
- GBP: £49,000–£75,000/year

**Typical employers for this background:**
- Enterprise software vendors (Salesforce, Micro soft, SAP, Oracle)
- SaaS companies (HubSpot, Slack, Intercom)
- Consulting firms (Deloitte, EY, Accenture)
- Systems integrators (Accenture, IBM, Cognizant)
- SA tech vendors and resellers with sales engineering teams

## Interview Preparation

### The "why IT?" question for sales/marketing backgrounds

Employers will be curious: why leave high-commission sales for technical role? You need a compelling answer.

**Framework for your answer:**
"I've spent [X years] understanding what customers need and translating that into solutions. I realized I'm more passionate about the solutions themselves—understanding how technology actually solves problems—than about the selling process. Sales Engineering combines my customer communication skills with technical product expertise. It's where my background in understanding customer needs meets my growing interest in technical solutions. I want to build and optimize the tools that sales teams use, not just sell them."

**Variation for CRM Admin path:**
"My experience implementing CRM tools and seeing how they transform sales teams made me realize I want to specialize in CRM administration and optimization. I want to become the expert who configures systems that actually drive business results."

### Common technical interview questions for Sales Engineer roles

1. **Q: "Walk me through how you'd demo Salesforce to a customer with objections: 'We already use Spreadsheets.'"**
   → **A:** Address the objection: "Spreadsheets work for small teams, but don't scale. I'd show: 1) Real-time visibility across all deals, 2) Automated reporting, 3) Forecast accuracy, 4) Team collaboration features. Let me show you [specific demo of how Salesforce solves these problems]." Show: sales mindset + product knowledge. Study: Salesforce product features and customer pain points.

2. **Q: "Explain Salesforce's core concepts: Accounts, Contacts, Leads, Opportunities."**
   → **A:** Accounts = companies, Contacts = people at companies, Leads = potential customers (early stage), Opportunities = deals in progress. Example: "If IBM is an Account with 5 Contacts, and we're selling software to IBM, that's an Opportunity." Simple, clear explanation. Study: Salesforce data model.

3. **Q: "How would you approach a customer who says, 'Salesforce is too complicated for our team'?"**
   → **A:** Understand concern, offer solutions: 1) Custom configuration to simplify their process, 2) Training program, 3) Managed services support. Show: customer-focused thinking + technical solutions. Study: Salesforce customization options.

4. **Q: "Describe a time you gathered customer requirements and translated them into a technical solution."**
   → **A:** Use a real sales example: "Customer needed visibility into sales pipeline. I: 1) asked questions about their current process, 2) understood pain points, 3) proposed Salesforce with custom reports, 4) explained ROI, 5) implemented solution. They gained [specific outcome]." Shows: customer thinking + technical execution.

5. **Q: "How would you build a business case for a Salesforce implementation?"**
   → **A:** Components: 1) Current state costs (time, errors, lost deals), 2) Salesforce solution benefits (time saved, deal visibility, forecast accuracy), 3) Implementation cost, 4) ROI and payback period. Example with numbers. Study: sales methodology and financial analysis.

### Red flags to avoid in interviews

- **"I know nothing about technology."** Wrong. You're Salesforce Certified. Say: "I bring sales expertise and Salesforce platform knowledge. I'm comfortable learning deeper technical concepts."

- **"I just want to leave sales."** Don't lead with "sales is hard." Lead with: "I'm passionate about understanding how technical solutions drive business outcomes. Sales Engineering combines both interests."

- **"Salesforce is just a CRM database."** Avoid oversimplifying. Show understanding: "Salesforce is a comprehensive customer relationship platform. I understand its core features and how it drives business value."

## South Africa Context

### Is this career change viable in SA?

Absolutely yes. SA has growing SaaS and tech vendor ecosystem. Salesforce partners (Accenture, EY, Deloitte, local SA consultants), HubSpot partners, and tech vendors all hire Sales Engineers. The demand for people who understand both sales and CRM systems is acute—most technical people don't understand sales, most sales people don't understand systems.

BEE/EE: Sales/marketing + tech certifications is good for large corporate targets. Many companies seek "previously advantaged" people with specialized expertise in technical fields.

Remote work: Viable for Sales Engineer roles (remote sales support). International SaaS companies hire SA-based Sales Engineers at USD/GBP rates (R50,000–R100,000+/month).

### SA success story

**Jabu's journey (representative story):**
Jabu was a SaaS sales director at a software company in Johannesburg, earning R55,000/month base + R30,000+ commission. He was successful but burned out from constant travel and client demands. In 2023, at age 37, he realized he was more interested in the product (Salesforce, customer solutions) than the selling process.

He enrolled in Salesforce Trailhead (free) and studied for Salesforce Certified Administrator exam (2 months of part-time study while working, R2,000 spent on practice tests). He passed ADM-201 (cost: R3,600 exam). Total investment: R5,600.

He applied to a Salesforce consulting firm and was hired as a Sales Engineer at R58,000/month base + R15,000 average quarterly bonus. His sales commission disappeared, but his total compensation was higher, and he now worked 40-hour weeks vs. 55+ hour weeks.

Within 18 months, after earning his Salesforce Certified Sales Cloud Consultant, he was promoted to Senior Sales Engineer earning R82,000/month. By year 3, he's earning R105,000/month as Solutions Architect. His 2-month transition investment paid off 100-fold in improved earnings and work-life balance.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Sales has high commission. Will Sales Engineer pay match?**

Probably not in base salary (Sales Engineer is typically salaried, not heavily commission-based). However, Sales Engineer roles often include: base salary + bonus + potential equity (in startups). Total compensation is often comparable or higher than top sales people. Trade-off: more predictable income, better work-life balance, but less upside from huge commission months.

**Q: Do I need to know how to code?**

No. Sales Engineering doesn't require coding. CRM Admin doesn't require coding. You need to understand how systems work and be able to configure/customize them, but not write code. (Salesforce Developer path requires coding, but that's optional specialization.)

**Q: Can I stay in sales and add Sales Engineering skills on the side?**

You could, but split focus reduces effectiveness in both. Full transition to Sales Engineering gives better career prospects and specialization. Many Sales Engineers come from sales but then specialize fully in the technical side.

**Q: What if I want to go deeper technically (CRM Developer)?**

You can. Start with Salesforce Admin, then pursue Salesforce Developer (Apex programming). Takes longer (10–12 months) but opens developer-level salaries (R65,000–R100,000+/month).

**Q: How long before I'm earning more than sales base salary?**

Immediately. Sales Engineer base (R45,000–R67,000) is higher than most sales base salaries. With bonuses, you're likely earning comparable to or more than your top sales years, with more stability and better hours.
