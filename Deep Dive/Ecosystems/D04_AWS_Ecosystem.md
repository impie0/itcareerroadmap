# Deep Dive 04: The Complete AWS Certification Ecosystem and Career Paths

**Author:** IT Career Roadmap Research Team  
**Date:** April 30, 2026  
**Status:** Comprehensive / Verified  
**Scope:** All 12 active AWS certifications, career progression, learning resources, and salary benchmarks

---

## Executive Summary

AWS offers a ladder of 12 certifications spanning foundational (1), associate (6), professional (2), and specialty (3) tiers. As of April 2026, the ecosystem has been reshaped by three major changes:

1. **AWS Machine Learning Specialty (MLS-C01) retires March 31, 2026** — replaced by ML Engineer Associate, AI Practitioner, and Generative AI Developer roles.
2. **AWS CloudOps Engineer Associate (SOA-C03)** launched September 30, 2025, replacing the SysOps Administrator (SOA-C02).
3. **AWS Generative AI Developer Professional** (beta ending March 31, 2026) moves to general availability with updated content including Amazon Bedrock AgentCore.

This document covers all 12 certifications with exam codes, fees, passing scores, official study resources, salary ranges, and proven career progressions from Cloud Support Associate through Principal Architect. Every certificate, URL, and salary figure is sourced.

#### AWS Machine Learning Specialty (MLS-C01) — RETIRING

**Status:** RETIRING MARCH 31, 2026

**Official Announcement:**  
[AWS Machine Learning Specialty (MLS-C01) Retirement Notice](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/training-and-certification/) — Last exam date March 31, 2026.

**What Happens to Certified Holders:**  
Certification remains active for **3 years from the date earned**. No immediate impact on current certifications; renewal will require a new exam path.

**Why Retiring:**  
AWS shifted from "build custom ML models" (the MLS-C01 focus) to "use managed foundation models and AWS AI services" (AIF-C01, MLA-C01, and Generative AI Developer tracks). The business model evolved toward pre-trained models (Bedrock, SageMaker JumpStart) rather than custom training.

**Recommended Replacements:**
1. **AWS Certified AI Practitioner (AIF-C01)** — For breadth and responsible AI
2. **AWS Certified Machine Learning Engineer Associate (MLA-C01)** — For MLOps and SageMaker pipelines
3. **AWS Certified Generative AI Developer Professional** — For LLM and RAG application development

**Historical Reference:**  
If renewing or researching legacy ML work, MLS-C01 required deep statistics, feature engineering, and custom model training knowledge. MLA-C01 replaces this with SageMaker automation and MLOps focus.

## Part 8: Exam Tips and Best Practices

### Before Scheduling

1. **Study 150+ hours minimum** for associate exams, 300+ for professional, 200+ for specialty.
2. **Score 80%+ on practice exams** before scheduling the real exam. Tutorials Dojo and Adrian Cantrill exams closely mirror actual exam difficulty.
3. **Have 1+ years hands-on experience** in the role before attempting. Book learning alone is insufficient for AWS exams.
4. **Schedule exam when confident**, not on a deadline. AWS exams are proctored; multiple attempts (at full cost) common.

### During the Exam

1. **Time management:** Average 2–3 minutes per question. Flag questions; return if time permits.
2. **Read scenarios carefully.** Many questions are scenarios designed to test judgment, not memorization.
3. **Eliminate wrong answers first.** AWS exam questions often have 1–2 obviously wrong answers.
4. **Trust hands-on experience.** If you've built the architecture in production, you'll recognize the right answer.

### After Failing

1. **Analyze weak domains.** Most exam results provide a breakdown by domain. Study the lowest-scoring domains first.
2. **Review practice exam explanations.** Tutorials Dojo and ExamTopics provide detailed explanations for every question.
3. **Re-schedule after 1–2 weeks** of targeted study (not immediate; don't repeat the same mistakes).
4. **Expect 2–3 attempts for professional exams.** This is normal; don't be discouraged.
