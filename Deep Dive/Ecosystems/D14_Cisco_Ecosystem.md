## Overview & 2026 Updates

Cisco has undergone a comprehensive certification overhaul in early 2026 to align credentials with AI and automation demands, simplify naming, and provide clearer career progression pathways.

### Key 2026 Changes

**DevNet → Automation Rebrand (February 3, 2026)**  
On February 3, 2026, the entire DevNet certification track was rebranded as the Automation track. All active DevNet Associate, Professional, and Expert holders automatically became CCNA Automation, CCNP Automation, and CCIE Automation certified—no additional exams required. The Professional core exam changed from DEVCOR (350-901 v1.x) to AUTOCOR (350-901 v2.0), with new emphases on AI-ready networking and infrastructure-as-code practices.

**CCNP Enterprise ENCOR v1.2 Release (March 19, 2026)**  
ENCOR v1.1 retired on March 18, 2026; v1.2 became the new standard on March 19, 2026. The major change: **removal of all wireless content**. Wireless design, QoS, security, and troubleshooting topics were stripped from ENCOR to tighten focus on enterprise core technologies (Architecture, Virtualization, Infrastructure, Network Assurance, Security, and Automation+AI). Wireless now falls under dedicated CCNP Wireless and CCIE Wireless credentials (launched March 19, 2026).

**Cybersecurity & Collaboration Unification**  
Cisco aligned cybersecurity under CCNA/CCNP Cybersecurity (February 2026 rebrand). Collaboration exams were updated to v2.0 (CLCOR 350-801 v2.0, effective February 3, 2026) to include cloud collaboration and updated IP Phone/UC endpoints.

## Entry Level: CCST

The **Cisco Certified Support Technician (CCST)** is the new foundational credential, designed to validate work-ready skills after ~150 hours of instruction. It bridges the gap between academic learning and hands-on IT roles. Three flavors exist; you pursue the one matching your target career.

### CCST IT Support (100-140)

**Purpose:** Prepare for entry-level IT help desk, end-user support, and computer support specialist roles.

**Exam Details:**
- **Exam Code:** 100-140
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Format:** Multiple-choice + performance-based questions
- **Passing Score:** Typically 825/1000
- **Fee:** ~$150–165 USD (varies by region; contact Pearson VUE for exact pricing)
- **Validity:** 3 years from passing

**Topics Covered:**
- Hardware troubleshooting (PCs, peripherals, connectivity)
- Operating system support (Windows, macOS, Linux basics)
- User support workflows and ticketing
- Basic network connectivity (Ethernet, Wi-Fi, TCP/IP)
- Security threat identification and reporting
- Mobile device support

**Career Fit:** Help Desk Analyst, IT Support Technician, Desktop Support Specialist, Junior IT Administrator

**Preparation:**
- Official study materials: Cisco Learning Network
- Books: "31 Days Before your Cisco Certified Support Technician (CCST) IT Support Exam" (Cisco Press)
- Free: Cisco Networking Academy (netacad.com) — courses available at partner schools and online

### CCST Networking (100-150)

**Purpose:** Entry-level networking technician pathway; foundational for CCNA.

**Exam Details:**
- **Exam Code:** 100-150
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Passing Score:** Typically 825/1000
- **Fee:** ~$150–165 USD
- **Validity:** 3 years

**Topics Covered:**
- OSI model layers 1–4
- Ethernet, IPv4, IPv6, DHCP, DNS
- Routing & switching fundamentals
- Basic network configuration and troubleshooting
- Network security principles
- Cabling standards and physical infrastructure

**Career Fit:** Network Support Technician, Junior Network Administrator, NOC Technician, Network Operations Analyst

**Official Study Material:**
- "Cisco Certified Support Technician Networking 100-150 Official Cert Guide" by Russ White (Cisco Press)
- Cisco Networking Academy CCST Networking course (netacad.com)

### CCST Cybersecurity (100-160)

**Purpose:** Entry into cybersecurity support and threat detection roles.

**Exam Details:**
- **Exam Code:** 100-160
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Passing Score:** Typically 825/1000
- **Fee:** ~$150–165 USD

**Topics Covered:**

## Associate Level: CCNA

The **Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA)** is the industry-standard associate-level credential for network engineers and infrastructure professionals. It validates foundational knowledge of routing, switching, IP services, security fundamentals, and (as of 2026) automation readiness.

### CCNA 200-301

**Exam Details:**
- **Exam Code:** 200-301 v1.1 (current)
- **Duration:** 120 minutes
- **Questions:** ~55–65 questions (mix of multiple-choice, drag-and-drop, simulations)
- **Passing Score:** 825/1000
- **Fee:** $300–330 USD (varies slightly by country/currency)
  - UK: ~£247
  - EU: ~€291
  - Australia: ~$484 AUD
  - India: ~₹25,963
- **Validity:** 3 years
- **Exam Format:** Pearson VUE (in-person or online proctored)

**Topics & Weight (approximate):**
- Network Fundamentals (15%)
- Network Access (20%)
- IP Connectivity (25%)
- IP Services (10%)
- Security Fundamentals (15%)
- Automation and Programmability (15%)

**Key Additions for 2026:**
- Expanded automation + AI topics (network programmability, Python basics, APIs, YANG, NETCONF)
- Implicit trust zero concepts
- Cloud connectivity fundamentals
- IoT security awareness

**Career Path Fit:**
- Network Engineer (junior to mid-level)
- Network Administrator
- Systems Engineer (with some systems background)
- NOC Engineer
- Cloud Network Engineer (with cloud training)

**Retake Policy:**
- Mandatory 5-day waiting period between attempts
- Unlimited retakes allowed
- Full fee ($300–330) per retake

**Official Study Materials:**
- **CCNA 200-301 Official Cert Guide Library (2nd Edition)** by Wendell Odom (Cisco Press, 2023)
  - Two volumes: comprehensive coverage, 800+ practice questions, 8+ hours video, network simulator
  - Considered the gold standard; used by most serious candidates
- Cisco Networking Academy CCNA course (netacad.com) — free at partner institutions
- Cisco Learning Network (learningnetwork.cisco.com) — free forum + study groups

**Paid Learning (Popular Vendors):**
- **CBT Nuggets:** CCNA training videos ($500–800 annual subscription)
- **INE:** CCNA courses + full lab environment ($500–2000 depending on tier)
- **Udemy:** Video courses ($10–60 on sale)
- **Boson ExSim-Max:** Practice exams with detailed explanations (~$150)
- **Kaplan (VCE):** Practice tests (~$100–150)

**Typical Total Cost:**
- Exam fee: $330
- Study materials: $200–500 (books + video courses)
- Practice exams: $50–150
- **Total: ~$600–1000 for a focused, self-paced study plan**

## Professional Level: CCNP

The **Cisco Certified Network Professional (CCNP)** is the specialist-level credential for advanced network engineers. Since 2020, Cisco has offered separate CCNP tracks by domain (Enterprise, Service Provider, Data Center, Security, Collaboration, Wireless, Automation) rather than a single "CCNP R&S."

All CCNP tracks follow this model:
1. Pass a **Core exam** (e.g., ENCOR for Enterprise, SPCOR for Service Provider)
2. Pass **one Concentration exam** from the domain (e.g., ENARSI, ENSDWI, ENSLD for Enterprise)
3. Earn CCNP [Domain] credential

### CCNP Enterprise

The largest and most popular CCNP track, covering enterprise network architecture, design, and advanced troubleshooting.

#### Core: ENCOR 350-401 v1.2 (March 19, 2026)

**Exam Details:**
- **Code:** 350-401
- **Version:** v1.2 (released March 19, 2026; v1.1 retired March 18)
- **Duration:** 120 minutes
- **Questions:** ~80–100 questions
- **Passing Score:** 825/1000
- **Fee:** ~$330 USD
- **Validity:** 3 years
- **Prerequisite:** CCNA (or equivalent) recommended; no hard requirement

**Major Changes in v1.2:**
- **Wireless content removed entirely** (now in dedicated CCNP Wireless/CCIE Wireless)
- "Automation and Artificial Intelligence" now the explicit 6th major domain
- New emphasis: AI-assisted network analytics, ML-based anomaly detection
- Enhanced SD-WAN and security services module (reflecting market adoption)

**Topics (6 domains, ~15% each):**
1. **Architecture** — Multi-tier design, fabric, overlay networks, SD-WAN, Segment Routing
2. **Virtualization** — VRF, VXLAN, Segment Routing (SRv6), SD-Access architecture
3. **Infrastructure** — EIGRP, OSPF, BGP, multicast, QoS, IP SLA
4. **Network Assurance** — NetFlow, sFlow, Cisco Tetration, ThousandEyes, troubleshooting methodologies
5. **Security** — Access control, zone-based firewalls, routing security, infrastructure ACLs
6. **Automation and AI** — Python, YANG, NETCONF, REST APIs, Ansible, Terraform, Cisco DNA Center automation

**Study Materials:**
- CCNP Enterprise Core ENCOR 350-401 Official Cert Guide v1.2 (Cisco Press, updated for v1.2)
- CBT Nuggets CCNP Enterprise Core (video training)
- INE CCNP Enterprise track
- Boson ExSim-Max practice exams

#### Concentration Exams (Choose One)

## Expert Level: CCIE

The **Cisco Certified Internetwork Expert (CCIE)** is the gold standard—the highest individual technical certification Cisco offers. CCIE is a two-part exam (written + 8-hour lab) and is only offered in specific technology tracks.

### CCIE Offerings (2026)

**Available Tracks:**
1. **CCIE Enterprise Infrastructure** (most common)
2. **CCIE Service Provider**
3. **CCIE Data Center**
4. **CCIE Security**
5. **CCIE Collaboration**
6. **CCIE Wireless** (relaunched March 2026)
7. **CCIE Automation** (formerly DevNet Expert; rebranded Feb 3, 2026)

### Exam Structure & Cost

**Part 1: Written Exam**
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Questions:** ~90–110 multiple-choice
- **Passing Score:** 790/1000
- **Fee:** $400 USD
- **Validity of Pass:** 18 months (gives you time to schedule and pass the lab)

**Part 2: Lab Exam**
- **Duration:** 8 hours (grueling, hands-on testing)
- **Format:** Hands-on configuration, troubleshooting, and design on real Cisco equipment
- **Passing Score:** 1000 points (not percentage-based; Cisco defines the cut-off)
- **Fee Options:**
  - **Cisco-provided mobile lab (with Cisco kit):** $1,900 USD
  - **Bring Your Own Device (BYOD):** $1,600 USD (if approved setup used)
- **Validity:** Valid for 3 years after passing

**Total CCIE Cost Per Attempt:**
- Written: $400
- Lab: $1,600–1,900
- **Minimum out-of-pocket: $2,000 per attempt** (not including study materials, travel, or retakes)

**Typical Total Investment to Earn CCIE:**
- Study materials, courses, lab subscriptions: $2,500–5,000
- Exam attempts (assume 2–4 tries): $4,000–8,000
- Travel & accommodation (if lab at testing center): $500–2,000
- **Total typical budget: $7,000–15,000**

Some candidates spend $20,000+ due to multiple retakes or premium training programs.

### CCIE Written Exam Topics (Example: Enterprise Infrastructure)

**Major domains** (each ~10–15%):
- Network fundamentals
- Advanced routing (EIGRP, OSPF, BGP)
- VPN technologies
- Infrastructure security
- Infrastructure services (QoS, multicast, IP SLA)
- Infrastructure automation
- Cloud connectivity

### CCIE Lab Exam (8 hours)

The lab is the ultimate proving ground:
- **Troubleshooting section (~2 hours):** 4–6 broken network scenarios; you diagnose and fix
- **Configuration section (~6 hours):** Build an enterprise network from topology diagram and detailed requirements

Candidates bring minimal notes; the focus is hands-on skill, not memorization. Lab labs are scheduled at Cisco testing centers worldwide (major cities only).

### CCIE Career Impact & Salary

CCIE holders command premium salaries and career opportunities:
- Typical base salary: $130,000–$185,000 USD
- Total compensation (bonus, stock, benefits): $180,000–$280,000+ USD
- Availability: CCIE-level roles often require relocation or frequent travel
- Typical job titles: Senior Network Architect, Principal Engineer, Network Solutions Architect, Consulting Engineer

## Design Expert: CCDE

The **Cisco Certified Design Expert (CCDE)** is an alternative expert-level credential focused on **network design** rather than implementation/troubleshooting.

**Exam Code:** 400-007 v3.0 (current)  
**Target:** Architects and senior engineers who excel at design but may not have deep lab skills

### CCDE Written Exam

- **Duration:** 2 hours (120 minutes)
- **Questions:** ~90–110 scenario-based multiple-choice
- **Passing Score:** Data-driven; typically ~70–75% to pass
- **Fee:** ~$400 USD (similar to CCIE written)
- **Format:** Pearson VUE
- **Validity:** 18 months

### CCDE Lab Exam (Optional)

- **Name:** CCDE Practicum
- **Duration:** 4 hours (half the length of CCIE lab)
- **Format:** Design scenarios; you propose and justify network designs
- **Fee:** $1,600 USD (if pursuing full CCDE Expert credential)

### CCDE Topics

- Enterprise network design (hierarchy, redundancy, scale)
- Routing and switching design
- Security design
- Data center and cloud design
- Service provider network design (for CCDE SP track)
- Design trade-offs and risk assessment

### Career Fit

CCDE is popular among:
- Solutions architects (pre-sales engineering)
- Enterprise architects
- Consulting architects
- Technical trainers and instructors
- Network design specialists in large organizations

**Salary:** Similar to CCIE ($130,000–$180,000+ base), though CCIE often commands a slight premium due to implementation depth.

## Career Progression Pathways

### Typical Progression (IT Professional → Network Architect)

```
CCST Networking
       ↓ (150–200 hours study; 1–2 months)
    CCNA 200-301
       ↓ (300–400 hours study; 3–6 months)
CCNP Enterprise (ENCOR + 1 Concentration)
       ↓ (600–800 hours study; 6–12 months)
 CCIE Enterprise
  (Written + 8-hour Lab)
       ↓ (1,000+ hours study; 12–24 months)
 Principal Architect / Consulting Engineer
```

**Timeline:** CCST → CCIE typically takes 2–4 years for high-performing, focused candidates.

**Security Focus:**
```
CCST Cybersecurity → CCNA Cybersecurity → CCNP Security → CCIE Security
```

**Cloud + Automation Focus:**
```
CCNA → CCNP Automation → CCIE Automation
  (often combined with cloud certifications: AWS Solutions Architect, AZ-104, etc.)
```

**Data Center Focus:**
```
CCNA → CCNP Data Center → CCIE Data Center
  (often combined with: Cisco ACI certifications, VMware VCP, Kubernetes certifications)
```

**Service Provider / Carrier Focus:**
```
CCNA → CCNP Service Provider → CCIE Service Provider
  (often combined with: Juniper JNCIE, Arista, open-source routing [FRRouting])
```

### Career Pivot: From Other IT Domains

**From Sysadmin/Cloud:**
- Strong foundation in automation and infrastructure-as-code accelerates CCNA→CCNP Automation
- AWS/Azure cloud networking experience transfers well to ENCOR study
- Recommend: CCNA → CCNP Automation (rather than CCNP Enterprise) → potential CCIE Automation

**From Security (SOC/Incident Response):**
- CCNA Cybersecurity → CCNP Security (natural fit)
- CCIE Security combines network security + threat intelligence
- Recommend: Simultaneously pursue CEH or OSCP alongside CCNP Security for hands-on hacking depth

**From Software Engineering / DevOps:**
- Strong Python, CI/CD, and container skills give advantage in CCNP Automation / CCIE Automation
- Network fundamentals (CCNA) are still required entry point
- Recommend: Self-study CCNA, then CCNP Automation (leveraging existing automation skills)

## Certification Maintenance & Renewal

All Cisco certifications (except CCST, which has no renewal requirement) require renewal every 3 years.

### Renewal Options

1. **Pass the current-version exam** (same fee as initial exam)
2. **Pass a higher-level exam** in the same domain (e.g., passing CCNP renews CCNA)
3. **Accumulate Continuing Education (CE) credits** via:
   - Passing any Cisco exam in the same tier or higher
   - Completing Cisco Learning Network courses (limited CE credits)
   - Publishing technical articles (1–5 credits)
   - Speaking at conferences (5–10 credits)
   - Teaching (CE credits vary)

**Example:** If you earn CCNA in 2024, you must renew it by 2027 by passing CCNA again, passing CCNP, or accumulating 40 CE credits.

## Conclusion & Recommendations

### For Career Starters (College Grad or Career Changer)

1. **Pursue CCST** first (150 hours, 1–2 months) → land first IT job as help desk, NOC tech, or junior network admin
2. **Pursue CCNA** next (300–400 hours, 3–6 months on the job) → transition to mid-level network engineer role
3. **Pursue CCNP** once you have 3+ years of hands-on experience → move to senior engineer or architect

**Realistic timeline:** CCST → CCNA → CCNP over 3–4 years with on-the-job learning

### For Mid-Career IT Professionals

If you're a sysadmin, cloud engineer, or security analyst considering a network specialization:

- **Cloud/Automation background?** → CCNA → CCNP Automation (6–9 months total)
- **Security background?** → CCNA → CCNP Security (9–12 months total)
- **Generalist IT?** → CCNA → CCNP Enterprise (12–18 months total)

All lead to $120K–$160K+ salaries by year 2.

### For Enterprise Architects & Consultants

- **CCDE** is faster than CCIE if your strength is design over hands-on configuration
- **CCIE Enterprise** is still the gold standard for prestige and premium compensation
- Both command $130K–$200K+ base salaries

### For DevOps & Platform Engineers

- CCNP Automation (formerly DevNet Professional) is your primary target
- Combine with Kubernetes (CKA), AWS (Solutions Architect Professional), or Terraform (HashiCorp Certified) for $160K–$200K+ total comp

### Free vs. Paid Trade-Off

- **Free approach:** Cisco Networking Academy + Cisco DevNet Sandbox + Jeremy's IT Lab YouTube = viable for CCNA, but time-intensive (8–12 months)
- **Recommended hybrid:** Cisco Networking Academy (free structured curriculum) + Wendell Odom books ($120) + Boson exams ($150) + 3 months CBT Nuggets ($75) = **~$350 total, high success rate**
- **Premium fast-track:** INE all-access ($2,000) completed in 6 months = justified if your employer reimburses
