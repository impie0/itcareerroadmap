## Acquisition & Strategic Context

On **July 2, 2025**, Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE) **successfully completed** its acquisition of Juniper Networks for **USD 14 billion**. This transaction represents one of the largest networking industry consolidations in recent history and reshapes the competitive landscape for enterprise networking, SASE, and data center infrastructure.

### Deal Closure & DOJ Settlement

The deal faced regulatory scrutiny from the U.S. Department of Justice (DoJ) but was ultimately approved following a settlement agreement reached just before trial. As part of the settlement, HPE was required to **divest its Instant On wireless LAN (WLAN) campus and branch network switching business**, including all associated assets, intellectual property, R&D employees, and customer relationships to a DoJ-approved buyer within 180 days. HPE was also mandated to **license Juniper's AI Ops for Mist source code**.

### Strategic Rationale

The combined entity creates a comprehensive, cloud-native, AI-driven networking and security portfolio. HPE Aruba Networking now spans:
- Enterprise wired and wireless campus access (Aruba CX, APs, Aruba Central)
- Data center and service provider routing (Juniper MX, PTX)
- Security-first SASE platforms (Aruba + Juniper consolidated)
- Network AI operations (Mist AI) integrated into management stacks

**Leadership:** Rami Rahim, former Juniper Networks CEO, now leads the combined HPE Networking business, operating under two branded segments: **HPE Aruba Networking** and **HPE Juniper Networking**.

## Post-Merger Organization & AI Integration

### Aruba Networking Central + Mist AI ("Build Once, Deploy Twice")

HPE announced a "build once, deploy twice" approach effective in Q1 2026, with shared capabilities between Aruba Central and Juniper Mist platforms rolling out progressively. The key innovation is **transplanting Mist's LEM (Learning Engine for Models)** into Aruba Central, enabling AIOps that understand context—for example, predicting call disconnections during Microsoft Teams video conferences based on interference pattern analysis.

### Unified SASE Strategy

HPE Aruba Networking's **unified SASE** (Secure Access Service Edge) converges:
- **SD-WAN** (via Aruba EdgeConnect, formerly Silver Peak)
- **Zero Trust Network Access** (ZTNA) via policy engine
- **Security Service Edge** (SSE) with Secure Web Gateway (SWG), Cloud Access Security Broker (CASB), and Data Loss Prevention (DLP)
- **Network Access Control** (NAC) via ClearPass

When paired with Aruba Central, the SASE platform applies zero trust principles to all users and devices using role-based controls and AI profiling. Industry projections suggest that by 2026, **85% of organizations** seeking CASB, secure web gateway, or ZTNA will obtain these from a converged solution rather than separate vendors.

### Subscription & CapEx-to-OpEx Shift

HPE GreenLake for Aruba Networking allows organizations to convert from capital expenditure (CapEx) to operating expenditure (OpEx) models via unified subscriptions combining hardware, software, management, and monitoring under fixed monthly payments (3- and 5-year terms). As of January 2026, Aruba CX Switching solutions are available as a service through HPE GreenLake, targeting retail and enterprise segments.

## Certification Ladder

Aruba certifications follow a clear progression from Associate through Professional, Expert, and Specialist tiers. All certifications are valid for **three years** from issuance; recertification is achieved by retaking the current exam or passing a higher-level certification.

### Associate Level

**Aruba Certified Associate – Campus Access (ACA)**
- **Exam Code:** HPE6-A85
- **Focus:** Foundational wired and wireless campus access skills
- **Candidates:** Network field technicians, deployment technicians, NOC Level 1, junior network engineers with ≥1 year hands-on experience
- **Content:** 
  - Basic routing and switching
  - RF (radio frequency) concepts
  - Basic security principles
  - Connectivity and monitoring/reporting
- **Exam:** PearsonVUE-delivered; typically 60–75 questions, 2 hours, ~60% pass threshold
- **Validity:** 3 years

**Related ACA Specializations:**
- ACA – Mobility (wireless focus)
- ACA – ClearPass (network access control)
- ACA – EdgeConnect (SD-WAN fundamentals)

### Professional Level

**Aruba Certified Professional – Campus Access (ACP)**
- **Exam Code:** HPE7-A01
- **Focus:** Design, implementation, and advanced troubleshooting of modern campus networks
- **Prerequisites:** Recommended (not mandatory) to first pass ACA or have equivalent experience
- **Content Distribution:**
  - Configuring and using Mobility Conductor and Mobility Controller: 25%
  - Implementing and operating secure wireless LAN (WLAN) features: 20%
  - Managing and monitoring WLAN: 30%
  - Configuring and optimizing network performance: 15%
  - Troubleshooting WLAN connectivity and performance issues: 10%
- **Exam:** PearsonVUE; 75 questions, 2 hours, 68% pass threshold
- **Validity:** 3 years

**Other ACP Specializations:**
- ACP – Mobility (advanced wireless design)
- ACP – ClearPass (HPE6-A85 exam code for NAC design and deployment)
- ACP – EdgeConnect SD-WAN

### Expert Level

**Aruba Certified Mobility Expert (ACMX)**
- **Exam Code:** HPE4-A38 (hands-on lab exam)
- **Focus:** Expert-level design and deployment of scalable Mobile First Networking solutions
- **Prerequisites:** Minimum 3–5 years experience designing and integrating complex Aruba Mobile First Networks
- **Target Audience:** Senior architects and enterprise wireless network designers
- **Exam Format:** 8-hour rigorous, **scenario-driven practical (hands-on) exam**—no written exam component
- **Content:** Plan, implement, and troubleshoot enterprise multi-site Aruba campus wireless networks, remote-access, and multi-tenant environments; analyze functional requirements to create network design and implementation plans
- **Assessment:** ArubaOS 8.6 configuration, design, and troubleshooting in a real-world lab environment
- **Validity:** 3 years
- **Credential:** Digital badge via HPE Credly system, shareable on LinkedIn, resume, email signature

**Aruba Certified Expert (ACE)** – Top-tier validation of enterprise-scale architecture, design, and deployment expertise across the entire Aruba product portfolio. (Specific exam code and prerequisites available via HPE Certification Learning portal.)

### Specialist & Industry-Aligned Certifications

**Aruba Certified SD-WAN Professional (ACSDP)**
- Focus on EdgeConnect SD-WAN (formerly Silver Peak) design, deployment, and operations
- Relevant for organizations migrating from traditional WANs to software-defined edge

**HPE Certified Sales Professional (HCSP) – Aruba Track**
- For sales engineers and account executives; emphasizes business case, competitive positioning, and solution stacking

## Product Portfolio

### Wired Campus Access

**Aruba CX Switches (AOS-CX)**
- Cloud-managed, non-blocking switching from 1GbE to 100GbE
- Fixed-port and modular chassis options for branch, campus, and data center deployments
- REST API and model-driven configuration for automation
- Integration with Aruba Central for zero-touch provisioning (ZTP) and telemetry
- Support for 802.1X, dynamic segmentation, and policy-based forwarding

### Wireless Access Points

**Aruba 700 Series Access Points**
- Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be) tri-band operation (2.4 GHz, 5 GHz, 6 GHz)
- Designed for high-density, mission-critical enterprise and campus environments
- Up to 9 Gbps aggregate throughput
- Integrated IoT radios (Thread, Zigbee, BLE) for IoT device management
- 5 GbE uplink connectivity

**Aruba 740 Series Wi-Fi 7 Campus Access Points**
- Enterprise-grade, cloud-managed via Aruba Central
- Advanced security integration (ClearPass, ZTNA, micro-segmentation)
- Dual 10 GbE uplinks for high-throughput deployments

### Cloud Management & Orchestration

**Aruba Central**
- Cloud-native network management for wired, wireless, and branch edge devices
- Zero-touch provisioning (ZTP) via templates
- AI Insights and AIOps for predictive analytics and automated troubleshooting
- ClearPass integration for enhanced device context and policy
- Role-based access control (RBAC) and multi-tenant support

**Aruba Orchestrator (SD-WAN)**
- Unified management and orchestration for EdgeConnect SD-WAN edge devices
- Business Intent Overlay (BIO) definition for application-aware WAN policies
- Tunnel bonding for link aggregation and QoS
- Integration with cloud security platforms (Netskope, Zscaler, Prisma Access)

### Network Access Control & Identity

**Aruba ClearPass**
- Next-generation 802.1X NAC for LAN and WLAN authentication and authorization
- Role-based policy enforcement for users, guests, and IoT devices
- Integration with Active Directory, LDAP, and multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Profiling and device intelligence for context-aware policy decisions
- Available in on-premises appliance or cloud-managed SaaS variants

### SD-WAN & Branch Edge

**Aruba EdgeConnect SD-WAN**
- Formerly Silver Peak EdgeConnect; now fully integrated into Aruba Networking
- Business Intent Overlay model for policy abstraction from underlying transport
- Support for broadband, MPLS, LTE, and satellite WAN links
- Unified management via Aruba Orchestrator
- Cloud-first design with integration into SASE stack (SSE, ZTNA, SWG, CASB, DLP)

### Security Service Edge (SSE)

**Aruba Networking SSE / SASE**
- Integrated Secure Web Gateway (SWG) for threat prevention and URL filtering
- Cloud Access Security Broker (CASB) for cloud application visibility and control
- Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA) for application-level segmentation
- Data Loss Prevention (DLP) policies for sensitive data protection
- Available via HPE GreenLake subscription or hybrid on-premises/cloud deployment

### HPE GreenLake for Networking (NaaS)

**Service Pack Model:**
- Combines hardware, software, management, monitoring, and professional services into unified monthly subscription
- Pre-designed packs for campus, branch, data center, and retail use cases
- 3- and 5-year fixed-payment terms with flexible capacity adjustments via change orders
- Consumption-based billing and OpEx advantages

## Career Roles & Salary Ranges

Aruba specialization is increasingly common for enterprise network professionals, with distinct career paths in campus access, wireless, SD-WAN, and security architecture. Salary ranges reflect U.S. market data as of early 2026.

### Junior Network Technician / Field Technician

**Typical Responsibilities:**
- Basic switch and AP deployment and configuration
- Hands-on cable management and wireless site surveys
- Device onboarding via Aruba Central templates

**Salary Range:** USD 65,000 – 90,000 / year
**Certifications:** ACA or equivalent foundation

---

### Aruba Network Engineer

**Typical Responsibilities:**
- Design and implement campus wired/wireless networks
- Aruba CX switch configuration, VLAN/trunk design, spanning tree
- AP placement, RF optimization, roaming tuning
- ClearPass policy authoring and troubleshooting
- Capacity planning and network telemetry analysis

**Salary Range:** USD 95,000 – 145,000 / year
**Certifications:** ACP (Campus Access) or ACP (Mobility); often pursuing or holding ACMX

---

### Senior Network Engineer / Aruba Specialist

**Typical Responsibilities:**
- Multi-site network design and deployment (campus, branch, remote access)
- Advanced RF design for high-density and outdoor environments
- ACMX-level troubleshooting and optimization
- Mentoring junior engineers; technical escalation path

**Salary Range:** USD 140,000 – 180,000 / year
**Certifications:** ACMX highly valued; ACE or pursuing ACE

---

### Network Architect / Aruba Solutions Architect

**Typical Responsibilities:**
- Enterprise network architecture using Aruba portfolio (wired + wireless + SD-WAN + SASE)
- Business requirements translation to technical design
- Vendor selection and RFP evaluation
- Integration of Aruba solutions with third-party security (Zscaler, Netskope, Prisma Access)
- Post-acquisition integration (e.g., Aruba + Juniper convergence planning)

**Salary Range:** USD 170,000 – 220,000 / year
**Certifications:** ACE or equivalent; often hold multiple professional certs (CISSP, CCNP)

## 2026 Strategic Roadmap

### AI & Mist Integration Acceleration

- **Q1 2026:** Full rollout of Mist's LEM (Learning Engine for Models) into Aruba Central; AIOps capabilities reach parity between Aruba and Juniper platforms
- **Ongoing:** Predictive analytics for network health, call quality prediction (Teams/Zoom), interference forecasting, and anomaly detection
- **Impact:** Reduced operational overhead; proactive incident prevention rather than reactive troubleshooting

### Wi-Fi 7 Rollout & 6 GHz Adoption

- **2026 Focus:** Aruba 700/740 Series Wi-Fi 7 deployment in high-density campuses, retail, and hospitality
- **Benefits:** Higher per-AP throughput (9+ Gbps); reduced interference via 6 GHz spectrum; support for next-gen IoT and mobile devices
- **Cross-portfolio:** Integration with Aruba Central for unified telemetry and policy across Wi-Fi generations

### ZTNA & SSE Expansion

- **2026 Maturity:** Aruba SASE platform (SD-WAN + ZTNA + SWG + CASB + DLP) increasingly positioned as comprehensive alternative to point-tool stacking
- **Integration:** Unified policy engine via Aruba Central; simplified operations for enterprises
- **Competitive:** Direct challenge to Fortinet Secure SD-WAN and Cisco Secure SD-WAN + cloud security integrations

### HPE GreenLake Unified Subscription Model

- **NaaS Acceleration:** Move from perpetual licensing to subscription-based consumption (CapEx → OpEx)
- **Service Packs:** Pre-configured bundles for campus, branch, retail, and data center
- **Flexibility:** Easy capacity adjustments; multi-year pricing locks in cost predictability

### Post-Juniper Integration Milestones

- **Instant On Divestiture:** Completion of HPE's required sale of Instant On WLAN business (180-day window from July 2, 2025 = by ~January 2026)
- **Juniper Mist AI Source Code Licensing:** HPE licensed to use Juniper's proprietary AI algorithms; integration into unified Central/Mist stack
- **Organizational Clarity:** Finalization of "HPE Aruba Networking" vs. "HPE Juniper Networking" portfolio segmentation and go-to-market strategy

### Certification Evolution

- **New Exam Rollout:** ACSDP (Aruba Certified SD-WAN Professional) gaining traction as enterprises adopt EdgeConnect
- **SASE-Focused Certs:** Potential new certification tracks combining ZTNA, NAC, and cloud security (aligned with industry shift toward converged SASE)
- **Juniper-Aruba Cross-Certification:** Potential hybrid credentials recognizing expertise across both platforms post-merger

## Competitive Landscape

Aruba competes across multiple networking domains, with competitive intensity varying by segment.

### vs. Cisco

- **Advantage Cisco:** Largest installed base; broadest portfolio (switching, routing, firewalls, cloud security); Cisco Learning Network (CLN) deep training ecosystem; Meraki for SMB/branch
- **Advantage Aruba:** Cloud-first approach (Aruba Central vs. Cisco on-prem DNA Center); Mist AI integration for predictive analytics; ClearPass agility for NAC; EdgeConnect (formerly Silver Peak) competitive in SD-WAN against Cisco SD-WAN
- **Overlap:** Campus switching and wireless dominance; SASE/SD-WAN convergence; enterprise sales cycles

### vs. Juniper

- **Now Sister Company:** Post-July 2025 acquisition, Aruba and Juniper operate as distinct brands under HPE Networking umbrella
- **Complementary Strengths:** Juniper leads in service provider and data center routing; Aruba leads in enterprise campus and branch; combined SASE play stronger than either alone
- **AI Convergence:** Mist AI (Juniper) being integrated into Aruba Central; eventual potential for unified AIOps across portfolio
- **Internal Positioning:** "HPE Aruba Networking" and "HPE Juniper Networking" brands maintained; channel and customer segmentation ongoing

### vs. Fortinet

- **FortiGate/FortiSwitch:** Fortinet's integrated NGFW + IPS + AV + SD-WAN + ZTNA in single box appeals to simplified operations
- **Advantage Fortinet:** Consolidation appeal (one box, one policy engine); strong SMB/mid-market footprint
- **Advantage Aruba:** Enterprise scale and complexity (multi-site, heterogeneous); cloud-managed architecture; open integration with best-of-breed security (Netskope, Zscaler, Prisma Access)
- **Overlap:** SD-WAN, SASE, NAC market share battles; SMB branch consolidation

### vs. Extreme Networks

- **Competitive Position:** Extreme positioned as challenger/niche player in switching/wireless
- **Extreme Cloud IQ:** Cloud-managed platform (analog to Aruba Central)
- **Market Share:** Smaller than Aruba, Cisco, or Juniper; strong in specific verticals (retail, education) and regions (Asia-Pacific)
- **Overlap:** Campus switching, wireless, and network management; price-sensitive segments

### vs. Arista

- **Market Position:** Arista dominates in high-speed data center switching (25G/40G/100G) and cloud networking
- **Limited Overlap:** Arista focuses on service provider, cloud hyperscaler, and data center; Aruba focuses on enterprise campus/branch
- **Potential Convergence:** As enterprises adopt DC-as-a-service and edge computing, overlap may increase in future
