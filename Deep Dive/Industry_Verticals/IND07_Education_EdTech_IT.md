# Deep Dive: Education & EdTech IT Careers

**Field:** K-12, Higher Education, Corporate Learning & Development | **Growth:** Accelerating since pandemic | **AI Impact:** High (Khanmigo, MagicSchool, tutoring automation) | **Remote-Friendly:** Moderate (hybrid common; infrastructure roles on-site)

---

## I. Sector Overview

Education IT spans three distinct contexts, each with different technology stacks, compliance regimens, and career pathways:

### 1. K-12 Public/Private Schools
- Technology directors, IT coordinators, support staff manage 500–10,000+ endpoint devices
- Primary systems: SIS (Student Information Systems), LMS (Learning Management Systems), network/WiFi, video conferencing, endpoint protection
- Pressure: budget constraints, aging infrastructure, educator upskilling bottlenecks
- Regulatory: FERPA (student records), COPPA (children's privacy under 13 in online services)

### 2. Higher Education (Universities/Colleges)
- Sophisticated ERP ecosystems (Workday, Banner, Oracle PeopleSoft)
- Research computing, data integration, campus APIs
- Compliance: FERPA, GDPR (international students), IPEDS reporting
- Roles: Campus IT directors, CIOs, systems integrators, database architects
- Budget: often $10M–$50M+ annual technology spend at large institutions

### 3. Corporate Learning & Development (L&D)
- In-house training departments at Fortune 500 / mid-market firms
- LMS-as-backbone; content authoring; learning analytics
- Smaller teams, more agile; often outsource infrastructure to cloud (Cornerstone, SuccessFactors)

**Growth Driver:** Post-pandemic digital acceleration, AI-powered tutoring, asynchronous workforce training, global compliance tightening (GDPR, COPPA, POPIA).

## V. Compliance & Regulatory Landscape

### US Regulations

#### FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)
**Source:** [Microsoft FERPA Compliance](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/compliance/regulatory/offering-ferpa), [FERPA Overview](https://studentprivacymatters.org/ferpa_ppra_coppa/)

- Applies to all US K-12 and higher ed schools receiving federal funding (essentially all public institutions)
- Protects student education records (grades, transcripts, personally identifiable information)
- **IT Impact:** Data classification, access controls, audit logging, secure deletion, vendor BAAs (Business Associate Agreements)
- Penalties: loss of federal funding (catastrophic)
- **Role:** IT admins often drive FERPA compliance architecture (encryption, role-based access)

#### COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act)
**Source:** [FTC COPPA Rule](https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/rules/childrens-online-privacy-protection-rule-coppa), [FTC Age Verification Policy (Feb 2026)](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/02/ftc-issues-coppa-policy-statement-incentivize-use-age-verification-technologies-protect-children)

- Applies to online services **directed to children under 13** (or with actual knowledge of collecting from children)
- Requires verifiable parental consent before collecting personal data from children
- **Recent:** FTC issued Feb 2026 policy statement encouraging age-verification technologies (exemption from enforcement for good-faith age-verification attempts)
- **IT Impact:** EdTech vendors (e.g., Khan Academy, Khanmigo, classroom AI tools) must implement parental consent workflows, data minimization, secure deletion
- **Non-compliance penalty:** up to $50,000 per violation

### International Regulations

#### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
**Source:** [Building Privacy-Compliant Systems: GDPR, COPPA, FERPA](https://6b.education/insight/building-privacy-compliant-systems-edtech-development-under-gdpr-coppa-and-ferpa/)

- Applies to schools/universities in EU + any institution serving EU students
- **More stringent than FERPA/COPPA:** explicit consent required (not just notice), data subject rights (deletion, portability, objection), DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) mandatory
- **IT Impact:** identity management, data residency (EU data must stay in EU), vendor compliance audits
- Cross-border schools must treat GDPR as the baseline (strictest requirement wins)

#### POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act)
**Source:** [POPIA South Africa](https://www.sabinetlaw.co.za/document/protection-personal-information-act-4-2013) — *general reference; education-specific guidance limited*

- South African federal data protection law (analogous to GDPR)
- Applies to education institutions processing South African citizen data
- **IT Impact:** consent, purpose limitation, security, international transfers
- **Enforcement:** Information Regulator (South Africa)
- **Penalty:** fines up to 10% of turnover or R100M+ (significant)

### US Reporting & Analytics

#### IPEDS (Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System)
**Source:** [IPEDS - NCES](https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds), [IPEDS About](https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/about-ipeds), [IPEDS Statutory Requirement](https://surveys.nces.ed.gov/ipeds/public/statutory-requirement)

- **Mandatory** for all institutions receiving Title IV federal student aid (Pell, loans, etc.)
- Annual data submission to US Dept of Education
- Collects: enrollment, degrees awarded, graduation rates, financial aid, finance
- **Compliance Requirement:** Missing/late submissions risk federal funding loss
- **IT Role:** data extraction, validation, integration with ERP/SIS, reporting pipelines

#### Common Data Set (CDS)
- Voluntary but de facto standard for higher ed reporting (used in rankings, public transparency)
- Overlaps with IPEDS; many universities automate both from same data sources
- **IT Impact:** data warehouse layer, ETL automation

## VII. AI in Education (2026 Landscape)

### Emerging Tools & IT Challenges

#### AI-Powered Tutoring
- **Khanmigo:** AI-guided tutoring (not just chat); launch with districts in 2026
- **MagicSchool.ai:** Lesson planning, assessment generation, differentiation
- **ChatGPT in classrooms:** Many districts piloting; FERPA/COPPA implications (data residency, parental consent)
- **AI Cheating Detection:** Turnitin, Chegg integration; academic integrity automation

#### IT Infrastructure Demands
- **GPU/compute:** Districts upgrading to run local AI models (privacy + cost)
- **Data governance:** FERPA-compliant AI training data (no student PII leaked to 3rd-party LLMs without consent)
- **Bias audits:** IT + data teams ensure AI not discriminating by race, gender, disability
- **Vendor vetting:** CISO/procurement need to validate vendor AI safety claims

#### Skills Gap
- Few IT professionals trained in AI governance in education
- Emerging role: **EdTech AI Compliance Officer** (hybrid of data, security, privacy)
- Salary projection: $120K–$160K (2026–2027)

## VIII. Key Career Roles & Salary Data

### Entry Level (0–3 years)

#### IT Support Technician (K-12 School)
- Endpoint support, password resets, device deployment, WiFi troubleshooting
- **Salary (US K-12):** $32K–$45K (varies by region)
- **Prerequisites:** CompTIA A+ or equivalent; help-desk experience
- **Pathway:** Help Desk → School IT Support → SIS Admin → Tech Director

#### LMS Content Developer / Instructional Technologist
- Create courses in Canvas/Blackboard; train faculty on LMS features
- **Salary:** $40K–$55K entry; $55K–$75K mid-level
- **Prerequisites:** Instructional design basics (ATD, ADDIE training); LMS certification
- **Pathway:** Instructor → Content Creator → Instructional Designer → Learning Operations Manager

### Mid-Level (3–7 years)

#### LMS Administrator (K-12 or Higher Ed)
- Manage Canvas/Blackboard instance; user provisioning, integrations, reporting
- **Salary (K-12):** $65K–$85K
- **Salary (Higher Ed):** $75K–$100K
- **Prerequisites:** Canvas/Blackboard certified; 2–3 years hands-on experience; SQL or basic API knowledge
- **Certifications:** Canvas Certified Technical Admin, Blackboard Learn Administration Essentials, Brightspace Certified Admin

#### SIS Administrator (K-12)
- PowerSchool/Skyward admin; data integrity, enrollment cycles, transcript generation
- **Salary:** $70K–$90K
- **Prerequisites:** PowerSchool/Skyward certification; 2–3 years in role
- **Pathway:** Help Desk → SIS Support → SIS Administrator → SIS Manager

#### Higher Ed Systems Analyst
- Support Workday/Banner; data integration, reporting, upgrades
- **Salary:** $85K–$110K
- **Prerequisites:** Workday certification or Banner training; data warehouse experience (SQL, ETL)
- **Certifications:** Workday CloudSource Academy (free training; no mandatory cert, but Workday Cloud+ credential available)

#### Educational Technology Specialist / Coordinator
- Mix of LMS, SIS, device management; teacher training; troubleshooting
- **Salary (K-12):** $50K–$70K
- **Salary (Higher Ed):** $65K–$85K
- **Certifications:** Google Certified Educator, Canvas, Moodle, or broad CompTIA Network+
- **Pathway:** Help Desk → EdTech Coordinator → EdTech Specialist → Director

### Senior-Level (7–15 years)

#### School Technology Director / IT Director (K-12)
- Oversee IT strategy, budget, staffing, infrastructure, vendor relationships
- **Salary (K-12):** $85,500–$141,000 (ZipRecruiter 2026 data); median ~$116,795
- **Salary variance:** region (CA/NY higher), district size (large districts pay 20–40% more), experience
- **Prerequisites:** 5–10 years IT experience; bachelor's degree in IT or related field; project management certification (PMP, CAPM) valued
- **Pathway:** IT Support → SIS/LMS Admin → IT Manager → Technology Director

#### Learning Management System Manager (Higher Ed)
- Oversee LMS roadmap, vendor relationships, institutional integrations
- **Salary:** $95K–$130K
- **Prerequisites:** 3–5 years LMS admin + 2–3 years management
- **Reports to:** CIO or VP Teaching & Learning

#### Director of Educational Technology (K-12 or Higher Ed)
- Strategic leadership on tech across institution; instructional design oversight; change management
- **Salary:** $100K–$140K+ (Glassdoor 2026)
- **Prerequisites:** Master's in Education/Instructional Technology preferred; 5–8 years EdTech experience
- **Organizations often reporting structure:** Reports to Superintendent (K-12) or VP Academic Affairs (Higher Ed)

#### Higher Education CIO
- Chief information officer for university/college
- **Salary:** $140,000–$250,000+ (per EDUCAUSE 2019; updated data limited 2024–2026)
- **Prerequisites:** 10–15 years IT + 3–5 years as director/VP; strategic planning, board exposure
- **Key factors (per EDUCAUSE):** Cabinet-level position (+$30K salary boost); doctoral institution (+40K advantage); master's degree (+$6,550 avg increase)
- **Pathway:** Systems Admin → Infrastructure Manager → Deputy CIO → CIO

### Emerging Role: EdTech Data Analyst

**Description:** Analyze LMS usage, student performance data, engagement metrics to inform instructional strategy
- **Salary:** $65K–$90K (K-12); $80K–$110K (higher ed)
- **Prerequisites:** SQL, Python/R, tableau/Power BI; understanding of FERPA/GDPR data handling
- **Certs:** Google Analytics for Education (free), Tableau Public (free), CompTIA Data+
- **Growth:** High (AI/ML integration accelerating)

- **Outcome:** Badge + certification; SIS-specific credentials

- **Cost:** FREE training; optional Workday Cloud+ credential (~$300)
- **Timeline:** Self-paced; 6–12 weeks typical
- **Outcome:** Cloud architect readiness; higher ed ERP expertise

## X. Career Entry: Teachers & IT Generalists

### Teacher → EdTech Specialist Transition

**Advantages of Teacher Background:**
- Deep understanding of pedagogy; credibility with faculty/students
- Awareness of classroom pain points (drives product design insight)
- Existing network within education; insider legitimacy
- Often lower salary expectations (moving from $45K–$65K teaching to $55K–$75K EdTech entry)

**Pathway:**
1. **Year 0:** Take LMS + instructional design mini-course (Canvas Essentials, Google Certified Educator L1, Coursera ID intro)
2. **Year 1:** Seek **Instructional Technologist** or **EdTech Coordinator** role (hybrid teaching/tech); ~$50K–$60K
3. **Year 2–3:** Full-time EdTech role (LMS admin, learning operations); upskill with Canvas Cert or ID Master's
4. **Year 3+:** Transition to management (EdTech Manager, Director of Teaching & Learning) or specialization (data analyst, AI governance)

**Sources:**
- [Eyes Up Here: Education Degree to IT](https://www.comptia.org/career-change/switching-career-education-path/from-education-to-it)
- [Entry Paths into EdTech for Former Educators](https://www.womentech.net/advice/17484/entry-paths-into-edtech-for-former-educators)
- [Transitioning from Teaching to EdTech (Medium)](https://theaveragejolene.medium.com/transitioning-from-teaching-to-edtech-8d69c8482990)

### IT Generalist → Education IT Specialist

**Advantages:**
- Already understand infrastructure, compliance, vendor management
- Compliance & security background (FERPA/GDPR learning curve shorter)
- Can command higher salary in education ($70K+ entry vs. $45K for non-IT background)

**Pathway:**
1. **Month 1–2:** Upskill on K-12/higher ed sector trends (read EDUCAUSE, ISTE reports)
2. **Month 2–3:** Take Canvas or LMS intro (online, self-paced)
3. **Month 3–6:** Seek role: IT Support at school, EdTech Coordinator, SIS Support
4. **Year 1–2:** Pursue formal certification (Canvas Technical Admin, PowerSchool SIS, or Workday)
5. **Year 2+:** Specialize (systems architect, compliance officer, data governance) or generalize (IT Director pathway)

## XIV. Current Trends & Future Growth (2026)

### AI-Powered Tutoring Adoption
- **Khanmigo district rollout beginning 2026:** IT teams need to vet for FERPA/COPPA compliance, manage SSO, audit training data
- **Classroom AI detection:** Turnitin, Chegg, GPTZero integrations → Academic integrity IT infrastructure
- **Risk:** Over-reliance on 3rd-party LLMs; privacy/licensing concerns

### ERP Modernization in Higher Ed
- **Workday gaining:** 2026 sees major rollouts (Miami, UNLV, others); high demand for Workday functional analysts, data engineers
- **Banner/Oracle declining:** Still large installed base, but new deals rare; strong salary for experienced admins, limited hiring

### Open-Source & Cost Containment
- **Moodle adoption growing:** especially EMEA, developing countries; cost advantage + customization appeal
- **Community-driven alternatives:** Canvas open-source forks being explored; Instructure limits OpenSource community access (strategic pressure)

### Compliance Tightening
- **FERPA enforcement increasing:** US Dept. of Education more active; school districts investing in audit infrastructure
- **COPPA age verification (2026 FTC policy):** EdTech vendors rapidly adopting; IT teams managing verification workflows
- **GDPR-K (GDPR for children):** EU pushing stricter child protections; global EdTech vendors adopting by default

### Workforce Demand
- **LMS admins:** High demand; shortage of certified professionals
- **Higher ed data architects:** Workday/Banner expertise commands premium
- **EdTech compliance officers:** Emerging role; 5-10% YoY growth projected
- **AI governance roles:** Nascent; likely explosive growth 2026–2028

## XV. Key Takeaways for Career Planning

1. **LMS expertise is foundational:** Pick one (Canvas, Brightspace, or Moodle); 70% of K-12 IT roles touch LMS.
2. **SIS admin is stable, lucrative:** PowerSchool/Skyward command strong salaries; high barrier to entry but good ROI.
3. **Higher ed is complex, strategic:** Workday, IPEDS, GDPR + FERPA compliance = sophisticated IT operation; pays 20–40% above K-12.
4. **Teaching background valuable:** Teachers can transition to EdTech Specialist in 1–2 years with modest retraining; soft skills matter.
5. **AI governance emerging fast:** COPPA enforcement + Khanmigo adoption + ChatGPT in classrooms = new specialization; get ahead with FERPA/GDPR knowledge.
6. **South Africa opportunity:** TVETs underserved; SchoolNet SA offers entry point; POPIA compliance growing field.
7. **Certifications pay off:** Canvas/PowerSchool certs have clear salary lift (+$10K–$15K); ROI justified.
8. **Community learning free:** ISTE, EDUCAUSE, Canvas Community offer peer support; conferences worth attending once established.
