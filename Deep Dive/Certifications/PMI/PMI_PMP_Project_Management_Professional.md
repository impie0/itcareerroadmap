# PMI Project Management Professional

**`PMP`** · ● **Active** · **Expert** · _PMI (Project Management Institute)_

> The PMI Project Management Professional (PMP) certification is the highest practitioner-level credential in project management. Globally recognized, it validates expertise across people, process, and business environment domains. A new exam version launches July 9, 2026, aligning with PMBOK Guide 8th Edition.

## About

The PMI Project Management Professional (PMP) is the gold-standard certification for project managers worldwide. Holders demonstrate mastery across people management, process execution, and business environment acumen—spanning predictive (waterfall), adaptive (agile), and hybrid methodologies. The credential is recognized across industries (IT, construction, finance, manufacturing, healthcare, government) and commands significant salary premiums (16–22% above non-certified peers). As of May 2026, a new exam version launches July 9, introducing PMBOK Guide 8th Edition content with expanded focus on artificial intelligence, sustainability, and value delivery. Existing PMP holders are grandfathered; current exam retires July 8, 2026.

## Domain context — Project Management

Project Management is a cross-domain discipline spanning all IT career tracks: infrastructure, software development, cloud migration, security programs, data initiatives, and enterprise transformation. PMI certification validates strategic thinking, team leadership, and delivery capability at the expert level.

## Topics covered (Current exam through July 8, 2026 — PMBOK 6th Edition basis)

- **People Domain (42%)** — approximately 76 questions: resource management, team development, stakeholder management, conflict resolution, leadership styles, emotional intelligence
- **Process Domain (50%)** — approximately 90 questions: project initiation, planning, execution, monitoring, controlling, closing; integration management; scope, schedule, cost, quality, procurement, communications management
- **Business Environment Domain (8%)** — approximately 14 questions: organizational strategies, compliance, governance, environmental factors, project portfolio alignment

*Source:* [PMP Exam Preparation ↗](https://www.pmi.org/certifications/project-management-pmp/pmp-exam-preparation)

**Note on July 2026 transition:** The new exam (effective July 9, 2026) realigns domains per PMBOK 8. Prospective candidates should verify current domain structure with PMI if taking the exam after July 8, 2026.

## Transition note: July 2026 exam update

**Current exam (through July 8, 2026):**
- PMBOK Guide 7th Edition base
- 180 questions, 240 minutes
- Domains: People (42%), Process (50%), Business Environment (8%)
- Passing: proficiency-based, no published numeric score

**New exam (effective July 9, 2026):**
- PMBOK Guide 8th Edition base
- 180 questions, 240 minutes
- Reweighted domains per PMBOK 8 performance domains
- New content: AI tools, sustainability, value delivery
- New question types: scenario sets, drag-and-drop, graphic interpretation
- Updated learning materials available April 14, 2026

**Action for candidates:** Decide whether to test before July 8 (current exam, lower AI/sustainability emphasis) or after July 9 (new exam, reflects 2026 PM practice). Both versions are valid; current exam candidates are grandfathered under existing renewal rules.
