## About

The PMI-ACP is a vendor-neutral, internationally recognized professional certification that validates expertise in Agile methodologies and practices. Launched in 2011, it was designed for project management professionals seeking to demonstrate mastery across multiple Agile frameworks (Scrum, Kanban, Lean, Extreme Programming, Test-Driven Development). Unlike framework-specific credentials, PMI-ACP emphasizes adaptability and Agile mindset across diverse contexts—making it valuable for teams working with hybrid or scaled Agile approaches.

## Topics covered

The PMI-ACP exam blueprint is organized into four domains with the following weight distribution:

- **Mindset (28%)** — Mastering agile principles to foster innovation, collaboration, and continuous learning
- **Leadership (25%)** — Empowering teams, facilitating conflict resolution, and promoting knowledge sharing
- **Product (19%)** — Refining product backlogs, managing value delivery, and ensuring alignment with business priorities
- **Delivery (28%)** — Optimizing flow, managing risks, and delivering customer value efficiently

## Skills validated

*Cert-specific — what this exam actually tests, distinct from the shared "Common skills" above.*

- Iterative and incremental delivery (Scrum sprints, story points, velocity tracking)
- Lean and Kanban principles (waste elimination, work-in-progress limits, flow)
- Agile metrics and reporting (burndown charts, cycle time, cumulative flow)
- Stakeholder engagement in distributed and co-located environments
- Team dynamics, psychological safety, and self-organization
- Value-driven delivery and product prioritization
- Risk management and adaptive planning in uncertainty
- Continuous improvement (kaizen, retrospectives, sprint reviews)
