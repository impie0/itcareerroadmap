## About

The ITIL 4 Specialist: Drive Stakeholder Value (DSV) certification validates expertise in understanding and managing stakeholder relationships, customer engagement, and value co-creation across the service lifecycle. Released in 2019 as part of the ITIL 4 managing professional pathway redesign, DSV is one of five specialist modules required to achieve ITIL 4 Managing Professional status. The exam assesses practical knowledge of stakeholder engagement models, customer journey design, service relationships, and value communication — essential competencies for customer success managers, service relationship managers, and IT leaders responsible for aligning service delivery with business outcomes and customer needs.

## Domain context — IT Service Management (ITSM)

IT Service Management encompasses the holistic, lifecycle-based approach to designing, delivering, and managing IT-enabled services. ITSM practitioners integrate business strategy with operational execution using frameworks like ITIL, which shifts focus from IT infrastructure to delivering measurable customer value and maintaining strategic stakeholder relationships.

## Topics covered

The DSV exam blueprint focuses on stakeholder engagement, relationship management, and value co-creation within the ITIL 4 service value chain. Key domains include:

- **Stakeholder Identification and Analysis** — identifying stakeholders; assessing stakeholder interests, expectations, and influence; segmentation models; stakeholder mapping
- **Customer Journey and Experience Design** — mapping customer touchpoints; understanding service experience; journey-based service improvement; omnichannel design
- **Relationship Management Practices** — building trust and partnerships; managing service relationships across the lifecycle; escalation and conflict resolution
- **Value Definition and Communication** — understanding value drivers (utility, warranty, cost); articulating value to diverse stakeholders; measuring and demonstrating value delivery
- **Service Co-creation and Collaboration** — engaging customers and partners in service design; collaborative service improvement; knowledge sharing and transparency
- **Onboarding and Offboarding** — smooth customer transitions; first impression management; graceful service exits; retention strategies
- **Service Relationship Metrics** — Net Promoter Score (NPS), customer satisfaction (CSAT), stakeholder engagement metrics; feedback loops; improvement initiatives

## Real-world application: Scenario walkthrough

**Scenario:** An enterprise IT service team is losing customer satisfaction scores (CSAT declining from 87% to 76% over six months). The service portfolio includes help desk, infrastructure, and enterprise applications support across 15 departments.

**ITIL 4 DSV Specialist approach:**

1. **Stakeholder Analysis:** Identify which departments are driving the decline. Interview stakeholders in those departments to understand unmet expectations. Are complaints about response time, resolution quality, communication, or perceived value?

2. **Customer Journey Mapping:** Map the actual experience of a typical user:
   - Submit a request → ticket auto-created
   - Assigned to tier-1 support → no communication during triage
   - Escalated to tier-2 → user kept waiting without status updates
   - Resolution → no follow-up or satisfaction survey

3. **Value Gap:** The service team delivers technical fixes (warranty) but doesn't communicate value to the customer. Users don't understand why resolution took time, or they perceive poor communication as poor service.

4. **Relationship Management Intervention:**
   - **Improve onboarding:** Send new users a welcome pack explaining service channels, response SLAs, and escalation paths.
   - **Enhance communication:** Tier-1 support sends status updates every 24 hours on pending tickets.
   - **Co-create improvements:** Host a quarterly "Service Council" with customer department heads. Review CSAT trends, jointly identify improvement priorities, and celebrate wins.
   - **Measure and adapt:** Track CSAT by department. Customize service offering for high-criticality departments (e.g., Finance gets 2-hour response, Operations gets 4-hour).

5. **Outcome:** CSAT recovers to 91% within 6 months, not because technical performance changed, but because stakeholder relationships and value communication improved.

## Exam preparation strategy

**Timeline:** 4–8 weeks for first-time specialists; 2–3 weeks if you have strong foundational ITIL and customer success experience.

**Study approach:**

1. **Establish ITIL foundation (weeks 1–2):** If DSV is your first specialist module, review ITIL 4 Foundation concepts (service value chain, guiding principles, four dimensions) and the DSV syllabus.

2. **Deep-dive stakeholder and relationship practices (weeks 2–4):** Study ITIL 4 Service Relationship Management, Engagement, and Continual Improvement practices. Understand the 34 ITIL practices and which ones enable stakeholder value.

3. **Learn value co-creation frameworks (week 4–5):** Study customer journey design, value definition models (utility vs. warranty), and stakeholder segmentation. Review real customer success case studies.

4. **Master DSV-specific terminology (week 5–6):** Understand concepts like NPS, CSAT, value streams, service outcomes, co-creation, and relationship governance. Build flashcards.

5. **Practice exams (weeks 6–7):** Take 3–4 full-length mock exams. Aim for 80%+ before attempting the real exam. Review incorrect answers; focus on scenario-based questions that test judgment, not just recall.

6. **Final review (week 8):** Re-read the official ITIL 4 book chapters on relationship management and service value. Take one final mock exam the day before.

**Common pitfalls to avoid:**
- Confusing stakeholder engagement with marketing or sales communication — it's about alignment and co-creation.
- Treating value as purely technical (MTTR, uptime) rather than business-aligned (cost savings, revenue impact, risk reduction).
- Neglecting the four dimensions — DSV requires balancing people, processes, technology, and partners.
- Overthinking complex scenarios — apply ITIL guiding principles (start where you are, think holistically, optimize iteratively).

**Passing rate:** ~72% of test-takers pass on the first attempt. Most candidates pass with 4–6 weeks of focused study.
