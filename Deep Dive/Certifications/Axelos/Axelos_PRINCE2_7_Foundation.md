## About

PRINCE2 7 Foundation is a vendor-agnostic, internationally recognized project management credential published by Axelos (a joint venture between the UK Cabinet Office and APMG International). The certification is based on the PRINCE2 7 methodology, which launched in September 2023, introducing a revamped focus on people management, sustainability, data governance, and flexible tailoring. Unlike PMI's PMP (which requires 3–5 years of documented PM experience), PRINCE2 Foundation is a low-barrier entry point with no prerequisites, making it ideal for career-changers, project coordinators, and junior PMs. The Foundation level validates understanding of PRINCE2's core structure (7 principles, 7 practices, 7 processes) and is a prerequisite for the Practitioner exam. PRINCE2 Foundation is particularly strong in UK, EU, and commonwealth organizations, and serves as a stepping stone to both the Practitioner cert and higher governance / program management roles.

## Domain context — Project Management

**Project Management** encompasses planning, execution, and delivery of time-bound initiatives within IT and broader business contexts. Project management spans entry-level (Project Coordinator, Junior PM), practitioner (Project Manager, Scrum Master, Delivery Lead), and advanced roles (Program Manager, PMO Director, Chief PM Officer). PRINCE2 Foundation sits at the **Entry level**—foundational structured PM methodology, process literacy, and readiness for formal PM responsibility.

## Topics covered

The PRINCE2 7 Foundation exam blueprint covers **five integrated learning outcomes**:

- **7 Principles** — Universal guidance on PRINCE2 PM approach: continued business justification, learning from experience, defined roles and responsibilities, manage by stages, manage by exception, focus on products, tailor to suit the project environment (~20% of exam)
- **7 Practices** — Continuous aspects addressed throughout project lifecycle: directing a project, managing product delivery, managing a stage boundary, controlling a stage, managing product acceptance criteria, capturing project issues and changes, managing project risks (~25%)
- **7 Processes** — Sequential/overlapping PM process groups: starting up a project, initiating a project, directing a project, controlling a stage, managing product delivery, managing a stage boundary, closing a project (~30%)
- **Roles and Organization** — Key project roles: Executive, Senior User, Senior Supplier, Project Manager, Team Manager, Project Assurance, and people-focused team composition (~15%)
- **Tailoring and Context** — Applying PRINCE2 flexibly to different project sizes, complexities, industry sectors, and organizational maturity (~10%)

## Skills validated

*Cert-specific — what this exam actually tests, distinct from the shared "Common skills" above.*

- **PRINCE2 Methodology & Framework** — The 7 principles, 7 practices, and 7 processes, and how they interconnect
- **Stage-Gate Project Delivery** — Understanding PRINCE2's stage-based gating and exception management approach
- **Product-Focused Delivery** — Product breakdown structure, product descriptions, acceptance criteria, quality tolerance
- **People Dimension in PM** — Identifying and managing stakeholder roles (Executive, Senior User, Senior Supplier, Team Manager)
- **Risk and Issue Escalation** — Formal risk registers, issue escalation triggers, exception reporting
- **Tailoring and Fit** — Adapting PRINCE2 to small, medium, and large projects; sector-specific and organizational contexts
- **PRINCE2 Terminology & Artifacts** — Business case, project mandate, stage plan, exception report, lessons learned register
- **Governance and Controls** — Directed control via exception management, decision gates, stage boundaries
- **Project Closure and Lessons** — Formal project closeout, lessons learned capture, team release, handover protocols
