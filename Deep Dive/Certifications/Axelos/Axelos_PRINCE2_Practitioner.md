## About

PRINCE2 Practitioner validates your ability to apply PRINCE2 principles and tailoring in real project environments. It is the second tier of the PRINCE2 certification pathway (Foundation first, then Practitioner). Launched in its current 7th edition format in 2021, it aligns with the latest PRINCE2 Manual and emphasizes practical context-driven decision-making — "principles, themes, and processes" rather than rote process outputs. Practitioner-level professionals are trusted to drive, control, and close projects in regulated, structured organizations across government, finance, manufacturing, and large services sectors. Prerequisite is an active PRINCE2 Foundation certification.

## Topics covered

The 7th edition exam focuses on seven **themes** and seven **processes**, plus overarching **principles**:

**Themes** (integrated, ongoing throughout project lifecycle):
- Business Case — value justification, cost-benefit, investment decision
- Organization — roles, responsibilities, stakeholder mapping (Project Board, Project Manager, Team Manager)
- Quality — criteria, tolerances, assurance, acceptance
- Plans — stages, schedules, resource allocation, risks
- Risk — identification, assessment, mitigation, escalation
- Change — change control, impact assessment, approval
- Progress — monitoring, reporting, stage boundaries, lessons learned

**Processes** (sequenced activities):
- Starting Up a Project — initiation triggers, project approach selection
- Directing a Project — Project Board oversight, exception handling, stage authorization
- Initiating a Project — detailed planning, baseline establishment, kickoff
- Controlling a Stage — day-to-day management, work packages, checkpoints
- Managing Product Delivery — supplier coordination, acceptance, handover
- Managing Stage Boundaries — stage closure, lessons review, next-stage planning
- Closing a Project — post-implementation review, benefits realization, archival

**Exam emphasis:** Practical tailoring, distinguishing when and why to apply specific themes/processes, recognizing exceptions, and justifying deviations from the standard model.

## Skills validated

- **PRINCE2 principles** — justifying why and when to apply the framework; recognizing when tailoring is required
- **Theme application** — integrating Business Case, Organization, Quality, Plans, Risk, Change, Progress into project context
- **Process execution** — sequencing and executing Starting Up, Directing, Initiating, Controlling, Managing Product Delivery, Stage Boundaries, Closing; understanding inputs/outputs
- **Exception management** — escalation triggers, tolerance thresholds, Project Board decision-making
- **Lessons learned** — capturing and applying organizational experience across project lifecycle
- **Stakeholder and change control** — mapping roles, managing approvals, handling scope/schedule/cost changes
