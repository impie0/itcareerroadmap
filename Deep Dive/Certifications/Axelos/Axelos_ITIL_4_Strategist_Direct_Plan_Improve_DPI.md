## About

The ITIL 4 Strategist: Direct, Plan and Improve (DPI) certification teaches IT professionals how to direct organizational change, plan continuous improvement initiatives, and align IT strategy with business objectives. Released in 2019 as part of the ITIL 4 Strategist module suite, DPI is the **only universal module** in the ITIL 4 certification scheme—it is **mandatory for both Managing Professional and Strategic Leader** career paths, making it essential for anyone pursuing advanced ITIL credentials. Unlike specialist modules focused on specific practices or stakeholder groups, DPI emphasizes holistic strategy, governance, and organizational alignment.

## Topics covered

DPI exam topics align with the ITIL 4 Strategist module blueprint and include:

- **Direction and Strategy** — Strategy formulation, strategic objectives, organizational alignment, executive communication
- **Governance and Risk Management** — Risk assessment, governance models, decision-making frameworks, compliance
- **Organizational Change Management** — Change readiness, stakeholder engagement, communication plans, resistance management
- **Continuous Improvement Model (CIM)** — PDCA cycle, measurement frameworks, improvement priorities, value delivery
- **Cascade and Alignment** — Translating strategy to tactical/operational levels, OKR/balanced scorecard techniques
- **Measurement and Reporting** — KPIs, metrics design, dashboard construction, reporting to C-suite
- **Service Management Integration** — How DPI principles connect to ITIL practices (ITSM, change, incident, problem management)
- **Decision-Making Methods** — Rational decision analysis, risk matrices, prioritization frameworks
