## About

The ITIL 4 Specialist: Create, Deliver and Support (CDS) certification validates expertise in designing, building, and operating IT services that align with customer needs and business objectives. Released in 2019 as part of the ITIL 4 managing professional pathway redesign, CDS is one of five specialist modules required to achieve ITIL 4 Managing Professional status. The exam assesses practical knowledge of service design and development, incident/problem management, service desk operations, and the service value chain — essential competencies for service managers, ITSM practitioners, and IT operations leaders responsible for end-to-end service delivery.

---

## Domain context — IT Service Management (ITSM)

IT Service Management encompasses the holistic, lifecycle-based approach to designing, delivering, and managing IT-enabled services. ITSM practitioners integrate business strategy with operational execution using frameworks like ITIL, which shifts focus from IT infrastructure to delivering measurable customer value.

## Topics covered

The CDS exam blueprint focuses on integrating service design, delivery, and support activities within the ITIL 4 service value chain. Key domains include:

- **Service Design and Development** — designing services that meet customer and business needs; service architecture; digital experience; service performance management
- **Service Desk and Incident Management** — first-line customer support; incident triage, logging, and resolution; service request fulfillment
- **Problem Management** — root-cause analysis; known-error database; proactive problem prevention; incident trend analysis
- **Knowledge Management** — capturing and sharing organizational knowledge; FAQ databases; training and awareness
- **Service Performance and Improvement** — SLA definition and monitoring; KPI tracking; continual service improvement (CSI) practices
- **Service Value Chain Integration** — aligning plan, engage, design, obtain/build, deliver, and support activities; cross-functional collaboration
- **Customer Journey and Digital Experience** — understanding customer touchpoints; service experience design; omnichannel delivery

## Skills validated

*Concrete competencies this exam assesses — not generic ITSM theory.*

- Service value chain integration and service value stream design
- Service desk operations and incident first-line triage
- Incident management workflows (log, classify, prioritize, escalate, resolve, close)
- Problem management: root-cause analysis and known-error database maintenance
- Service request management and fulfillment automation
- Knowledge management system design and maintenance
- Service performance measurement (KPI / SLA definition and monitoring)
- Customer journey mapping and digital experience optimization
- Service design aligned to customer and business needs
- Cross-functional service coordination (design, build, deliver, support teams)
- ITIL 4 practice integration: 34 practices across the service value system
- Continual service improvement metrics and CSI planning
