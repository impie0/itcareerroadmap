## About

The Workday Pro HCM Core Certification validates expertise in Human Capital Management (HCM) functional configuration, business process design, and core HR operations. This professional-level credential is designed for Workday administrators, HR consultants, and implementation professionals who configure and maintain Workday HCM modules. As a **gated certification**, it is only available to employees of Workday, authorized systems integration partners, and deployed Workday customer organizations; public enrollment and independent contractor access are not permitted.

---

## Domain context — HCM / HR Technology

Human Capital Management (HCM) systems manage the end-to-end employee lifecycle: recruiting, onboarding, compensation, benefits, time tracking, absence management, performance management, and talent development. Workday is one of the dominant cloud-native HCM platforms, competing with SuccessFactors, Paychex, Rippling, and others. Domain expertise in HCM systems is a sustained premium-pay specialty across mid-market and enterprise.

## Topics covered

Based on the official exam blueprint, the Workday Pro HCM Core exam covers:

- **HCM Fundamentals (30%)** — Core HR concepts, Workday HCM suite architecture, navigator, setup, security basics
- **Business Process Framework (10%)** — Business process design, supervisory organizations, staffing models, organizational structure configuration
- **Security Configuration (15%)** — Security rules, roles, domain security, data security, privacy compliance
- **Compensation & Organizations (20%)** — Job profiles, salary grades, compensation planning, bonus, merit increases, organizational hierarchy
- **Reporting & Analytics (10%)** — Delivered reports, report writing, worklet usage, analytics principles
- **Talent Management Core (15%)** — Recruiting, onboarding, learning & development, talent review, performance management entry

## Gating — access and eligibility

**This is a gated certification.** Public enrollment is not available. Access requires:

1. **Direct Workday employment** — Workday corporate staff (all geographies)
2. **Partner status** — Employee of a Workday Systems Integration Partner (Deloitte, EY, Accenture, IBM, etc.)
3. **Customer deployment** — Employee of an organization that has licensed and deployed Workday (verified via Workday partner portal)

**Restrictions:**
- Independent contractors and consultants **cannot** pursue this certification unless directly employed by a partner or customer
- Public exam registration is not available; all enrollment flows through employer or partner administrator
- Training credits are purchased by the employer or partner and allocated to employees
- Sandbox access (required for hands-on practice) is employer-provided; no public trial tenants exist

**Cost model:** Employers fund training and certification costs through partnership agreements or enterprise licenses. Typical investment per candidate: $1,500–$2,500 (training) + exam fees (not separately published).

## Certification access notice

**For candidates seeking Workday certification:**

This credential is only available through employer or authorized partner enrollment. If you are interested in Workday HCM expertise:

1. **Current Workday employee or partner employee:** Contact your learning administrator to enroll in Workday Education courses and schedule the certification exam.

2. **Employee at a Workday customer:** Request that your employer's Workday admin or HR team enroll you in partner-provided learning programs (e.g., through implementation partners like Deloitte, EY, or your system integrator).

3. **Not yet in a Workday environment:** Consider the following paths:
   - Apply to a Workday implementation partner as a consultant or analyst (typical entry requirement: 2–3 years HCM/HR systems experience)
   - Join a Workday customer organization in HR operations or systems roles
   - Build foundational HR systems knowledge via roles in SuccessFactors, SAP HCM, ADP, Paychex, or Oracle HCM Cloud; Workday certification becomes accessible once you join a Workday environment

4. **Supplementary learning:** Third-party courses (TechPratham, CloudShine) can reinforce concepts but do not grant certification eligibility; they are for those already working in Workday environments.
