## About

The Oracle Fusion Cloud Applications Financials Implementation Professional credential (1Z0-1054-25 General Ledger module) validates hands-on expertise in configuring, implementing, and operating Oracle's cloud-native ERP financial suite. Candidates demonstrate mastery of ledger architecture, period-close automation, multi-currency handling, and journal processing within Oracle Fusion's modern SaaS environment. Released in 2025, this cert is part of Oracle's modular Financials family—implementations typically require passing multiple focused exams (GL, AP, AR, Cash Management) depending on role scope.

---

## Domain context — ERP/Finance

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) cloud platforms automate core financial and operational workflows for mid-market to enterprise organizations. Oracle Fusion Cloud is a comprehensive SaaS ERP competing with SAP S/4HANA Cloud, NetSuite, and Microsoft Dynamics 365 Finance. Implementation professionals design data models, configure business rules, perform end-to-end testing, and guide customers through hypercare (go-live support).

## Topics covered

The General Ledger (GL) implementation exam focuses on the financial accounting backbone:

- **Ledger & Chart of Accounts Design** — Primary ledger, subledgers, multi-ledger architecture
- **Multi-Currency & Intercompany Accounting** — Revaluation, translation, intercompany settlement
- **Period-Close Processes** — Period open/close, mass allocations, recurring journals
- **Journal Entry Processing** — Manual journals, suspense accounts, approval workflows
- **Financial Reporting & Controls** — Trial balance reconciliation, audit trails, compliance
- **General Ledger Configuration** — Accounting calendars, ledger rules, transaction sources
- **Account Balances & Reconciliation** — Balance inquiry, variance analysis, bank reconciliation
- **Integration Patterns** — GL data import, subledger matching, account mapping

## Skills validated

*Cert-specific — what this exam actually tests.*

- General Ledger (GL) data model design and multi-ledger configuration
- Chart of Accounts (CoA) structure, segment hierarchies, and primary ledger mapping
- Period-close automation: recurring journals, allocations, rounding rules
- Multi-currency revaluation and foreign-exchange (FX) gain/loss processing
- Intercompany accounting setup: clearing accounts, elimination workflows
- Journal entry approval workflows and suspense account handling
- Financial reporting using GL trial balance, ledger summaries, and ad-hoc queries
- Integration between subledgers (AP, AR, FA) and GL; transaction matching
- Account analysis, balance inquiries, and variance investigation tools
- Audit trail configuration and compliance controls for GL transactions
