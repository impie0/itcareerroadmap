# Certified ScrumMaster (CSM)

**`CSM`** · ● **Active** · **Associate** · _Scrum Alliance_

> The Scrum Alliance Certified ScrumMaster (CSM) validates understanding of Scrum framework fundamentals and the Scrum Master role in empirical process control. Requires completion of a 16-hour, in-person or live-online training course delivered by a Certified Scrum Trainer (CST), followed by a proctored exam. Current status: active and widely recognized across North American and European organizations.

## About

The Certified ScrumMaster (CSM) is Scrum Alliance's flagship entry-level Agile credential, introduced in 2001 as part of the original Scrum framework publication. It certifies practitioners in Scrum Theory (empirical process control), the three Scrum roles (Product Owner, Development Team, Scrum Master), five Scrum events (Sprint, Daily Scrum, Sprint Planning, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective), and three artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment). The CSM is designed for Scrum Masters, Agile coaches, project coordinators, and technical leads entering Agile-based organizations. It remains one of the most widely recognized Agile credentials globally, particularly in North America, and serves as a prerequisite foundation for advanced Scrum Alliance credentials (A-CSM, CSP-SM).

## Topics covered

- **Scrum Theory and Empirical Process Control** — Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation; origins in complex work
- **Scrum Framework Overview** — Three roles, five events, three artifacts; timeboxing
- **Scrum Roles** — Product Owner (backlog management, prioritization), Development Team (cross-functional delivery), Scrum Master (servant-leader, impediment removal, process facilitation)
- **Sprint Planning** — Goal definition, capacity planning, Sprint Backlog creation
- **Daily Standup** — 15-minute timeboxed synchronization; tracking progress toward Sprint Goal
- **Sprint Review** — Inspection of product increment; stakeholder feedback; backlog refinement
- **Sprint Retrospective** — Team reflection; continuous process improvement; action items for next Sprint
- **Product Backlog Management** — Refinement, estimation (story points), ordering; relationship to Sprint Backlog
- **Definition of Done** — Quality criteria; consistency across Sprints
- **Scaling Scrum** — Multi-team coordination; dependencies; enterprise patterns (referenced, not deeply tested at CSM level)
- **Scrum Master Coaching Skills** — Conflict resolution, stakeholder management, servant-leadership
- **Impediment Removal and Facilitation** — Identifying blockers; organizational change; working agreements
