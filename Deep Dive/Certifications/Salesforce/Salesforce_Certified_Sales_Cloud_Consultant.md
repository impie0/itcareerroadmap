## About

The Salesforce Certified Sales Cloud Consultant credential validates expertise in designing, deploying, and optimizing Salesforce Sales Cloud solutions for enterprise organizations. Holders demonstrate mastery of sales process design, opportunity and account management, pipeline visibility, and integration strategies. This professional-level certification requires the Sales Cloud Administrator prerequisite and is designed for consultants, business analysts, and technical architects who manage end-to-end Sales Cloud implementations.

## Topics covered

The Sales Cloud Consultant exam blueprint emphasizes hands-on solution design and consulting competencies:

- **Industry Knowledge** (5%) — Understanding different sales models, industries, and organizational structures
- **Implementation Strategies** (15%) — Methodology, phasing, data migration, change management, and project planning
- **Sales Cloud Solution Design** (16%) — Architecture decisions, customization vs. configuration, process automation
- **Marketing and Leads** (8%) — Lead management, lead scoring, integration with marketing automation, lead assignment
- **Account/Contact/Opportunity Management** (15%) — Core Sales Cloud objects, relationship structures, deal tracking, forecasting
- **Sales Productivity and Integration** (12%) — Einstein Analytics, Salesforce Einstein Insights, third-party integrations, workflows
- **Consultation and Communication** (14%) — Stakeholder management, requirement gathering, documentation, project governance
- **Data and Analytics** (15%) — Reporting, dashboards, data governance, Einstein Analytics, predictive analytics, ROI measurement

## Skills validated

*What this exam concretely tests, beyond the shared "Common skills" domain competencies.*

- Salesforce Sales Cloud architecture and org design (single / multi-org strategies)
- Opportunity and Account management workflows (custom fields, record types, page layouts)
- Lead management and assignment rules (web-to-lead, lead routing, scoring)
- Sales process design (stages, picklists, validation rules, formula fields)
- Forecasting and pipeline visibility (forecast categories, roll-up summaries, custom reports)
- Salesforce Einstein (AI-powered insights, lead/opportunity scoring, activity capture)
- Integration patterns (API-based vs. middleware, data sync, third-party apps)
- CPQ (Configure, Price, Quote) fundamentals and quoting workflows
- Data governance and security (organization-wide defaults, role hierarchies, sharing rules)
- Salesforce AppExchange and ecosystem navigation
- Change management and stakeholder communication for Sales Cloud deployments
