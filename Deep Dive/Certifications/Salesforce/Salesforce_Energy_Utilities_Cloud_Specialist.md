## About

The Salesforce Certified Energy & Utilities Cloud Accredited Professional credential validates expertise in Salesforce's purpose-built Industry Cloud for energy and utilities organizations. This exam tests knowledge of the Energy & Utilities Cloud data model, service-to-cash workflows, field service orchestration, premise and service account management, outage management, and smart meter integration patterns. The certification is intended for professionals implementing and managing Salesforce solutions in electric, gas, and water utility sectors. Released as part of Salesforce's industry-specific cloud expansion (2023–2024), it sits at the Professional level and targets consultants, functional analysts, and business users who work on utility-sector implementations.

Salesforce's specialized cloud for energy and utility providers, including electric, gas, and water distribution. Combines account management, service delivery, billing integration, and field operations into a unified data model tailored to regulated utility business processes.

## Topics covered

- **Energy & Utilities Cloud Data Model** — Premise, Service Point, Service Account, Account hierarchies, and customer-account relationships in utility context
- **Service-to-Cash Workflow** — End-to-end service delivery, appointment scheduling, technician dispatch, work order management, and billing integration
- **Outage Management** — Creation, escalation, communication, and resolution of outage records; outage impact analysis
- **Field Service Integration** — Salesforce Field Service Lightning (FSL) orchestration, scheduling, and dispatch with Energy & Utilities data
- **Smart Meter & IoT Integration** — Premise-level consumption data, meter reading workflows, and meter-to-cloud integration patterns
- **OmniStudio Components** — OmniScript, Vlocity Card, OmniInteraction for customer-facing and internal workflows in utility operations
- **Account & Premise Management** — Multi-level account hierarchies, premise attributes, service connections, and regulatory compliance
- **Utility-Specific Business Processes** — Billing cycles, disconnection workflows, customer communication, and compliance reporting
- **Security & Access** — Org-wide defaults, role hierarchy, and field-level permissions for utility data sensitivity
- **Reporting & Analytics** — Utility-specific dashboards, Einstein Analytics for outage analysis, KPI tracking
