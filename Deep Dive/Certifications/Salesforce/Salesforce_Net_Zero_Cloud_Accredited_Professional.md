## About

The Salesforce Net Zero Cloud Accredited Professional (AP-218) credential recognizes practitioners with expertise in translating sustainability and ESG requirements into Salesforce solutions. Launched in 2023 as part of Salesforce's Accredited Professional tier, this exam validates skills in carbon accounting, emissions data modeling, GHG Protocol compliance, sustainability reporting (CDP/SBTi alignment), and implementation of Net Zero Cloud for enterprise environmental management. Intended for functional and technical consultants, business analysts, solution architects, and sustainability technology specialists. No formal prerequisites, though Salesforce Administrator certification is strongly recommended. Valid for 2 years; recertification via exam re-take required.

## Domain context — Sustainability/ESG Technology

Salesforce-based carbon accounting and environmental, social, and governance (ESG) data management platform. Enables enterprises to collect, calculate, analyze, and report Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions; track sustainability initiatives; and align with international reporting standards (GHG Protocol, CDP, Science Based Targets initiative / SBTi). Growing specialization as net-zero commitments and regulatory pressure (SEC, EU Taxonomy, Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive) drive investment in emissions tracking infrastructure.

## Topics covered

Official exam blueprint weighted domains (Salesforce Partner Learning Camp):

- **Discovery & Requirements (30%)** — Sustainability use case identification, business requirements gathering, Net Zero Cloud capability mapping, emissions calculation needs assessment, stakeholder alignment.
- **Net Zero Cloud Data Model (25%)** — Emissions object structure, Supplier and Emissions records, data hierarchy, carbon footprint calculation methodology, custom field design, relationship mapping, Scope 1/2/3 data separation.
- **Configuration & Implementation (25%)** — Standard platform features setup, data pipeline configuration, external data source integration (e.g., CDP, suppliers, utility providers), user permission sets (Manager, Admin, Auditor), action plans and reduction tracking.
- **Analytics & Reporting (15%)** — Tableau CRM dashboard design for sustainability metrics, Einstein Discovery for emissions pattern analysis, compliance reporting (GHG Protocol certification format, CDP alignment, SBTi tracking), export formats for audit and regulatory submission.
- **Integration & Compliance (5%)** — Third-party data connectors (ESG data aggregators), audit trail and data governance, security and access control, GDPR/data residency compliance for sustainability data.

*Source:* [Salesforce Partner Learning Camp (AP-218 blueprint)](https://1.salesforce-partners.com/net-zero-cloud-ap)

## Skills validated

*What the AP-218 exam specifically tests.*

- **Net Zero Cloud data model** — Emissions object, Supplier records, carbon footprint calculations, Scope classification (1/2/3), custom field mapping, relationship design.
- **GHG Protocol compliance** — Scope 1/2/3 emissions definition, calculation methodologies, boundary setting, materiality assessment, organizational vs. product level accounting.
- **CDP and SBTi alignment** — CDP questionnaire mapping, Science Based Targets framework, net-zero pathway design, emissions reduction roadmap.
- **Energy and emissions data collection** — Utility bill integration, supplier data aggregation, primary vs. secondary data handling, data quality validation.
- **Sustainability dashboards** — Tableau CRM setup for ESG KPIs, Einstein Discovery for emissions trend analysis, custom report design, stakeholder communication visuals.
- **Action plan management** — Emissions reduction initiative tracking, accountability assignment, progress monitoring, carbon offset and renewable energy credit integration.
- **User and security management** — Net Zero Cloud permission sets (Manager, Admin, Auditor), role-based access, audit trail, compliance feature enablement.
- **Third-party integrations** — ESG data vendor connections, ERP/HR system linking, SFTP and API-based data pipelines, external reporting tool exports.
- **Regulatory and standards knowledge** — SEC climate disclosure requirements, EU Taxonomy alignment, CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive), audit-ready data organization.
