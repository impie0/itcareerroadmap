## About

The Salesforce Certified Business Analyst (CBA) validates professional capability to conduct customer discovery, map business processes, define and communicate requirements, and facilitate stakeholder alignment during Salesforce implementations. Launched in 2016 and continuously updated with Salesforce releases, it is designed for IT professionals, functional analysts, and consultants who work between business teams and platform technical experts. No prerequisites; hands-on practice via Trailhead and real implementation experience recommended. Stacks well with Salesforce Administrator and progresses toward Sales Cloud or Service Cloud Consultant roles.

## Topics covered

Salesforce publishes an official exam blueprint. Study approximately these domain weights:

- **Customer Discovery and Stakeholder Analysis (17%)** — Understanding current state, identifying pain points, stakeholder mapping, user personas, requirements gathering.
- **Collaboration with Stakeholders (23%)** — Effective communication, managing expectations, documentation standards, sign-off processes, change management.
- **Business Process Mapping and Analysis (15%)** — Current and future state workflows, process decomposition, gap analysis, process optimization.
- **Requirements Definition and Management (20%)** — Functional requirements, user stories, acceptance criteria, requirements traceability matrix, non-functional requirements.
- **User Stories and Acceptance Criteria (10%)** — Story structure, INVEST principles, acceptance test-driven development, writing testable criteria.
- **User Acceptance Testing (UAT) and Quality (15%)** — UAT planning, test case design, defect triage, sign-off documentation, production readiness.

*Source:* [Official exam blueprint ↗](https://trailhead.salesforce.com/credentials/businessanalyst)

## Skills validated

*What the CBA exam specifically tests, distinct from shared Associate-level business analysis skills.*

- **Stakeholder identification and analysis** — Power/interest grids, communication plans, resistance management.
- **Current-state documentation** — Process walkthroughs, as-is workflows, pain point mapping, data flow diagrams.
- **Future-state design** — To-be processes, Salesforce-specific workflow improvements, role-based scenarios.
- **User story construction** — Format, acceptance criteria, story points, backlog refinement.
- **Requirements traceability** — Mapping requirements to test cases, design documents, and implementation.
- **Data modeling for CRM** — Entity relationships, field definitions, data hierarchies for Sales Cloud and Service Cloud contexts.
- **UAT planning and execution** — Test case creation, defect logging, sign-off documentation, regression testing.
- **Change management basics** — Communication, training plans, resistance handling, post-go-live support.
- **Salesforce-specific considerations** — Multi-cloud integration (Sales, Service, Commerce), API impact, customization vs. configuration trade-offs.
- **Quality assurance and validation** — Acceptance criteria validation, business rule testing, edge case identification.
