## About

The Tableau Certified Data Analyst (DA-201) certification validates professional-level proficiency with Tableau Desktop, Tableau Prep, and Tableau Server/Cloud for exploratory data analysis, advanced visualizations, and business insights delivery. Positioned as the "associate" tier (between entry-level Desktop Specialist and expert-level Architect certs), DA-201 emphasizes practical problem-solving: connecting to real data sources, preparing complex datasets, creating advanced calculated fields and LOD expressions, designing interactive dashboards with actions, and publishing content at scale. Recommended for business analysts, data analysts, BI developers, and reporting specialists who enable stakeholders to make data-driven decisions. Valid for 2 years; retake required to recertify.

## Topics covered

- **Connect and Prepare Data** — Data source connectivity (live/extract), relationships and joins, data modeling, Tableau Prep workflows, authentication, data quality
- **Explore and Analyze Data** (42% of exam) — Calculated fields, Level of Detail (LOD) expressions, statistical functions, trend analysis, forecasting, aggregation strategies
- **Create Content** (26% of exam) — Advanced chart types, dual-axis and combined visualizations, dashboard design, actions and interactivity, story structure, formatting
- **Publish and Manage Content** (8% of exam) — Publishing to Tableau Server/Cloud, permissions and security, data source management, refresh scheduling, content organization

*Source:* [Tableau Certification Exam Guide ↗](https://www.tableau.com/learn/certification/tableau-certified-data-analyst-exam-guide)

## Skills validated

- **Advanced data connection:** Live and extract connections, relationships (1-to-1, many-to-1), join types, authentication and permissions
- **Data preparation:** Tableau Prep workflows, data cleansing, calculated fields, binning, custom groups, parameters, set operations
- **Advanced calculations:** Level of Detail (LOD) expressions (FIXED, INCLUDE, EXCLUDE), table calculations, running sums, percentile ranking
- **Statistical analysis:** Trend lines, forecasting, confidence intervals, distributions, correlation, hypothesis-like thinking
- **Advanced visualizations:** Dual-axis charts, combined worksheets, geographic mapping, density plots, box plots, reference lines/bands
- **Dashboard interactivity:** Actions (filter, highlight, URL, go-to-sheet), dynamic parameters, drill-down navigation, flow control
- **Story design:** Sequencing worksheets and dashboards to communicate insights, annotation, narrative flow
- **Publishing & governance:** Tableau Server/Cloud publishing, permissions, data source curation, refresh scheduling, content organization
- **Performance optimization:** Extract refresh tuning, calculation optimization, query performance, metadata design
