## About

The Salesforce Manufacturing Cloud Accredited Professional certification (AP-213) validates expertise in implementing and configuring Salesforce Manufacturing Cloud solutions for discrete and process manufacturers, supply-chain networks, and channel-partner ecosystems. This professional-level credential is designed for consultants, administrators, solution architects, and implementation specialists who have hands-on experience deploying Manufacturing Cloud to manage sales forecasting, inventory planning, order management, supply-chain optimization, and multi-tenant tenant workflows. The certification launched in 2018 and remains actively maintained by Salesforce, with exam updates released annually to reflect new features and industry best practices.

Candidates for AP-213 should have 2–3 years of hands-on manufacturing consulting experience or equivalent Salesforce implementation background. The exam emphasizes practical, design-centric knowledge across the full implementation lifecycle: discovery and requirements analysis, data model design and configuration, build-out of Sales Agreements and Account-Based Forecasting, integration with ERP/PLM systems, and deployment readiness. Passing the AP-213 demonstrates competency in the Manufacturing Cloud data model, multi-country rollout strategy, role-based access control, manufacturing intelligence dashboards, and supply-chain process optimization. The credential is increasingly sought by Salesforce partners serving manufacturing verticals (automotive, industrial equipment, consumer goods, pharmaceuticals) and by manufacturing-focused enterprises undergoing digital transformation.

## Topics covered

The exam blueprint is organized around four main domain areas reflecting the manufacturing solution lifecycle, with equal weighting across each domain (approximately 25% per section):

### Discovery (25%)
- Analyzing current business processes and workflows to identify inefficiencies, bottlenecks, and pain points
- Assessing manufacturing business requirements (demand forecasting, inventory optimization, order fulfillment, supply-chain dynamics)
- Identifying key performance indicators (KPIs) and success metrics relevant to manufacturing operations
- Understanding integration dependencies with existing ERP, PLM, and supply-chain systems
- Evaluating multi-country deployment considerations (localization, regulatory compliance, currency handling)
- Determining phased rollout strategies and organizational readiness

### Design (25%)
- Translating discovery findings into detailed technical specifications and business process maps
- Mapping Manufacturing Cloud capabilities and configurations to identified business requirements
- Conducting gap analysis between current state and desired future state
- Designing Manufacturing Cloud data models for Sales Agreements, Account-Based Forecasts, and run-rate business structures
- Planning role-based access control (RBAC) and security models for multi-tenant deployments
- Documenting design decisions and creating solution architecture diagrams

### Configure and Build (25%)
- Setting up Manufacturing Cloud org configuration and user permissions
- Configuring Sales Agreements and managing channel partner territory assignments
- Building Account-Based Forecasting (ABF) models and run-rate business structures
- Setting up Manufacturing Intelligence dashboards and reporting
- Implementing third-party integrations (ERP sync, PLM connectors, supply-chain systems)
- Executing data migration strategies and object modeling for custom manufacturing processes
- Deploying Service Cloud functionality for field service and manufacturing support

### Validate and Deploy (25%)
- Supporting user acceptance testing (UAT) planning and execution
- Conducting system integration testing (SIT) across Manufacturing Cloud and connected systems
- Monitoring application performance and system health post-implementation
- Managing cutover planning and data validation prior to go-live
- Executing post-go-live optimization and hypercare support
- Training end-users and change management for manufacturing teams

## Exam strategy and study tips

**Time management on exam day:**
- 105 minutes for 60 questions means approximately 1.75 minutes per question. Multi-scenario questions may require longer.
- Review the first 30 questions carefully; use remaining time to revisit any flagged difficult questions.
- Flag uncertain answers (most testing platforms allow this) and allocate final 10 minutes to review.

**Recommended study approach (6–8 week timeline):**
- **Weeks 1–2:** Complete the free Trailhead Fundamentals module to establish baseline knowledge. Study the Manufacturing Cloud architecture, standard objects, and data model.
- **Weeks 3–4:** Work through specialized Trailhead modules (Account-Based Forecasting, Sales Agreements, Data Models). Focus on how features are configured, not just what they do.
- **Weeks 5–6:** Take practice exams from Whizlabs or ExamTopics. Review failed areas systematically.
- **Week 7:** Read official Salesforce documentation on integration patterns and deployment best practices.
- **Week 8:** Final practice exam and review of weak areas. Focus on scenario-based questions.

**High-impact study focus areas:**
1. **Account-Based Forecasting (ABF):** This is heavily tested. Understand how ABF differs from traditional forecasting, how territories and hierarchies affect forecasts, and how run-rate business models are structured.
2. **Sales Agreements:** Know the lifecycle of a Sales Agreement, rebate calculation, and how they integrate with forecasting.
3. **Data Model and Object Relationships:** Manufacturing Cloud objects (Account, Product, Manufacturing Orders, Forecasts) and their relationships are fundamental.
4. **Integration Patterns:** ERP sync scenarios and data migration strategies appear frequently. Understand the concept of "source of truth" for each object.
5. **Multi-Country Deployments:** Know localization considerations (currency, language, tax codes, regional compliance).
6. **Discovery and Design Phases:** AP-213 emphasizes consulting skills, not just technical configuration. Expect questions on gathering requirements, documenting design, and justifying architectural decisions.

**Common pitfalls to avoid:**
- Confusing Manufacturing Cloud with general Sales Cloud — focus on manufacturing-specific capabilities.
- Overlooking the importance of third-party integration — ERP and PLM connectivity is critical.
- Underestimating the design and discovery phases — the exam is not heavily technical, it's solution-design focused.
- Missing nuances in multi-country or multi-subsidiary scenarios — these are test favorites.

**Post-pass certification maintenance:**
- The AP-213 credential is valid for 3 years. Renewal requires retaking the exam (no continuing education credits option as of May 2026).
- Keep track of release dates for Manufacturing Cloud feature updates; Salesforce typically updates the exam blueprint annually in Spring.
- Consider pursuing higher Salesforce certifications (Technical Architect, Application Architect) within your 3-year window to stack credentials.
