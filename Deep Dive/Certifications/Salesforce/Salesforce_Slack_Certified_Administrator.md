## About

The Salesforce Certified Slack Administrator (Slack-Admn-201) credential validates the ability to configure, manage, and optimize Slack workspaces and organizations. Launched in 2021 as Slack integrated into the Salesforce portfolio, this Associate-level certification is designed for IT administrators, workspace owners, and collaboration tools specialists. Covers workspace setup, user provisioning, channel management, app governance, security, compliance, and change enablement. Recommended for IT professionals transitioning to modern communication-platform administration and organizations deploying Slack enterprise-wide.

## Topics covered

Salesforce publishes an official exam blueprint. Study approximately these domain areas:

- **Slack Fundamentals (15%)** — Slack architecture, plan features, user roles (Member, Admin, Owner, Org Owner), workspace vs. organization structure.
- **Workspace Administration (18%)** — Workspace creation and approval process, workspace settings, org-level settings and policies, billing and licensing.
- **User Management and Authentication (20%)** — User provisioning (email, SAML/SSO, SCIM, JIT), user lifecycle management, role assignments, deprovisioning.
- **Channels and User Groups (18%)** — Public and private channels, channel naming and organization, user groups, multi-workspace channels (org-wide), Slack Connect external collaboration.
- **Apps and Integrations (15%)** — App approval workflow, app marketplace, app permissions, custom integrations, Enterprise Grid app controls.
- **Security and Compliance (10%)** — Data encryption, audit logs, DLP (data loss prevention), GDPR and compliance features, access controls, IP whitelisting.
- **Enabling Slack Success (4%)** — Usage analytics and dashboards, change management, adoption metrics, training and communication strategy.

## Skills validated

*What the Slack-Admn-201 exam specifically tests, distinct from shared Associate-level collaboration skills.*

- **Slack workspace architecture** — Workspace creation, organization structure, workspace-level vs. org-level settings.
- **User provisioning and SSO** — SAML, SCIM, JIT provisioning, user role assignment, bulk user operations.
- **Channel governance** — Public/private channels, channel naming, archiving, multi-workspace channels, external collaboration via Slack Connect.
- **User groups and team management** — Creating and managing user groups, nested groups, team hierarchies.
- **Admin roles and permissions** — Workspace Admin, Org Admin, Owner roles, permission delegation, Org Owner responsibilities.
- **App lifecycle and governance** — App approval workflows, app marketplace navigation, app permission scopes, custom app integration.
- **Security and compliance** — Org-wide shared channels, IP whitelisting, data encryption at rest and in transit, DLP policies.
- **Audit and compliance** — Audit logs, data export, GDPR/HIPAA features, compliance reports, login activity monitoring.
- **Enterprise Grid features** — Grid org structure, multi-workspace management, grid app controls, centralized administration.
- **Slack analytics and adoption** — Workspace analytics dashboard, member engagement metrics, usage reporting, recommendations engine.
- **Change management and training** — Adoption planning, internal communication for Slack rollouts, best practices documentation.
