## About

The Adobe Certified Professional (ACP) in InDesign validates foundational competency in document layout, print and digital design, page management, typography, and publishing workflows. Aimed at graphic designers, print production specialists, and publishing professionals entering or transitioning into InDesign-focused roles. The exam combines selected-response questions with live performance tasks inside Adobe InDesign CC, assessing both conceptual knowledge and hands-on application. Not a prerequisite for other Adobe certs; stands as an associate-level credential suitable for entry-level design teams and freelance portfolios.

## Topics covered

Based on the official Adobe Certified Professional exam blueprint:

- **Document Setup and Workflow** — Creating documents with appropriate settings for print, web, and mobile; configuring page size, orientation, margins, columns, bleed, slug, facing pages, and text frames.
- **Pages and Master Pages** — Adding and deleting pages; using master pages to ensure design consistency; assigning alternate layouts; understanding pages vs. spreads.
- **Type and Typography** — Formatting text; applying character and paragraph styles; managing fonts; text flow and threading; hyphenation and justification.
- **Tables and Structured Content** — Creating and formatting tables; managing table styles; cell formatting; long-document organization.
- **Color Management** — Working with color spaces; applying swatches; managing color consistency across print and screen output.
- **Styles and Templates** — Using paragraph, character, object, and table styles; creating and applying style sheets for consistency.
- **Graphics and Objects** — Placing and anchoring objects; working with frames; text wrapping; grouping and alignment.
- **Long-Document Features** — Managing multi-page documents; creating table of contents and indexes; section numbering; cross-references.
- **Export and Output** — Exporting to PDF (print-ready and interactive); saving for web and digital distribution; preflight checks; resolving errors.
- **Interactive PDFs** — Creating clickable buttons, form fields, bookmarks, and navigation in exported PDFs.
- **Design Principles** — Applying rule of thirds, emphasis, hierarchy, negative space, grids, and focal point concepts to layouts.
