## About

The AD0-E117 Adobe Certified Master – AEM Sites Architect credential recognizes expert-level proficiency in designing, architecting, and delivering enterprise content management solutions on Adobe Experience Manager. It assumes deep knowledge of AEM 6.4, 6.5, and AEM as a Cloud Service. Candidates must demonstrate ability to translate business requirements into scalable, secure, performant AEM architectures; design content models and component hierarchies; plan integrations with third-party and Adobe systems; and define governance and operational strategies. This is a lifetime credential — no renewal required.

## Topics covered

Per the Adobe Architect Master exam blueprint:

- **Discovery & Assessment** (25%)
  - Conduct stakeholder interviews and assess current infrastructure
  - Document functional and non-functional requirements
  - Identify integration touchpoints and define content taxonomy
  - Establish success metrics and baseline performance benchmarks

- **Solution Design** (35%)
  - Design high-level and detailed architectural blueprints
  - Design component hierarchies and content governance models
  - Plan AEM Sites instance topology (single vs. multi-site)
  - Design caching strategies (Dispatcher, CDN, browser cache)
  - Design security models (authentication, authorization, encryption)
  - Specify third-party integrations (APIs, DAM, CRM, Commerce)
  - Design replication and distribution strategies

- **Implementation** (25%)
  - Execute architecture through AEM configuration and development
  - Configure OSGi, context-aware configs, and Cloud Manager pipelines
  - Design and develop custom components and templates
  - Manage code deployments and CI/CD workflows
  - Ensure quality assurance across dev, stage, and production

- **Maintenance & Operations** (15%)
  - Plan ongoing support, monitoring, and optimization
  - Design troubleshooting strategies
  - Plan for scalability and evolution of the solution
