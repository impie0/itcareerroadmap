## About

The Adobe Certified Professional (ACP) certification in Graphic Design & Illustration using Adobe Illustrator validates foundational and applied proficiency with vector design, digital illustration, and professional asset export. Candidates must demonstrate hands-on competency in the Illustrator application alongside conceptual knowledge of design principles. This entry-to-associate-level cert is administered by Certiport (Pearson VUE) and is intended for students, early-career designers, and professionals transitioning to graphic design roles.

## Topics covered

- **Design fundamentals** — aspect ratio, rule of thirds, foreground/background, color theory, contrast, composition, framing
- **Document setup** — creating documents for mobile, web, print, film/video; artboards, bleed, resolution, color mode (RGB/CMYK)
- **Layers and organization** — creating, managing, locking, hiding, nesting, reordering, targeting objects in Layers panel
- **Drawing and paths** — Pen tool, Curvature tool, shapes (rectangle, ellipse, polygon), Blob Brush, path editing, stroke/fill
- **Type and text** — creating point/area text, font selection, character/paragraph formatting, text on path, threading
- **Color and swatches** — RGB vs CMYK, color modes, creating/managing swatches, swatch libraries, gradients, color harmonies
- **Effects and filters** — raster effects, live effects, blur, distortion, stylize, 3D effects
- **Brushes and symbols** — art brushes, scatter brushes, calligraphic brushes, symbol creation and management
- **Export and publishing** — preparing assets for web (SVG, PNG, JPEG), print (PDF, EPS), video formats; asset optimization

## Skills validated

*Cert-specific — what this exam actually tests, distinct from the shared "Common skills" above.*

- **Pen tool and bezier manipulation** — drawing curves, editing paths, converting anchor types, combining paths
- **Vector shape construction** — using Pathfinder operations (union, intersect, exclude, divide), shape mode transformations
- **Text formatting and typography** — character/paragraph styles, font management, text wrapping, OpenType features
- **Color modes and export preparation** — CMYK conversion, RGB-to-print workflow, soft proofing, export format selection
- **Layer hierarchy and organization** — nested layers, sublayers, targeting, selection via Layers panel
- **Artboards and document structure** — multiple artboards, artboard sizing, orientation, bleed and trim
- **Symbols and brushes** — creating and managing symbol libraries, dynamic symbols, brush variants
- **Asset export and optimization** — SVG export, web-ready PNG/JPEG, PDF for print, scale-independent export
