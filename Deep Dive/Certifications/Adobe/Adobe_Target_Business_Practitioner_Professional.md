## About

The Adobe Certified Professional - Adobe Target Business Practitioner (AD0-E408) certifies foundational proficiency in A/B testing, personalization, and experience optimization using Adobe Target. Designed for marketing professionals, optimization specialists, and digital practitioners with entry-level to associate-level experience, this credential validates the ability to design experiments, set up personalization rules, interpret analytics results, and manage optimization campaigns. The exam was introduced in Adobe's current certification structure (2023 onwards) and remains the primary Associate-level Target credential for business practitioners.

---

## Domain context — Personalization & Marketing

Application of data-driven testing and personalization techniques to optimize digital experiences across web, mobile, and email channels. Adobe Target is the central platform for A/B testing, multivariate testing, automated personalization, and recommendations within the Adobe Experience Cloud.

## Topics covered

Official exam blueprint topics for AD0-E408:

- **Define and align business KPIs with optimization goals** — translating business objectives into measurable optimization metrics; understanding lift, confidence, and statistical significance
- **Identify opportunities for experimentation** — analyzing data to discover test hypotheses; segmenting audiences for targeted experiences
- **Construct valid test hypotheses and understand KPI-based success metrics** — designing hypothesis statements; defining primary and secondary metrics
- **A/B/Multivariate Testing (A/B, XT, MVT)** — launching and managing A/B tests, Experience Targeting, and Multivariate Test activities; interpreting results
- **Automated Personalization (AP)** — configuring and evaluating AP models; understanding machine learning-driven personalization
- **Recommendations** — setting up product and content recommendations; integrating behavioral data sources
- **Audience creation and management** — building audiences from first-party data; applying audience rules and logic
- **Visual Experience Composer (VEC) and Form-Based Composer** — creating experiences in both environments; troubleshooting common VEC issues
- **Activity QA and launch procedures** — testing activities before go-live; validating on-page behavior and analytics integration
- **Reporting and analytics (A4T)** — reading Adobe Target reports; using Adobe Analytics for detailed analysis (A4T integration)
- **Authentication and framework integration** — basic JavaScript integration; troubleshooting implementation issues
- **Profile and targeting options** — using mboxes, parameters, and profile scripts for targeting logic
