## About

Adobe Certified Expert — Real-Time CDP Developer (AD0-E605) is an expert-level credential validating advanced proficiency in implementing, configuring, and maintaining the Adobe Real-Time Customer Data Platform (RTCDP) as part of the Adobe Experience Cloud. The exam tests deep knowledge of Experience Data Model (XDM) schema design, identity graph resolution, data ingestion architecture (batch and streaming), segmentation methodologies, activation patterns, customer profile service, privacy and consent workflows, Query Service, and Federated Audience Composition. Required for senior developer, customer data platform engineer, and data architect roles in enterprise marketing technology and customer experience organizations. RTCDP expertise is increasingly central to enterprises implementing composable, API-first customer data strategies.

## Topics covered

Adobe Real-Time CDP architecture, data modeling, and operational integration.

- **Data Architecture & XDM** (22%): Experience Data Model (XDM) schema design, custom data types, profile composition, data governance, NoSQL vs. relational patterns.
- **Identity Service & Graph** (18%): Identity resolution, namespace configuration, identity linking rules, profile merge policies, identity graph traversal, handling identity conflicts.
- **Data Ingestion** (16%): Batch ingestion via UI/API, streaming ingestion via HTTP/Kafka, source connectors, dataflow configuration, error handling, validation.
- **Segmentation & Audiences** (20%): Segment Builder, streaming vs. batch segmentation, audience composition, segment activation workflows, segment refresh cadences, edge segmentation.
- **Activation & Destinations** (12%): Destination configuration, audience mapping, scheduled vs. streaming activation, personalization on-site, customer data platform guardrails.
- **Customer Profile Service** (6%): Real-Time Customer Profile assembly, edge profiles, profile merge behavior, profile lookups via API.
- **Privacy, Consent & Governance** (6%): Privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA, LGPD), consent enforcement, data lifecycle management, field-level access controls.
