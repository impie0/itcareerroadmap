## About

The Palo Alto Networks Certified Cybersecurity Entry-level Technician (PCCET) was an entry-level credential designed for professionals with foundational networking and cybersecurity knowledge seeking to validate entry-level competency in security operations and Palo Alto Networks concepts. The exam was released around 2019 as the foundational tier of Palo Alto's legacy certification path. It was retired on January 31, 2025, as Palo Alto Networks transitioned to role-based certification exams aligned with current job market requirements. Holders of active PCCET certifications remain valid for two years from their achievement date.

## Topics covered

The PCCET exam blueprint covered four primary domains:

- **Fundamentals of Cybersecurity** (network security, threat types, risk management concepts)
- **Network Security Components** (firewalls, VPNs, intrusion prevention, network segmentation)
- **Cloud Technologies** (cloud security principles, shared responsibility model, cloud threats)
- **Elements of Security Operations** (SIEM basics, incident response, threat hunting fundamentals)

*Source:* [Official PCCET Blueprint ↗](https://www.paloaltonetworks.com/content/dam/pan/en_US/assets/pdf/datasheets/education/pccet-blueprint.pdf)

## Books

No standalone books were published specifically for the PCCET certification. Candidates relied on:
- The official Palo Alto Networks PCCET blueprint (PDF)
- General cybersecurity foundational textbooks (e.g., *CompTIA Security+* study guides, which cover overlapping fundamentals)
- Vendor-supplied training materials and documentation

**Book rule:** The absence of dedicated PCCET study books reflects its positioning as a foundational entry-level exam; Palo Alto Networks prioritized online learning over print materials for this tier.

## Migration path (2025 onward)

**For PCCET holders:** Your certification remains valid for two years from your achievement date. To maintain your credential standing in Palo Alto's ecosystem after your PCCET expires or if you wish to pursue current certifications:

1. **Immediate entry:** Enroll in [Cybersecurity Practitioner ↗](../../Certifications/Palo_Alto/Palo_Alto_Cybersecurity_Practitioner.md) ($150 exam, free training)
2. **Career progression:** PCCET knowledge directly supports Cybersecurity Practitioner readiness; your fundamentals will transfer
3. **Advanced track:** After Practitioner, move to Specialist or Professional track based on your role focus (NGFW, Threat Intelligence, SOC/XDR, etc.)

**For new entrants (2025+):** Start with [Cybersecurity Apprentice ↗](../../Certifications/Palo_Alto/Palo_Alto_Cybersecurity_Apprentice.md) (foundational networking + security, no Palo Alto experience required) or jump directly to [Cybersecurity Practitioner ↗](../../Certifications/Palo_Alto/Palo_Alto_Cybersecurity_Practitioner.md) if you already have baseline security knowledge.
