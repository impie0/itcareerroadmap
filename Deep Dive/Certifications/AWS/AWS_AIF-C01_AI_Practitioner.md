## About

The AWS Certified AI Practitioner validates foundational knowledge of artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning (ML), and generative AI (GenAI) concepts, with focus on practical business applications of AI on AWS. Launched in August 2024 (beta) and reaching general availability in October 2024, AIF-C01 targets business users, product managers, sales engineers, and IT professionals seeking to understand AI/ML without requiring deep technical expertise. It is the entry point to AWS's AI/ML certification ladder, positioned below the MLA-C01 (Machine Learning Engineer – Associate) and intended to democratize AI literacy across non-specialist IT and business roles.

## Topics covered

The exam validates mastery of five content domains:

- **Fundamentals of AI and ML (20%)** — Definitions of AI, ML, deep learning; supervised / unsupervised learning; use case distinctions; basic model selection
- **Fundamentals of Generative AI (24%)** — Foundation models, large language models (LLMs), diffusion models, multimodal models, prompt engineering, in-context learning
- **Applications of Foundation Models (28%)** — Foundation model selection and deployment; retrieval-augmented generation (RAG); fine-tuning strategies; AWS Bedrock and SageMaker integration
- **Guidelines for Responsible AI (14%)** — AI ethics, bias detection, hallucination mitigation, fairness frameworks, responsible AI principles
- **Security, Compliance, and Governance for AI Solutions (14%)** — Data privacy, model governance, Amazon SageMaker Model Cards, compliance frameworks, MLOps practices

*Source:* [Official exam guide ↗](https://d1.awsstatic.com/training-and-certification/docs-ai-practitioner/AWS-Certified-AI-Practitioner_Exam-Guide.pdf)

## Skills validated

- AWS Bedrock (foundation model access and deployment)
- Amazon SageMaker (basic ML workflows, notebooks, training, hosting)
- Amazon Q Developer / Q Business (AWS AI coding and business assistant)
- Amazon Comprehend (natural language processing and sentiment analysis)
- Amazon Translate (machine translation)
- Amazon Polly (text-to-speech)
- Amazon Transcribe (speech-to-text)
- Amazon Rekognition (computer vision and image analysis)
- Amazon Lex (chatbot frameworks)
- Amazon Personalize (recommendation systems)
- Prompt engineering (zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought)
- Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) concept
- Vector embeddings and semantic search
- Model evaluation and selection frameworks
- Responsible AI tools and governance with Amazon SageMaker Model Cards
- Foundation model lifecycle (data selection, pre-training, fine-tuning, deployment, feedback)
