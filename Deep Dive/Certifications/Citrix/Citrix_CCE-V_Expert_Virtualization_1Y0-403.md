## About

The CCE-V is Citrix's expert-tier virtualization certification, validating the skills of experienced IT solution designers—architects, consultants, and senior engineers—who assess and design enterprise-scale Citrix Virtual Apps and Desktops (CVAD) solutions. Launched in January 2020, the 1Y0-403 exam emphasizes architectural decision-making, advanced configuration, and practical troubleshooting in complex, multi-site environments. Only a small percentage of Citrix professionals achieve this level, making it a significant credential differentiator. CCE-V holders typically architect large-scale VDI deployments, lead design reviews, and consult on optimization and disaster recovery strategies.

## Topics covered

Exam topics based on the official 1Y0-403 blueprint, emphasizing architectural and design scenarios:

- **Determining design decisions based on business drivers** — translating business requirements into technical architecture
- **Categorizing applications** — understanding application layering, delivery models, and packaging strategies
- **Endpoint and peripheral selection** — choosing device types (thin client, zero client, repurposed hardware) and peripheral support
- **Securing Virtual Delivery Agent (VDA) machines** — hardening VM security, isolation, and network policies
- **Selecting delivery options** — deciding between hosted shared desktops, dedicated desktops, app streaming, and hybrid models
- **Policy implementation** — designing and applying Citrix policies for performance, security, and user experience
- **Management and administration architecture** — designing administrative delegation, monitoring, and operational models
- **Hardware and hypervisor selection** — evaluating hypervisor options (VMware vSphere, Hyper-V, Nutanix), compute/storage requirements
- **Security objectives and best practices** — multi-factor authentication, encryption, least-privilege access, compliance
- **Multi-location and multi-site architecture** — WAN optimization, site failover, global load balancing
- **Profile and data management** — user profiles, personal vDisk, app data synchronization across sites
- **Disaster recovery design** — RTO/RPO targets, backup strategies, failover automation
- **Resource optimization and performance tuning** — memory optimization, I/O tuning, user density
- **Citrix Cloud and DaaS considerations** — cloud-hosted CVAD deployment models

*Source:* [1Y0-403 Exam Prep Guide ↗](https://www.citrix.com/content/dam/citrix/en_us/documents/guide/citrix-virtual-apps-and-desktops-7-assessment-design-and-advanced-configuration-exam-prep-guide-403.pdf)
