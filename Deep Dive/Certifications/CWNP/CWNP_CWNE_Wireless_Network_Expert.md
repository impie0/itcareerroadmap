## About

The CWNE is CWNP's elite Expert-level credential, recognizing wireless network professionals at the pinnacle of the field. Rather than a traditional exam, CWNE employs a rigorous credentialing process: applicants must hold CWNA-109 and three professional-level CWNP certifications (typically CWSP, CWAP, and CWDP), demonstrate 3+ years of documented wireless networking experience, obtain three professional letters of recommendation from industry peers, and submit a portfolio showcasing real-world projects and deep technical expertise. CWNP evaluates the full application holistically before awarding the credential. Holders are rare and highly respected in enterprise wireless engineering, RF design, and security architecture communities. The credential signals elite expertise in 802.11 standards, RF propagation, wireless security, and large-scale WLAN deployments.

## Prerequisites and credential path

To be eligible for CWNE credentialing:

1. **Hold CWNA-109** — Foundational wireless networking certification (or equivalent predecessor).
2. **Hold three CWNP professional certifications** — Any combination of:
   - CWSP (Certified Wireless Security Professional) — 802.11 security, RADIUS, encryption, threat mitigation
   - CWAP (Certified Wireless Analysis Professional) — packet analysis, spectrum analysis, protocol troubleshooting
   - CWDP (Certified Wireless Design Professional) — RF design, capacity planning, site surveys, network architecture
   - CWIIP (Certified Wireless IoT Professional) — industrial wireless, edge security, IoT protocols
3. **Accumulate 3+ years wireless networking experience** — Hands-on professional practice in roles such as wireless engineer, RF engineer, network architect, or security engineer. Experience must span multiple project types (deployments, redesigns, troubleshooting, security assessments).
4. **Secure three professional letters of recommendation** — Written endorsements from colleagues, managers, or respected peers in the wireless industry who can attest to your technical expertise and professional standing.
5. **Prepare and submit application** — Detailed portfolio including:
   - Resume / CV highlighting wireless achievements
   - Project case studies demonstrating technical depth
   - References to real deployments, RF designs, or security implementations
   - Attestation of continuous wireless networking work

## Application and credentialing process

The CWNE credentialing process is managed directly by CWNP and evaluated on a rolling basis:

- **Step 1: Verify prerequisites** — Confirm CWNA + 3 specialty certs, and validate 3+ years experience from applicant timeline.
- **Step 2: Portfolio review** — CWNP reviews submitted case studies, project documentation, and technical depth evidence.
- **Step 3: Reference evaluation** — Three letters of recommendation are assessed for credibility and alignment with professional standards.
- **Step 4: Decision** — CWNP notifies applicant of approval or requests additional information.
- **Timeline** — Typical decision window is 4–8 weeks from complete application submission.
- **Cost** — Application and credentialing fee is $300–$500 USD (non-refundable).

## Skills validated

*Comprehensive expertise validated by CWNE credential holder status:*

- **Radio Frequency (RF) Engineering** — Propagation modeling, fading analysis, interference mitigation, antenna design optimization, path loss prediction, and site survey execution at enterprise scale.
- **802.11 Protocol Mastery** — Frame-level deep knowledge of 802.11a/b/g/n/ac/ax/be standards, MAC operations, QoS design, roaming behavior, and edge-case troubleshooting.
- **Wireless Security Architecture** — End-to-end WLAN security design: authentication systems (802.1X, EAP variants), encryption (WPA2-Enterprise, WPA3), threat modeling, compliance (PCI-DSS, HIPAA), and incident response.
- **Packet and Spectrum Analysis** — Ability to capture, decode, and interpret wireless frames and RF spectrum in real-time using protocol analyzers and spectrum analyzers to diagnose complex issues.
- **Large-Scale WLAN Design** — Multi-building, multi-site deployments: capacity planning, controller architecture, access point placement algorithms, failover design, integration with SD-WAN and cloud infrastructure.
- **Performance Optimization** — Diagnosing and resolving coverage gaps, interference, capacity bottlenecks, and roaming delays in production networks.
- **Regulatory and Compliance** — FCC, ETSI, ISED frequency allocations; power limits; country code enforcement; standards evolution tracking.
- **IoT and Edge Wireless** — Advanced knowledge of industrial IoT protocols (Zigbee, Thread, LoRaWAN) and edge security integration.
- **Leadership and Mentorship** — Demonstrated ability to architect solutions, mentor junior engineers, and drive wireless engineering strategy in enterprise environments.
