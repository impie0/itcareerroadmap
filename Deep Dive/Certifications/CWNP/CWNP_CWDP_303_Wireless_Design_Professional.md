# Certified Wireless Design Professional (CWDP)

**`CWDP-303`** · ● **Active** · **Professional** · _CWNP_

It is required for professionals architecting enterprise wireless networks and is the gateway to CWNP's Expert level (CWNE).

## About

The CWDP-303 is CWNP's Professional-level wireless design certification, establishing mastery of WLAN design methodology and the complete project lifecycle. Updated in 2023 to reflect Wi-Fi 6E (802.11ax), modern coverage tools, and high-density deployment patterns, it validates the ability to manage requirements, design networks for specific use cases (stadiums, hospitals, offices, outdoor venues), conduct pre- and post-deployment site surveys, and integrate with existing infrastructure. Held by wireless architects, RF engineers, and network consultants responsible for designing enterprise WLANs. Prerequisite: current CWNA-109. Passing: 70% (42/60 questions in 90 minutes).

## Domain context — Networking / Wireless Design

Wireless design is a specialized discipline within enterprise networking focused on optimizing radio-frequency (RF) propagation, antenna placement, channel planning, capacity management, and seamless roaming across multi-vendor infrastructure. It bridges RF physics with business requirements, translating performance targets into testable design specifications.

## Topics covered

The CWDP-303 exam blueprint covers nine primary knowledge domains:

- **WLAN Design Methodology & Project Lifecycle** (10%) — Design phases, stakeholder requirements gathering, budgeting, documentation, handoff to deployment teams, post-go-live support.
- **Pre-Deployment Site Survey** (15%) — Passive, active, and predictive survey methodologies; RF baseline measurement; interference identification; capacity profiling; site documentation; device density assessment.
- **RF Fundamentals & Propagation** (10%) — Wavelength, frequency, modulation, path loss, fading (multipath, shadowing, fast fading), reflection, refraction, diffraction, absorption, throughput vs. coverage trade-offs.
- **Coverage Requirements & Capacity Planning** (15%) — Design for specific use cases (office, warehouse, stadium, outdoor); user density calculations; throughput per AP; redundancy and failover strategies; voice, video, and IoT device requirements.
- **Access Point Placement & Antenna Selection** (15%) — Omnidirectional vs. directional antennas; sector antennas; MIMO benefits; antenna mounting height and orientation; placement algorithms; power management (TPC); channel overlap; interference mitigation.
- **Channel Planning & Reuse** (10%) — 2.4 GHz, 5 GHz, 6 GHz band characteristics; channel width selection (20/40/80/160 MHz); DFS and TPC regulatory requirements; co-channel interference (CCI) and adjacent-channel interference (ACI); channel assignment tools.
- **High-Density WLAN Design** (10%) — Arenas, convention centers, education campuses; ultra-dense AP deployments; beamforming and spatial reuse; band steering; load balancing; client density limits; latency and jitter optimization.
- **Wireless Bridge & Backhaul Design** (5%) — Point-to-point and point-to-multipoint bridging; backhaul topology; microwave alternatives; redundancy; throughput vs. latency trade-offs.
- **Post-Deployment Validation & Documentation** (10%) — Coverage verification; capacity validation; interference testing; client behavior assessment; performance reporting; design vs. reality reconciliation; continuous monitoring; design documentation templates.

## Skills validated

*Concrete technologies and methodologies tested in the CWDP-303 exam:*

- WLAN design methodology and project management (requirements → design → deployment → validation)
- Pre-deployment site survey techniques: passive, active, predictive modeling
- RF propagation theory: path loss, fading, multipath, interference identification
- Coverage planning for specific use cases (office, warehouse, stadium, outdoor, healthcare)
- Capacity planning: user density, throughput per AP, device types (mobile, VoIP, IoT)
- AP placement algorithms and optimization for performance and cost
- Antenna selection and placement: omnidirectional, directional, sector, MIMO
- Channel planning: 2.4 GHz, 5 GHz, 6 GHz band management; DFS; TPC compliance
- High-density WLAN design for arenas, convention centers, campuses
- Wireless bridge and backhaul design (point-to-point, point-to-multipoint)
- Post-deployment RF validation: coverage, capacity, interference testing
- VoWiFi design: latency, jitter, and packet loss thresholds
- IoT/device-dense WLAN design (Zigbee, Bluetooth, cellular coexistence)
- Guest network and VLAN segmentation in wireless designs
- Design documentation and reporting standards
- Integration with existing wired infrastructure (switching, routing, security)
- Wireless security design: 802.1X, WPA2/WPA3, rogue AP mitigation
