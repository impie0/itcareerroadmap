## About

The F5 Certified Technology Specialist – BIG-IP LTM (301a) exam validates expert-level competency in architecting, configuring, deploying, and optimizing F5 BIG-IP Local Traffic Manager (LTM) solutions in complex application delivery networks. This is the first of two specialist exams (301a and 301b) required to earn the full F5 Certified Technology Specialist, BIG-IP LTM credential. Candidates must first hold the F5 Certified Administrator BIG-IP (201) or demonstrate equivalent hands-on experience. The 301a focuses on initial design and deployment, while the 301b (Maintain and Troubleshoot) covers operations and advanced troubleshooting. Together, these form the specialist-level pathway for load-balancing architects and application delivery engineers.

## Domain context — Networking and Application Delivery

Application delivery platforms manage load balancing, SSL/TLS termination, content acceleration, traffic steering, and security across distributed infrastructure. BIG-IP (TMOS) is F5's flagship appliance, deployed globally by enterprises, cloud providers, and service providers. The LTM module specializes in Layer 4–7 local traffic management, iRules scripting, and advanced persistence and health monitoring.

## Topics covered

Based on the official F5 exam blueprint:

- **BIG-IP Architecture & Design Principles** — TMOS fundamentals, module licensing, resource allocation, performance considerations
- **Virtual Server Design** — Profile selection, policy binding, iRule integration, traffic processing chain
- **Advanced Pool Configuration** — Pool member selection algorithms, dynamic pool membership, priority groups, ratio weighting
- **Persistence Profiles** — Source IP, cookie-based, SSL session ID, and custom persistence mechanisms
- **Health Monitoring** — Custom health monitors, SNMP integration, monitor templates, timeout and interval tuning
- **iRules & Traffic Manipulation** — TCL syntax, event processing model, payload inspection and modification, request/response rewriting
- **SSL/TLS Offloading & Inspection** — Client and server SSL profiles, certificate chains, cipher suites, SSL options
- **Content Switching & Policies** — Layer 7 traffic steering, URI-based switching, host-based routing, policy rules
- **Application Profiles** — HTTP, FTP, DNS, SIP, RTMP profiles; compression and caching configurations
- **SNAT & Source Address Translation** — SNAT pools, dynamic SNAT, SNAT options
- **Advanced Networking** — VLANs, VXLAN, self IPs, routes, traffic groups for HA failover
- **High Availability & Clustering** — Active-standby configuration, ConfigSync, state failover, cluster management
- **BIG-IP DNS (GTM) Integration** — WideIP concepts, pool linking, iQuery for dynamic updates
- **Deployment & Best Practices** — Configuration backup, UCS export/restore, upgrade paths, capacity planning

*Source:* [F5 301a Exam Blueprint ↗](https://techdocs.f5.com/dam/f5/kb/global/solutions/K29900360.html/Exam301a_BluePrint.pdf)
