## About

The SAFe 6.0 Practitioner (SP) certification, also known as "SAFe for Teams," validates foundational expertise in executing Agile practices within a Scaled Agile Framework context. The cert is aimed at individual contributors, team members, and expert practitioners who work on Agile Teams within organizations practicing SAFe—not leadership roles (see SAFe Scrum Master for team-level coaching). Prerequisite is a mandatory 2-day course delivered by a certified SAFe Practice Consultant (SPC). The exam covers iteration execution, team ceremonies (standups, planning, reviews, retrospectives), collaboration with other teams, backlog management, and how AI tools can optimize personal and team productivity. Valid for 1 year; renewal requires annual SAFe Community Platform membership and either passing a renewal assessment or retaking the full exam.

## Domain context — Agile/Scrum/SAFe

Enterprise-scale Agile framework (SAFe) used by 650,000+ practitioners globally in financial services, technology, healthcare, manufacturing, and government sectors. SAFe extends Scrum to program and portfolio levels, adding synchronization across teams (PI Planning), release trains (ARTs), and governance layers. The Practitioner role in SAFe acts as a fully engaged, self-organizing team member within a larger scaled structure, practicing lean-agile principles and driving continuous improvement.

## Topics covered

The SP 6.0 exam covers six major domains:

- **Foundations of SAFe** — Lean-Agile principles, the Scaled Agile Framework structure, values (alignment, transparency, built-in quality, program execution), and iterative delivery mindset
- **Agile Release Train (ART) and Team Context** — Understanding the role of the Agile Release Train, team dependencies, portfolio strategy alignment, and how individual contributions roll up to program goals
- **Iterating in the Team** — Sprint/iteration ceremonies (planning, daily standup, review, retrospective), timeboxing, sustainable pace, team dynamics, and psychological safety
- **Planning the Iteration** — Breaking down PI objectives, story decomposition, estimation using story points, backlog refinement, and acceptance criteria
- **Executing the Iteration** — Sprint execution, daily standup patterns, managing work in progress (WIP), handling blockers, and maintaining velocity
- **Improving the Iteration** — Iteration retrospectives, continuous improvement (kaizen mindset), inspecting velocity trends, adapting team processes, and celebrating successes
