## About

The SAFe 6 Release Train Engineer (RTE) certification validates expertise in leading large-scale Agile Release Trains (ARTs) within the Scaled Agile Framework. RTEs serve as servant leaders who facilitate PI Planning, coach team execution, manage risk, and drive continuous improvement across multi-team delivery. This Professional-level cert requires completion of a 3-day vendor-delivered course and demonstrates readiness to manage release-train dynamics in enterprise environments. Introduced as part of SAFe's professional certification ladder, the RTE role is critical in organizations adopting SAFe at scale.

## Topics covered

The SAFe 6 RTE exam blueprint assesses competency across six weighted domains:

- **Lean-Agile Mindset** (5%) — Core SAFe values, Agile Manifesto, servant leadership principles
- **Building a High-Performing ART** (15%) — ART structure, role definitions, psychological safety, team dynamics
- **Planning and Executing PI** (35%) — PI Planning facilitation, PI execution, dependency management, metrics
- **Coaching ART Execution** (20%) — Iteration planning, system demos, inspect & adapt workshops, flow optimization
- **Managing Risks and Driving Relentless Improvement** (15%) — Risk identification, mitigation strategies, improvement frameworks
- **Coaching Other Agilists** (10%) — Mentoring Scrum Masters, facilitating organizational adoption

*Source:* [Scaled Agile exam guide ↗](https://support.scaledagile.com/en/articles/9791380-exam-study-guide-rte-2025-11-25-safe-release-train-engineer)

## Skills validated

*Concrete technologies and practices this exam assesses.*

- SAFe Framework (values, principles, ceremonies, roles, artifacts)
- Program Increment (PI) Planning facilitation and execution
- Agile Release Train (ART) leadership and team dynamics
- Lean value flow and metrics (velocity, CFD, cycle time, predictability)
- Risk management and dependencies across ARTs
- Servant-leadership coaching and conflict resolution
- DevOps and Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD) principles
- PI Objectives, Features, User Stories, and story estimation
- System and Solution Demos, Inspect & Adapt workshops
- Scaled Agile tooling (e.g., Jira, AzureDevOps) integration with SAFe cadence
