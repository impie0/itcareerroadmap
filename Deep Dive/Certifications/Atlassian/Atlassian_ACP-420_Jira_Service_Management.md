## About

The **Atlassian Certified Professional — Managing Jira Service Projects for Cloud (ACP-420)** is an Associate-level credential validating hands-on expertise in administering Jira Service Management (JSM) projects on Atlassian Cloud. Launched in 2023 and updated in 2025, this certification focuses on service desk operators, ITSM administrators, and JSM project admins responsible for configuring request portals, managing workflows, defining SLAs, implementing automation, and supporting customer and agent users. It covers service project setup, queue management, self-service configuration, asset management integration, and integration with Jira Software for cross-functional incident and change management. The credential is active and widely recognized in enterprises using JSM for IT service desks, customer support, and internal request management.

---

## Domain context — IT Service Management

Service desk and incident-response platform administration. Jira Service Management is the dominant cloud ITSM platform for enterprises transitioning from legacy service desk tools (ServiceNow, BMC Remedy, etc.) or coordinating dev-to-ops workflows via Jira integration. This domain encompasses request lifecycle, SLA enforcement, knowledge management, asset/CMDB integration, automation, and multi-team coordination.

## Topics covered

The ACP-420 exam blueprint (April 2025) covers:

- **Jira Service Management Project Configuration** (15–20%) — Creating request types, configuring fields and custom fields, field layouts, request type groups, portal customization
- **Request Portal & Channel Setup** (12–15%) — Customer portal configuration, email channel integration, request input, field visibility for customers vs. agents
- **Queue & Board Configuration** (12–15%) — Queue setup and filtering, board display (Kanban, Scrum-style), queue assignment rules, automation triggers on queue transitions
- **SLA Configuration & Time Metrics** (15–20%) — Goal setup (response time, resolution time), SLA calendars, business hours configuration, SLA tracking and escalation
- **Automation Rules in JSM** (10–15%) — Automation condition/action pairs, triggers, field updates, notifications, permission checks, integration with other Atlassian products
- **Permissions & Roles in JSM** (10–15%) — Agents, customers, approvers, observers, organization access, permission schemes, project roles, global vs. project-level permissions
- **Asset Management & CMDB Integration** (8–12%) — Asset creation, CMDB linking (Insight), asset requests, linking assets to incidents/changes
- **Incident, Change & Problem Management Workflows** (10–15%) — Status and transition design, workflow automation, change advisory boards, problem categorization
- **Jira Software Integration** (8–10%) — Linking service requests to development issues, development information display, two-way sync, handoff workflows
- **Reporting & Metrics** (5–8%) — Built-in reports (queue aging, SLA compliance, request trends), custom insights dashboards, metrics interpretation
- **Knowledge Management / Self-Service** (5–8%) — Article creation, portal knowledge base, self-service article suggestions, reducing ticket volume
