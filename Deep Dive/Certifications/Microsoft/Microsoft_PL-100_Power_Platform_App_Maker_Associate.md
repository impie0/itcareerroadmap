# PL-100 · Microsoft Power Platform App Maker Associate

**`PL-100`** · ● **Retired** (June 28, 2024) · **Associate** · _Microsoft_

> **IMPORTANT — RETIREMENT NOTICE:** This certification was officially retired on June 28, 2024. Microsoft has replaced it with a modular certification stack: **PL-7001** (Canvas Apps), **PL-7002** (Automated Processes), and **PL-7003** (Model-Driven Apps). Learners pursuing Power Platform certification should enroll in the PL-7000 series instead. This document is preserved for historical reference and for professionals who earned the PL-100 before its retirement.

## About

The PL-100 was a foundational Microsoft Power Platform certification for citizen developers and app makers who design, build, and deploy low-code business solutions using Power Apps, Power Automate, and Power BI. The credential validated skills in creating canvas and model-driven apps, automating business processes, and analyzing data to solve operational problems. Released around 2020, it remained a core entry point for the Power Platform ecosystem until its retirement on June 28, 2024. Professionals who earned the PL-100 before that date retain the credential for one year following their pass date, after which they must transition to the modular PL-7000 series to maintain currency.

## Domain context — Low-Code / Business Applications

Low-code / no-code platforms empower business users and citizen developers to build enterprise applications without deep programming expertise. Microsoft Power Platform is a leading ecosystem in this space, alongside Salesforce (Salesforce Platform / Apex), ServiceNow, and others. Apps built on these platforms automate workflows, surface data insights, and extend enterprise systems—typically with faster time-to-value and lower total cost of ownership than custom code.

## Topics covered

The PL-100 exam blueprint measured skills across four core domains (weights per Microsoft official blueprint):

- **Design business solutions** (20–25%) — Analyzing requirements, designing app architecture, planning data models, considering governance and security
- **Analyze and visualize data** (10–15%) — Building Power BI reports and dashboards, connecting data sources, creating charts and KPIs, surfacing insights
- **Create business solutions** (60–65%) — Building canvas apps and model-driven apps, designing user interfaces, implementing business logic, configuring Power Automate flows
- **Implement and manage solutions** (none explicit, embedded in "Create") — Deploying apps, managing environments, configuring permissions, monitoring performance

*Source:* [Microsoft Exam Readiness Live: PL-100 modules ↗](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/shows/exam-readiness-zone/)

## Skills validated

*Specific technologies and concepts tested by PL-100.*

- **Canvas Apps** — UI-first, responsive design; formulas and expressions (Power Fx)
- **Model-Driven Apps** — Form and view design; metadata-driven architecture; Dataverse integration
- **Power Automate** — Cloud flows, desktop flows, business process flows; connectors and actions
- **Power BI** — Reports, dashboards, measures, DAX basics
- **Dataverse** — Tables, columns, relationships, data import/export, environments
- **Power Apps Portals** — Low-code customer/partner portals
- **Security and governance** — Environment management, role-based access, solution packaging
- **Integration** — Connecting to on-premises and cloud data sources (SQL, SharePoint, Dynamics, custom APIs)
