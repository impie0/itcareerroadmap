## About

The MS-721 certifies IT professionals as Microsoft 365 Collaboration Communications Systems Engineers — specialists in planning, deploying, configuring, managing, and troubleshooting Teams Phone, Teams meetings, Teams Rooms, and modern UC infrastructure within the Microsoft 365 ecosystem. Launched in 2023, MS-721 replaced the retired Skype for Business infrastructure exams and reflects Microsoft's shift toward cloud-first unified communications. Candidates should have hands-on experience with Teams Phone administration, Direct Routing, Operator Connect, or similar cloud-calling deployments. The exam was substantially updated in February 2026 to reflect new Teams Premium features, Teams Rooms Pro Management, and advanced telephony scenarios.

## Topics covered

The MS-721 exam covers five major domain areas:

- **Plan and design collaboration communications systems** (20–25%) — Voice strategy, telephony architecture, network requirements, calling plans vs. Direct Routing, SIP trunk selection.
- **Configure and manage Teams meetings, webinars, and town halls** (15–20%) — Meeting policies, recording & transcription, live events, Teams Premium, attendee experience, device management.
- **Implement and configure Teams Phone** (30–35%) — Auto attendants, call queues, calling plans, Operator Connect, Direct Routing, emergency calling, dial plans, number management.
- **Manage Teams Rooms and devices** (10–15%) — Microsoft Teams Rooms deployment, Pro Management, device firmware, troubleshooting, analytics.
- **Monitor, troubleshoot, and optimize Teams Phone and communications** (10–15%) — Call quality analytics, logging and diagnostics, performance tuning, user support escalation.
