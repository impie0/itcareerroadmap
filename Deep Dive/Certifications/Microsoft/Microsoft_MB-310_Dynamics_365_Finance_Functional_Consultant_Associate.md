## About

MB-310 is Microsoft's dedicated certification for Dynamics 365 Finance functional consultants. Released in its current form in January 2024 (replacing the legacy MB-300 prerequisite model), this exam validates the ability to analyze business requirements, configure D365 Finance to support core financial operations, implement general ledger workflows, manage accounts payable/receivable, handle budgeting and fixed assets, and deliver financial reporting and consolidation. The certification targets consultants working for Microsoft partners or internal IT teams deploying D365 Finance in mid-market to enterprise environments, particularly in manufacturing, distribution, and professional services where multi-currency and complex GL hierarchies are common.

**Prerequisite requirement (critical):** Earning the full "Microsoft Certified: Dynamics 365 Finance Functional Consultant Associate" credential requires **both** the MB-310 exam **and** the PL-200 (Power Platform Functional Consultant) certification. This reflects Microsoft's strategy of bundling D365 functional certs with Power Platform fluency (Power Automate, Power Apps, dataverse integration). However, candidates may sit and pass MB-310 standalone without holding PL-200; the credential is awarded only when both are held.

**Career trajectory:** Entry point for ERP finance professionals transitioning from on-premises systems (SAP, Oracle, NetSuite) or accounting backgrounds. Typical progression: Accountant / Finance Analyst → D365 Finance Business Analyst → D365 Finance Functional Consultant (with MB-310) → Senior Consultant / Solution Architect (MB-700 track). Related specializations: D365 Supply Chain (MB-330), D365 Human Resources (MB-380), or lateral move to D365 Business Central (MB-800).

## Exam prep timeline & industry demand

**Study duration:** For candidates with prior D365 or on-premises ERP experience (SAP, Oracle, NetSuite), 4–8 weeks of part-time study suffices. For career-changers from accounting/finance without ERP tech background, 10–14 weeks is more realistic. Finance professionals already familiar with GL, AP/AR processes can focus purely on D365 mechanics; technical background candidates must also learn domain fundamentals.

**Recommended study sequence:**
1. Complete Microsoft Learn module path (free; ~20 hours guided learning)
2. Study official exam guide (Microsoft Learn PDF; 2–3 hours review)
3. Watch instructor-led course (Pluralsight, Udemy, AXcademy; 15–30 hours depending on pace)
4. Hands-on lab environment (Microsoft Learn sandbox or trial tenant; 10–15 hours)
5. Practice exams (MeasureUp, Pearson official; 3–5 full practice runs, 2–3 hours each)
6. Weak-area drills (focus on lowest-scoring domains from practice tests; 5–10 hours)

**Industry demand (2026):** D365 Finance positions remain in high demand, particularly among Microsoft partners (Capgemini, Accenture, Deloitte, EY, KPMG) and mid-market manufacturers/distributors transitioning from on-prem ERP. Estimated 5,000–8,000 active job postings in North America listing D365 Finance experience at any given time. Recruitment window is 2–3 months from certification (passive job boards move slowly); active job-board searches and LinkedIn recruiter outreach yield placements within 1–2 weeks for strong candidates with hands-on project experience (not cert-only).

## Career advancement strategies

**Certification-to-project pathway:** MB-310 alone does not guarantee employment; hands-on experience is critical. Strategy: pursue training at a Microsoft partner (Capgemini, Accenture, Deloitte, EY, KPMG, Merkle, Wyle, etc.) that offers "cert + project placement" programs, or negotiate a contract-to-hire role immediately after passing. First 2–3 years should be spent on full-cycle implementations (greenfield deployments or lift-and-shift migrations from legacy ERP), not support/maintenance. Employers value breadth across GL, AP, AR, budgeting, and consolidation.

**Specialization options post-MB-310:**
- **Finance depth:** Pursue MB-700 (Solution Architect Expert) or specialized master-data certifications (GL hierarchies, segment reporting, compliance frameworks). Become the "finance rules engine" expert for your consulting firm.
- **Supply Chain companion:** Earn MB-330 (Supply Chain Management) in parallel or within 12 months. Manufacturing/distribution clients often require both; dual certification commands 10–15% pay premium and expands addressable project pool.
- **Developer track:** Transition to MB-500 (Developer) if you enjoy technical customization (X++ extensions, plugins, extensions). Takes 6–12 months of additional study and hands-on coding.
- **Architecture path:** After 3–5 years consultant experience (and MB-700 exam), move to Solution Architect or presales Engineering roles ($150K–$220K USD range).

**Hands-on experience multiplier:** Each successful full-cycle D365 Finance implementation adds ~2–3% to salary range (beyond standard promotion cadence). By year 5 with 5–7 implementations under your belt, expect $140K–$180K USD total comp (salary + bonus). High-touch, high-stakes implementations (multi-entity consolidation, international cash management, regulatory compliance) add premium valuations.

**Industry/vertical specialization:** D365 Finance adoption varies by industry. Manufacturers (discrete + process) and distributors have the highest implementation velocity. Specializing in one vertical (e.g., automotive manufacturing, pharmaceutical/biotech, food & beverage distribution) allows you to command 15–20% premium due to deep domain knowledge (e.g., multi-site inventory accounting, batch lot-tracking GL posting, cGMP compliance). Government/public sector D365 Finance roles exist but are fewer and lower-paid; avoid unless you have clearance interest.

**Geographic arbitrage:** D365 Finance consultant salaries vary dramatically. US/Canada/UK consultants earn 2–3x Indian/Philippines equivalents. Remote work has partially flattened this, but employers often adjust offers based on location. Consider: relocate to US (especially technology hubs: Seattle, Austin, San Francisco, Charlotte) for 5-year stint, bank aggressively, then relocate to lower cost-of-living country and maintain US client base (via remote). South African consultants can often access London/NYC contracts (via Microsoft partners) at GBP/USD rates while living in ZAR markets.
