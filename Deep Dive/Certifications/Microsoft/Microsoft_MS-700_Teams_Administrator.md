## About

The MS-700 validates the skills of Microsoft Teams administrators who plan, implement, and manage Microsoft Teams environments for internal and external collaboration. Launched October 2021, it covers Teams architecture, governance, security compliance, calling/meeting infrastructure, and real-world troubleshooting. The certification demonstrates readiness for mid-level Teams administration roles managing enterprise deployments. Version 1.1 (current 2026) emphasizes network automation, security fundamentals, and AI integration alongside traditional Teams management.

## Topics covered

Official exam blueprint (2026 v1.1):

- **Plan and configure Microsoft Teams environment** (45–50%)
  - Network planning and optimization
  - Microsoft Teams licensing and subscriptions
  - Teams architecture and infrastructure
  - Microsoft Teams policies and settings
  
- **Manage chat, teams, channels, and apps** (25–30%)
  - Create and manage teams and channels
  - Manage external users and guest access
  - Configure Teams apps and integrations
  - Manage Teams data and governance
  
- **Manage meetings and calling** (15–20%)
  - Plan and manage meetings
  - Configure calling and phone systems
  - Manage meeting policies and room devices
  
- **Monitor and troubleshoot Microsoft Teams environment** (5–10%)
  - Monitor Teams health and diagnostics
  - Troubleshoot communication issues (audio, video, client)
  - Review audit logs and reports

*Source:* [Official exam objectives ↗](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/resources/study-guides/ms-700)

## Skills validated

*Specific technologies and capabilities tested by MS-700 beyond general M365 knowledge.*

- Microsoft Teams deployment architecture (hybrid, cloud-native)
- Teams policies: messaging, meeting, calling, app permission policies
- Teams governance: naming conventions, lifecycle management, retention
- Azure AD and Microsoft Entra integration for Teams identity
- Phone System and Direct Routing configuration
- Meeting recording, transcription, and compliance capture
- Teams security: data loss prevention, threat protection
- Guest access and external sharing controls
- App Store, custom apps, and third-party app governance
- Teams analytics, audit logs, and diagnostic tools
- Network planning: bandwidth, Quality of Service (QoS)
- Troubleshooting: connectivity, audio/video quality, client issues
