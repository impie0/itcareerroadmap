# Microsoft Certified: Information Protection and Compliance Administrator Associate

**`SC-400`** · ● **Retired (May 31, 2025)** · **Associate** · _Microsoft_

> **STATUS UPDATE:** SC-400 was retired on May 31, 2025, at 11:59 PM CST. Existing certifications remain valid and visible on transcripts, but no new candidates can sit the exam, and renewal is no longer possible after that date. Microsoft replaced this credential with **SC-401 (Information Security Administrator)**, which focuses specifically on data security and information protection. This page documents SC-400 for historical reference; candidates should pursue SC-401 for current Information Security credentials.

## About

SC-400 was Microsoft's role-based certification for information protection and compliance professionals, launched in February 2021. It validated hands-on expertise in implementing data protection, sensitivity labeling, data loss prevention (DLP), retention policies, eDiscovery, and audit in Microsoft 365 environments. Target audience included compliance engineers, data governance analysts, Microsoft Purview administrators, and DLP specialists managing information protection across hybrid and cloud-first organizations. The exam assumed foundational knowledge of Microsoft 365, cloud security principles, and preferably prior hands-on experience with Purview and compliance features.

**Retirement Rationale:** Microsoft retired SC-400 on May 31, 2025, recognizing that the original exam scope conflated two distinct professional roles—data security/information protection specialists and compliance/governance professionals. The retirement feedback indicated that organizations needed more specialized credentials for these separate skill sets. Microsoft replaced SC-400 with **SC-401 (Information Security Administrator)**, which focuses exclusively on data security, information protection, and insider risk management in Microsoft 365. Compliance-focused roles will be covered under a separate certification stream (not yet released as of 2026).

**For existing SC-400 holders:** Certifications earned before the retirement date remain valid and visible on transcripts indefinitely. No renewal was required after May 31, 2025 — the credential is permanently recorded. New candidates cannot sit SC-400 after the retirement date.

## Topics covered (archived exam blueprint)

The exam covered information protection and compliance across four domains, weighted as follows:

- **Implement information protection and data lifecycle management in Microsoft Purview (35–40%)** — Sensitivity labels (classification, encryption, marking, auto-labeling), sensitivity label policies, Purview Information Protection scanner, data lifecycle management (retention labels, retention policies, records management), content explorer and activity explorer
- **Implement data loss prevention (30–35%)** — DLP policies (Exchange, SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams, Endpoints), DLP policy conditions and actions, endpoint DLP, Defender for Cloud Apps integration with DLP, DLP reports and alerts
- **Implement and manage Microsoft Purview eDiscovery and Audit (20–25%)** — eDiscovery (Standard and Premium), search and export, holds, custodians, audit (standard and premium), log queries in Microsoft Purview

*Source:* [Official exam guide (archived) ↗](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/resources/study-guides/sc-400)

- Configuration of sensitivity labels, retention policies, and DLP policies

- Knowledge of regulatory requirements and their technical enforcement in Microsoft 365
- Experience with security and compliance reporting, monitoring, and alerting
