## About

The ISTQB Certified Tester Advanced Level Test Management (CTAL-TM) is a professional-level certification validating competency in managing all testing activities across the software development lifecycle. Released in v3.0 in 2024, it supersedes the v2.12 version, which retires in May 2025 for English-language exams. The certification addresses test planning, team leadership, budget and resource allocation, risk-based testing methodologies, and organizational test strategy alignment. It is targeted at test managers, QA leaders, Scrum masters, project managers, and anyone in roles requiring oversight of testing operations and quality assurance initiatives.

---

## Domain context — QA/Test Management

Test management and quality assurance leadership encompass the practices, tools, and organizational frameworks for controlling software testing activities, directing test teams, allocating budgets, managing risk, and establishing testing strategies aligned with business objectives. The CTAL-TM validates advanced expertise within this domain.

---

## Topics covered

*From official ISTQB CTAL-TM v3.0 Syllabus:*

- **Test Planning & Organization** — Defining test scope, objectives, approach, and resource allocation based on organizational test strategy; designing test phases and schedules
- **Test Team Leadership** — Selecting and developing test staff; managing team competencies; building and mentoring test teams; establishing team culture
- **Test Monitoring & Control** — Defining and collecting test metrics (coverage, defect density, schedule adherence); interpreting metrics to inform decisions; managing test execution; communicating status
- **Defect Management** — Defining defect classification schemes; tracking defects; analyzing defect trends; managing defect resolution workflows
- **Risk-Based Testing** — Identifying product and project risks; prioritizing testing based on risk analysis; managing test coverage against risk exposure
- **Test Process Improvement** — Evaluating and enhancing test processes; applying continuous improvement methodologies; managing testing maturity
- **Budget & Resource Planning** — Allocating budgets; managing test tool procurement; forecasting resource needs; managing vendor relationships

*Source:* [ISTQB CTAL-TM Syllabus v3.0 ↗](https://isqi.org/media/56/57/20/1718264547/ISTQB_CTAL-TM_Syllabus_v3.0_ALL_.pdf)

## Common skills at QA/Test Management · Professional

*Shared competencies for test management roles at professional level:*

- **Test strategy and planning** — Defining organizational test approaches; aligning testing with business goals; prioritizing testing activities
- **Risk assessment and mitigation** — Identifying testing risks; implementing risk-based testing priorities; managing quality trade-offs
- **Team management and mentorship** — Building high-performing test teams; developing staff competencies; providing technical and career guidance
- **Metrics and data-driven decision-making** — Collecting, interpreting, and communicating quality metrics; using data to forecast schedule and budget impact
- **Stakeholder communication** — Presenting testing status, risks, and recommendations to technical and non-technical audiences; managing expectations
- **Process governance** — Implementing testing standards; ensuring process compliance; driving continuous improvement initiatives
