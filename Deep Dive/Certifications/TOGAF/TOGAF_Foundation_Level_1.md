# TOGAF 10 Enterprise Architecture Foundation (Level 1)

The TOGAF Foundation certification is the entry-level certification from The Open Group, validating foundational knowledge of enterprise architecture concepts, terminology, and the TOGAF Architecture Development Method (ADM).

## About

The TOGAF Foundation certification demonstrates that a candidate has achieved a level of competency consistent with the Foundation Level of the TOGAF Learning Experience. This certification validates:

- Understanding of TOGAF terminology and concepts
- Knowledge of the Architecture Development Method (ADM) phases (A through H)
- Familiarity with Architecture Building Blocks (ABBs) and Solution Building Blocks (SBBs)
- Comprehension of enterprise architecture deliverables and governance
- Basic ability to contribute to organizations using TOGAF as an enterprise architecture framework

Badge earners can use TOGAF concepts, communicate with EA teams, and understand organizational change management at a foundational level.

## Domain Context — Enterprise Architecture

Enterprise Architecture (EA) is a business discipline that helps organizations align IT strategy with business goals. TOGAF is the most widely adopted framework globally, used by Fortune 500 companies, government agencies, and large enterprises to:

- Standardize architecture methods and terminology across teams
- Reduce risk in major transformation projects
- Improve stakeholder communication
- Create repeatable, governance-driven change processes
- Bridge business and technology initiatives

The TOGAF Standard, 10th Edition (released 2022) includes:
- Enhanced ADM guidance
- Architecture governance frameworks
- Stakeholder management
- Digital transformation considerations
- Cloud and emerging technology integration

## Topics Covered

### Architecture Development Method (ADM) Phases

The core of TOGAF, the ADM is an iterative, eight-phase cycle:

| Phase | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Phase A: Architecture Vision** | Define business requirements, establish sponsorship, scope, constraints, and high-level architecture vision |
| **Phase B: Business Architecture** | Define the business operating model, value chains, and stakeholder roles |
| **Phase C: Information Systems Architecture** | Design data and application architecture to support business |
| **Phase D: Technology Architecture** | Define infrastructure, platforms, and technical services |
| **Phase E: Opportunities & Solutions** | Identify consolidated requirements, packages, and transformation roadmap |
| **Phase F: Migration Planning** | Create detailed implementation and deployment plans |
| **Phase G: Implementation Governance** | Oversee implementation and ensure compliance with architecture |
| **Phase H: Architecture Change Management** | Manage updates to architecture post-implementation |
| **Preliminary Phase** | Define organizational context, framework, and principles |

### Key Concepts

- **Architecture Building Blocks (ABBs)**: Logical business/information/application components
- **Solution Building Blocks (SBBs)**: Concrete technology solutions that implement ABBs
- **Architecture Governance**: Frameworks and processes ensuring standards compliance
- **Stakeholder Management**: Identifying and managing diverse stakeholder interests
- **Architecture Deliverables**: Artifacts produced at each ADM phase (viewpoints, models, matrices)
- **Enterprise Architecture Repository**: Centralized storage of architecture assets
- **Architecture Principles**: Rules guiding architectural decisions
- **Business Scenarios**: Narrative descriptions of business requirements and outcomes
- **Architecture Patterns**: Reusable solutions to recurring architecture problems

## Exam Strategy & Tips

### Before the Exam
- Study the 8 ADM phases until you can describe each in detail
- Memorize key acronyms (ABB, SBB, BoK, RAP, RUP, etc.)
- Focus on the *processes* and *deliverables* of each phase
- Practice with official exam questions multiple times
- Review The Open Group glossary for precise terminology

### During the Exam
- Read each question twice before answering
- Flag questions you're unsure about and review them at the end
- Manage time: ~1.5 minutes per question on average
- Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
- Trust your study; don't second-guess correct answers

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Confusing ADM phases (A-H are in sequence)
- Mixing up ABBs (logical) vs. SBBs (concrete)
- Assuming hands-on implementation skills = certification (it doesn't)
- Overlooking governance and stakeholder management content
- Not studying business architecture deeply enough (~30% of exam)
