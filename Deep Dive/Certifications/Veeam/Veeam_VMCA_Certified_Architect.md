## About

The Veeam Certified Architect (VMCA) was the highest level of Veeam data protection certification, designed for architects, senior engineers, and solution designers responsible for enterprise-scale backup and disaster recovery deployments. Launched in 2022 with v12.1 curriculum, the VMCA demonstrated mastery of the Veeam Architecture Methodology—a six-phase process (Discovery, Conceptual Design, Logical Design, Physical Design, Implementation/Governance, Validation/Iteration) for designing secure, scalable backup solutions. The certification was discontinued November 30, 2025, as Veeam (acquired by Commvault in 2024) restructured its certification roadmap. Existing VMCA holders retain valid credentials; the successor pathway includes VMCSE and VMCE+.

## Topics covered

- **Discovery & Assessment** – Assessing customer infrastructure, workloads, RPO/RTO requirements
- **Conceptual Design** – High-level backup topology and strategy alignment with business goals
- **Logical Design** – Repository selection (fast clone, dedupe, object storage), proxy placement, replication streams
- **Physical Design** – Hardware sizing, network bandwidth, storage configuration for Veeam Availability Suite components
- **Implementation & Governance** – Deployment methodology, policies, compliance integration (GDPR, HIPAA, etc.)
- **Validation & Iteration** – Testing recovery procedures, optimization, continuous improvement
- **Security & Encryption** – Backup immutability, encryption in-flight and at-rest, role-based access control
- **High-Availability Solutions** – Multi-site redundancy, failover automation, business continuity planning
- **Cloud & Hybrid Architecture** – AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, and on-premises backup integration
