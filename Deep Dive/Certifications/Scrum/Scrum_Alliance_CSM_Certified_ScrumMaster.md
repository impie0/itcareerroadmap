## About

The Certified ScrumMaster (CSM) is an entry-level practitioner credential for individuals who understand the Scrum framework and can apply it in real-world environments. Unlike some certifications, CSM requires mandatory attendance at a 2-day live training course delivered by a Certified Scrum Trainer (CST) before sitting the exam. The exam itself is online and unproctored, delivered immediately after course completion or within 90 days. CSM is one of the most widely recognized agile practitioner credentials, particularly valued in organizations adopting Scrum for product development and project delivery.

## Topics covered

Official Scrum Alliance CSM exam domains:

- **Scrum Framework Fundamentals** — roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team), events (Sprint, Sprint Planning, Daily Standup, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective), artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment)
- **Scrum Master Accountability** — servant-leader role, removing impediments, facilitating Scrum events, coaching team and organization on empiricism
- **Empiricism & Self-Management** — transparency, inspection, adaptation; supporting Development Team autonomy and self-organization
- **Scrum Values** — Commitment, Focus, Openness, Respect, Courage
- **Working with the Product Owner** — backlog refinement, stakeholder management, ensuring clear user value
- **Scaling & Organizational Context** — applying Scrum in multi-team environments, dependencies, organizational change

## CSM vs. PSM I: Key differences

Many practitioners ask whether to pursue Scrum Alliance's **CSM** or Scrum.org's **PSM I**. Both certify Scrum Master competency but differ in approach:

| Aspect | CSM | PSM I |
|---|---|---|
| **Training** | Required 2-day live course from CST (Scrum Alliance) | Optional; can test without training (Scrum.org) |
| **Exam** | 50 questions, 60 min, 74% passing, unproctored | 80 questions, 180 min, 85% passing, proctored (more rigorous) |
| **Cost** | $700–$1,500 (course + exam included) | $200 (exam only); optional courses available separately |
| **Renewal** | 20 SEUs + $100 fee every 2 years | Lifetime (no renewal required) |
| **Career Path** | A-CSM → CSP-SM (advanced tracks) | PSM II, PSM III (multi-level progression) |
| **Reputation** | Workshop-oriented, practical coaching emphasis | Rigorous assessment, appeals to experienced practitioners |

**Best for CSM:** Organizations valuing structured workshop training, hands-on facilitation skills, and shorter time-to-certification.  
**Best for PSM I:** Individual practitioners with prior agile experience seeking validation and lifetime credential without renewal burden.

Many practitioners pursue **both** to signal comprehensive Scrum knowledge across assessment styles.
