## About

The Professional Scrum Master I (PSM I) is Scrum.org's foundational Associate-level certification, demonstrating that you understand the Scrum framework as described in the Scrum Guide and can apply it in team contexts. Created by Ken Schwaber (Scrum co-inventor) and maintained by Scrum.org, PSM I validates knowledge of Scrum roles (Product Owner, Development Team, Scrum Master), events (Sprint Planning, Daily Standup, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective, Refinement), and artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment). Unlike the CSM (Certified Scrum Master) from the Scrum Alliance, PSM I requires no course prerequisite and is assessed entirely online without proctoring. The exam is demanding (85% passing threshold is high industry standard) and focuses on scenario-based judgment rather than memorization. First released in 2011, PSM I remains the most widely recognized entry-level Scrum credential and a common requirement in job postings for Scrum Masters, Agile Coaches, and Agile Project Managers.

Agile and Scrum represent the dominant iterative, incremental software development and product-delivery paradigm adopted by enterprises across all industries (not just software). Scrum is the most popular Agile framework globally (>80% adoption among Agile teams). The Scrum Master role sits at the intersection of team enablement, coaching, and organizational change; PSM I is the entry-level credential for this role.

## Topics covered

*Based on Scrum Guide 2024 and official Scrum.org exam syllabus.*

**Core Scrum Framework (60% of exam)**
- Scrum roles: Product Owner, Development Team (Developers), Scrum Master accountabilities and interactions
- Scrum events: Sprint Planning (goal + scope), Daily Standup (transparency + impediment identification), Sprint Review (inspect increment + gather feedback), Sprint Retrospective (inspect process + commit to improvement)
- Sprint Refinement / Backlog Grooming: Product Owner involvement, team preparation, story sizing, Definition of Done
- Scrum artifacts: Product Backlog (prioritized, emergent), Sprint Backlog (committed forecast + pull mechanism), Increment (potentially shippable, done work)
- The Scrum Values: Commitment, Focus, Openness, Respect, Courage — and how they guide team interaction

**Scrum Master Accountabilities & Servant Leadership (25% of exam)**
- Scrum Master as servant-leader, not command-and-control manager
- Coaching the Product Owner: refining backlog, stakeholder engagement, value maximization
- Coaching the Development Team: self-organization, technical excellence, Definition of Done evolution
- Removing impediments and blockers; escalation vs. problem-solving mindset
- Facilitating Scrum events without dominating or prescribing solutions
- Protecting the team from mid-sprint scope changes and interruption
- Organizational change facilitation: working with management, HR, other teams; advocating for Scrum adoption

**Common Challenges & Practical Judgment (15% of exam)**
- Handling partial team attendance, understaffing, technical debt accumulation
- Difficult conversations: with Product Owners over unrealistic commitments, with management over quarter-end disruptions
- Multi-team coordination (Scrum of Scrums, cross-team dependencies)
- Working with distributed / remote teams; asynchronous daily standups, time zone challenges
- Metrics that matter: velocity trends, cycle time, burndown interpretation (what burndown does NOT tell you)
- When and how to break Scrum (minimal acceptable subset) vs. when NOT to compromise on roles/events/artifacts

*Source:* [Scrum Guide 2024 ↗](https://scrumguides.org/scrum-guide.html)

## Common skills at Agile / Scrum · Associate

*Shared competencies for Agile and Scrum professionals at Associate level — not specific to PSM I.*

- Iterative / incremental delivery mindset (Agile Manifesto values)
- Empirical process control: transparency, inspection, adaptation
- Backlog prioritization and value-driven delivery
- Sprint planning and estimation (story points, velocity)
- Daily standup facilitation and impediment tracking
- Retrospective running and process improvement
- Stakeholder communication and expectation management
- Cross-functional team collaboration
- Basic Agile metrics (velocity, burndown, cycle time interpretation)
- Introduction to Scrum scaling (Scrum of Scrums, SAFe, Nexus, LeSS concepts)

## Skills validated

*Cert-specific — concrete Scrum knowledge and judgment this exam validates.*

- **Scrum Guide mastery:** All three roles (Scrum Master, Product Owner, Developers), five events, three artifacts, Scrum Values, Definition of Done, Sprint mechanics
- **Servant leadership & coaching:** Facilitating without directing, removing blockers, helping teams self-organize, addressing conflict, stakeholder management
- **Event facilitation:** Sprint Planning (goal + forecasting), Daily Standup (impediment focus), Sprint Review (feedback gathering), Sprint Retrospective (process improvement commitment)
- **Backlog management:** Product Backlog ownership (not SM job, but SM understands), Sprint Backlog pull mechanism, estimation, refinement process
- **Scrum in practice:** Handling team absences, mid-sprint changes, stakeholder interruptions, velocity/burndown interpretation, common anti-patterns
- **Organizational context:** Working with managers, HR, other teams, advocating for Scrum without being dogmatic, incremental adoption
