## About

The Certified Information Privacy Professional / Europe (CIPP/E) is a vendor-neutral, globally-recognized credential that validates advanced knowledge of European data protection law, specifically the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and ePrivacy Directive. Launched by the International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP), this exam is widely considered the gold standard for privacy professionals operating in or advising on European compliance. The CIPP/E targets legal counsel, compliance officers, data protection officers, and privacy engineers who need demonstrated mastery of GDPR's complex requirements, member state implementations, and real-world compliance scenarios.

The current exam blueprint (effective September 2025) reflects the evolving regulatory landscape, including coverage of the EU AI Act and NIS2 Directive alongside core GDPR topics. Approximately 30 hours of dedicated study is recommended for preparation.

## Topics covered

The CIPP/E Body of Knowledge (BoK) is organized into two major domains with question weights per the official exam blueprint:

**European Data Protection Law and Regulation** (18–28 scored questions)
- EU Data Protection Directive (Directive 95/46/EC) history and evolution
- GDPR structure, recitals, and key definitions
- Personal data, processing, controller, processor, data subject
- Special categories of personal data (sensitive data)
- Processing requirements and transparency principles
- Data subject rights (access, erasure, portability, objection, restriction, rectification)
- Lawful bases for processing (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests)
- Data controller obligations (privacy by design, data protection impact assessments / DPIAs)
- Records of processing and accountability
- Data Protection Officers (DPO) role, independence, and responsibilities
- Supervisory authorities and the one-stop-shop mechanism
- Administrative fines (up to €20 million or 4% global annual turnover)
- Breach notification (72-hour reporting to authorities, data subject communication)

**European Data Processing** (13–21 scored questions)
- Data processing agreements (DPA) between controller and processor
- International data transfers (Standard Contractual Clauses / SCCs, Binding Corporate Rules / BCRs, adequacy decisions, Schrems II implications)
- ePrivacy Directive (Directive 2002/58/EC) and cookies / electronic communications
- National implementations across EU member states
- Employment data and HR privacy
- Children's data protection (Article 8)
- Privacy by design and privacy impact assessments
- Technical and organizational security measures
- Interaction with GDPR in context of emerging regulations (AI Act, NIS2)

*Source:* [CIPP/E Body of Knowledge 2024–2025 ↗](https://www.privacybootcamp.com/Resources/Article/cipp-e-body-of-knowledge-2024-2025) · [IAPP Exam Blueprint ↗](https://iapp.org/certify/cippe)
