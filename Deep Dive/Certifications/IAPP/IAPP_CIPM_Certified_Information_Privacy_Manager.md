## About

The Certified Information Privacy Manager (CIPM) is a vendor-neutral, globally-recognized credential that validates advanced knowledge of privacy program management, governance, and operations. Launched by the International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP), the CIPM is distinct from the CIPP (which emphasizes legal/regulatory knowledge) and CIPT (which emphasizes technology). The CIPM targets privacy program managers, data protection officers with operational focus, chief privacy officers, privacy operations managers, and compliance leaders responsible for building and maintaining privacy programs across organizations. The current exam blueprint reflects real-world privacy program lifecycle management: framework design, governance structures, risk assessment, operational procedures, incident response, and continuous improvement.

Approximately 30–40 hours of dedicated study is recommended for preparation.

## Topics covered

The CIPM Body of Knowledge (BoK) is organized into six domains with question weights per the official exam blueprint:

**Privacy Program Framework** (7–17 scored questions)
- Privacy program definition, maturity, and evolution
- Organizational structure and scope
- Accountability and governance models
- Privacy program charter and mandate

**Privacy Program Governance** (7–17 scored questions)
- Governance models and frameworks
- Roles and responsibilities (CPO, DPO, Privacy Analysts, Cross-functional teams)
- Staffing and resource planning
- Executive sponsorship and board reporting
- Committee structures and escalation paths

**Privacy Risk Framework** (11–21 scored questions)
- Privacy risk identification and assessment methodology
- Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) and Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)
- Risk thresholds and scoring systems
- Vendor/processor risk management
- Third-party assessment and due diligence

**Operationalizing Privacy** (11–21 scored questions)
- Data lifecycle management (collection, use, retention, deletion)
- Privacy notices and transparency documentation
- Privacy policies and procedures
- Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) and contract terms
- Consent management and opt-out mechanisms
- Data subject access request (DSAR) procedures

**Monitoring, Measuring, and Auditing** (11–21 scored questions)
- Privacy metrics, KPIs, and dashboards
- Privacy maturity assessments and roadmaps
- Privacy auditing and program reviews
- Compliance monitoring and control testing
- Privacy training effectiveness
- Continuous improvement frameworks

**Managing Privacy Incidents** (5–15 scored questions)
- Incident detection and classification
- Breach response procedures and timelines
- Notification obligations and requirements
- Regulatory reporting and communication
- Post-incident analysis and remediation

*Source:* [CIPM Body of Knowledge ↗](https://iapp.org/certify/cipm) · [IAPP Exam Blueprint ↗](https://iapp.org/certify/cipm)
