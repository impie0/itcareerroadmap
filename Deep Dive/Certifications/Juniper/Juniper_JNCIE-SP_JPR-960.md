## About

The **JNCIE-SP (JPR-960)** is Juniper Networks' flagship hands-on lab exam validating mastery of large-scale service provider network design, implementation, and troubleshooting. Candidates build and operate a realistic multi-device service provider topology using Juniper virtual routers (vMX), demonstrating expertise in advanced IGP protocols (IS-IS, OSPF), MPLS (RSVP-TE, segment routing), BGP route reflection and confederations, L2/L3 VPN services, multicast (MVPN), and carrier-class QoS. Launched alongside JNCIE as a vendor-agnostic tier above JNCIP-SP, the lab exam became an on-site practical to enforce rigorous hands-on validation. JNCIE-SP holders are exceptionally rare (~0.1% of networking professionals) and typically occupy senior architecture and specialist roles at global carriers, large enterprises, and equipment vendors. Certification valid for three years; JNCIE Emeritus status available after 10+ years (lifetime).

## Domain context — Networking/Service Provider

Service provider routing and switching encompasses the design and operation of carrier-grade networks serving multiple customers, with emphasis on inter-AS peering, MPLS virtual private networks (L2/L3), multicast, traffic engineering, and QoS at scale. The JNCIE-SP sits at the peak of the Service Provider path, validating mastery across all major protocols in hyperscale carrier backbone environments. Juniper MX series platforms dominate core infrastructure at global ISPs, telecom carriers, and CDNs.

## Topics covered

**From official exam blueprint — key technical areas:**

- **Device Infrastructure** (System configuration, routing engine architecture, RE redundancy, JUNOS filesystem)
- **IGP: Interior Gateway Protocol** (IS-IS advanced features, multi-instance IS-IS, link-state optimization)
- **MPLS: Multiprotocol Label Switching** (Label distribution protocol, RSVP-TE, segment routing, traffic engineering, fast reroute)
- **BGP: Border Gateway Protocol** (Route reflection, confederations, route policies, multi-AS scenarios, convergence optimization)
- **L3 VPN: Layer 3 Virtual Private Networks** (MPLS/BGP VPN (RFC 2547), inter-AS approaches, route target filtering, VRF routing)
- **L2 VPN: Layer 2 Virtual Private Networks** (VPLS, Martini drafts, Ethernet VPN (EVPN))
- **Multicast** (Multicast VPN, PIM, IGMP, SSM, any-source multicast)
- **Carrier Ethernet** (VLAN-based services, Provider Backbone Bridging, Metro Ethernet)
- **Class of Service (QoS)** (Packet loss priority, forwarding classes, scheduling, policing, hierarchical CoS, buffering)
- **Troubleshooting & Monitoring** (Packet capture, flow analysis, system metrics, logging)

*Source:* [Juniper JNCIE-SP Exam Overview ↗](https://www.juniper.net/us/en/training/certification/tracks/service-provider-routing-switching/jncie-sp.html)

## Skills validated

*Concrete technologies and protocols validated by the JPR-960 lab exam.*

- **Juniper JUNOS OS** (system configuration, RE management, routing engine architecture)
- **IS-IS (Intermediate System to Intermediate System)** — multi-level, multi-instance, link-state optimization
- **MPLS (Multiprotocol Label Switching)** — LSP creation, RSVP-TE, SR-TE, segment routing, MPLS OAM
- **BGP (Border Gateway Protocol)** — route reflection, confederations, multi-AS design, outbound route filtering
- **L3 VPN (RFC 2547 MPLS/BGP VPN)** — VRF configuration, route target, inter-AS VPN approaches
- **L2 VPN (VPLS / Martini)** — virtual Ethernet service, transport mechanisms
- **Multicast** — PIM-SM, IGMP, mVPN, SSM, multicast load-balancing
- **Class of Service (CoS)** — packet loss priority, forwarding classes, hierarchical queuing, policing
- **EVPN (Ethernet VPN)** — next-generation L2/L3 VPN over MPLS/BGP
- **Carrier Ethernet** — PBB, VLAN-based services, MEF compliance
- **Fast Reroute (FRR)** — link and node protection, MPLS-based convergence speedup
- **Troubleshooting tools** — packet capture (tcpdump), flow tracing, route analysis, system monitoring

## Context

**JNCIE-SP at a glance:**

- **Rarity:** ~0.1% of global networking professionals hold JNCIE-SP; holders are sought by carriers, Tier-1 enterprises, and equipment vendors.
- **Hands-on rigor:** 6–8 hour lab with no multiple-choice bypass; candidate must build and troubleshoot realistic multi-AS service provider network.
- **Prerequisite path:** JNCIA-SP (Associate) → JNCIS-SP (Specialist) → JNCIP-SP (Professional) → **JNCIE-SP (Expert)**.
- **Study investment:** 1,500–2,000 hours recommended; bootcamps strongly advised due to hands-on nature.
- **Vendor dominance:** Juniper routers (vMX, MX series, PTX) dominate large carrier networks; JNCIE-SP expertise is directly monetizable in that tier.
- **Lifetime option:** JNCIE Emeritus status available after 10+ years; holders retain credential indefinitely.
- **Salary premium:** JNCIE-SP roles command 15–25% premium over general network architect due to rarity and hands-on validation.
