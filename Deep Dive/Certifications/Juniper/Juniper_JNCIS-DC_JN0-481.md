## About

JNCIS-DC is the specialist-level certification in the Juniper data center track, designed for networking professionals with intermediate knowledge of Juniper Apstra, IP Fabric design, and modern data center architectures (EVPN-VXLAN). Candidates must hold current JNCIA-Junos before attempting this exam. The certification validates understanding of Apstra workflows, overlay/underlay fabric design, QFX series switches, multitenancy, intent-based analytics, and data center interconnect patterns.

---

## Domain context — Networking / Data Center

Data center architecture and operations — the design, deployment, and troubleshooting of IP fabrics, overlay networks, and Apstra-orchestrated infrastructure. JNCIS-DC focuses on Juniper's Junos and Apstra ecosystem in hyperscale and enterprise data center environments.

## Topics covered

The JN0-481 exam blueprint covers the following major domains:

- IP Fabrics and EVPN-VXLAN underlay/overlay architecture
- Juniper Apstra Architecture — components, design, build, deploy, and operations phases
- Apstra Design Phase — device profiles, templates, interface naming, rack/pod/spine topology
- Apstra Build and Deploy Phases — device provisioning, fabric bring-up, and validation
- Blueprint Operations — compliance checks, intent validation, and troubleshooting
- Data Center Multitenancy and segmentation models
- Virtual Network Identifiers (VNI) and Virtual LAN (VLAN) mapping
- EVPN route types (Type 2, Type 5 ERB — Ethernet Route Broker)
- VXLAN encapsulation and tunnel mechanics
- QFX series configuration and operational commands
- Intent-based analytics and telemetry
- Data Center Interconnect (DCI) patterns
- Junos operational and configuration modes

*Source:* [Juniper JNCIS-DC Exam Syllabus ↗](https://www.nwexam.com/juniper/juniper-jn0-481-certification-exam-syllabus)
