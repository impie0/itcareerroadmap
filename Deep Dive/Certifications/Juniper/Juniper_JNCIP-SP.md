## About

JNCIP-SP is the professional-level certification in Juniper's Service Provider Routing and Switching track, designed for networking professionals with advanced knowledge of routing and switching implementations on Junos OS. The exam validates expertise in carrier-class routing protocols, MPLS Layer 2 and Layer 3 VPN scaling, multicast, segment routing, and troubleshooting in large-scale service provider networks. Prerequisite: JNCIS-SP certification is strongly recommended but not strictly enforced.

## Topics covered

- **OSPF** — Multi-area OSPF design, LSA types, metric calculation, convergence behavior in carrier-grade topologies (~15–20% weight)
- **IS-IS** — Level 1/Level 2 adjacencies, route summarization, wide metrics, metric tuning for large-scale routing domains (~15–20% weight)
- **BGP** — BGP route reflectors, confederation design, inter-AS peering, policy, route filtering, community manipulation (~20–25% weight)
- **Class of Service (CoS)** — Traffic classification, congestion management, scheduling, policer configuration, rate limiting
- **IP Multicast** — IGMP, PIM dense mode, PIM sparse mode, SSM (Source Specific Multicast), multicast VPN design
- **Layer 3 VPN (L3VPN)** — VRF configuration, PE/CE interaction, route target filtering, MPLS/BGP signaling
- **Layer 2 VPN (L2VPN)** — LDP, Circuit Cross-Connect (CCC), Kompella signaling, VPWS and VPLS scaling
- **MPLS and traffic engineering** — LDP configuration, RSVP-TE signaling, constraint-based routing, fast reroute (FRR), point-to-multipoint LSP
- **Segment Routing** — SRv6, segment identifier semantics, steering, interoperability with BGP/IS-IS
- **Inter-AS routing** — Option A/B/C design, ASBR behavior, route leaking, scalability considerations
