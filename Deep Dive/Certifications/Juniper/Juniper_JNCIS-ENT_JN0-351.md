## About

JNCIS-ENT is the specialist-level certification in the Juniper enterprise routing and switching track, designed for experienced networking professionals with beginner to intermediate knowledge of Junos routing and switching implementations. Candidates must hold current JNCIA-Junos before attempting this exam. The certification validates understanding of layer 2/3 protocols, BGP, MPLS basics, high availability, IPv6, and enterprise network management on Juniper platforms.

## Topics covered

The JN0-351 exam blueprint covers the following major domains:

- Layer 2 Switching and VLANs (VLAN concepts, configuration, monitoring, troubleshooting)
- Spanning Tree Protocol (STP) — loop prevention and topology management
- Layer 2 Security (MAC filtering, port security, firewall filters)
- BGP (Border Gateway Protocol) — operational and conceptual elements
- Protocol-Independent Routing
- IS-IS (Intermediate System to Intermediate System)
- MPLS Basics (Label-switched paths, traffic engineering concepts)
- IPv6 in enterprise networks
- Tunnels (Generic Routing Encapsulation, IP-in-IP)
- High Availability (VRRP, link aggregation, graceful restart, redundancy)
- Network management and monitoring on Junos

*Source:* [Juniper JNCIS-ENT Exam Syllabus ↗](https://www.nwexam.com/juniper/juniper-jn0-351-certification-exam-syllabus)
