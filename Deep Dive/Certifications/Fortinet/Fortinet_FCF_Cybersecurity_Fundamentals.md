## About

The FCF (Fortinet Certified Fundamentals in Cybersecurity) is Fortinet's free, entry-level certification validating foundational knowledge in cybersecurity principles, the modern threat landscape, and basic security practices. Unlike traditional proctored exams, FCF is earned through completion of two mandatory online courses within the Fortinet Training Institute, with certification valid for two years. Launched in 2024 as the successor to the legacy NSE 1-2 designation, FCF consolidates foundational cybersecurity training into a unified entry point with no prerequisites. 

The certification targets absolute beginners, junior IT staff, and career-changers entering the cybersecurity field. As the gateway to the Fortinet NSE ecosystem, FCF holders can progress to the Fortinet Certified Associate (FCA) and higher-level professional certifications (FCP, FCSS, FCX). The certification is administered entirely through Fortinet's online training platform with no external proctoring or exam fees, making it the most accessible entry point into vendor-specific cybersecurity training. All course materials are freely available through the Fortinet Training Institute, requiring only an account registration.

## Domain context — Cybersecurity Fundamentals

Entry-level cybersecurity foundations covering threat landscape awareness, basic security hygiene, and vendor-neutral security principles applicable to any infrastructure. This domain emphasizes foundational understanding suitable for IT professionals transitioning into security roles and provides the conceptual groundwork for advancing to intermediate and professional-level certifications.

The cybersecurity fundamentals domain is increasingly important as organizations face expanding attack surfaces, cloud migration, remote workforce challenges, and regulatory compliance requirements. Entry-level knowledge ensures that all IT professionals have baseline awareness of security principles regardless of their primary role.

## Topics covered

**Required Course Components:**

1. **Introduction to the Threat Landscape**
   - Modern cyber threats and attack types (malware, ransomware, phishing, DDoS, zero-day exploits)
   - Threat actors, motivations, and attack patterns (criminal groups, state-sponsored actors, hacktivists)
   - Impact of data breaches and security incidents on organizations and individuals
   - Global threat trends and emerging attack vectors in 2025–2026
   - Importance of threat awareness in cybersecurity and defense strategies
   - Cost of cyber incidents and risk quantification for business decision-making
   - Real-world case studies of major breaches and lessons learned

2. **Getting Started in Cybersecurity (NSE 1) OR Technical Introduction to Cybersecurity (NSE 2)**
   - Cybersecurity roles, responsibilities, and career paths in modern enterprises
   - Core security principles: confidentiality, integrity, availability (CIA triad)
   - Additional pillars: authentication, authorization, accounting (AAA)
   - Security best practices and cyber hygiene fundamentals
   - Network security basics: firewalls, intrusion detection, access controls
   - Endpoint security and malware protection concepts
   - Introduction to Fortinet products and the NSE ecosystem (NSE 2 emphasis)
   - Incident response fundamentals and breach notification procedures
   - Compliance and regulatory requirements in cybersecurity (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS basics)
   - Security awareness and user training importance
