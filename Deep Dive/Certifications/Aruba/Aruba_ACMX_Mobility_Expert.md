## About

The Aruba Certified Mobility Expert (ACMX) is the highest-level credential in the Aruba Mobility certification track, designed for senior wireless network architects and experts responsible for designing, implementing, and troubleshooting complex, multi-site, highly available enterprise WLAN deployments using Aruba/HPE solutions (AOS8.6+). The certification consists of two required components: a written exam (HPE6-A79) testing WLAN design, RF optimization, advanced security, and enterprise architecture; and a comprehensive 8-hour practical lab exam (HPE0-A125P) validating hands-on configuration, integration with ClearPass, mobility controller clustering, redundancy, and troubleshooting in real-world scenarios. ACMX is positioned as the Expert tier above the retired Professional level (ACMP), and candidates are expected to have 3–5 years of hands-on Aruba mobility deployment experience. Though no formal prerequisite exists as of Nov 2019, the exam assumes deep familiarity with ACMP-level content.

## Topics covered

Per official exam blueprint for HPE6-A79 (AOS8.6):

- **Analyzing Functional Requirements** (24%) — enterprise network design, scalability, redundancy, HA requirements, multi-site architecture, integration with wired infrastructure
- **Configuring & Validating Aruba WLAN Solutions** (21%) — Mobility Master clustering, high availability (VRRP, failover), controller architecture (MC, VMC, MM), access point modes (Local, Tunneled, MultiZone), WLAN configuration, RF optimization
- **Implementing Advanced Services & Security Solutions** (20%) — 802.1X, RADIUS integration, ClearPass Policy Manager deep integration, user-centric networking, role-based access control (RBAC), guest networks with captive portal, dynamic segmentation, UCC optimization (voice, video), WPA3 deployment
- **Managing & Monitoring Aruba Solutions** (17%) — AirWave / Aruba Central monitoring, AirMatch ARM (Adaptive Radio Management), analytics, dashboards, syslog, SNMP, NetFlow, troubleshooting frameworks
- **Performing Advanced Troubleshooting** (18%) — roaming analysis (fast roaming 802.11r/k, band steering), QoS validation, interference analysis, controller-to-controller communication, client troubleshooting, advanced RF debugging

*Source:* [HPE Certification Learning: HPE6-A79 Blueprint ↗](https://certification-learning.hpe.com/TR/datacard/Exam/HPE6-A79)

## Skills validated

- AOS8.6+ architecture (Mobility Master, Controllers, Access Point models: CAP, UAP, RAP, IAP, VIA)
- Mobility Master clustering and high availability (VRRP, failover, backup/restore)
- Multi-site WLAN architecture (tunneled, local, MultiZone modes)
- Advanced RF management and AirMatch ARM tuning (channel optimization, power control, band steering)
- 802.1X and certificate-based authentication at scale (RADIUS, EAP-TLS, PAC, PEAP)
- ClearPass Policy Manager deep integration (endpoint profiling, guest provisioning, role-based access)
- User-centric networking and dynamic segmentation
- Guest access architecture (captive portal, RADIUS CoA, VLAN assignment)
- Voice over WiFi (VoWiFi) / UCC optimization (codec negotiation, QoS, roaming)
- Mesh networking (802.11s) design and troubleshooting
- WPA3 enterprise deployment
- L2/L3 infrastructure integration (switches, routing, multicast)
- AirWave and Aruba Central monitoring, analytics, dashboards
- Advanced roaming troubleshooting (802.11r/k, fast roaming, client state machine)
- QoS and traffic management in enterprise WLAN
- Network slicing and policy-based forwarding
- Remote Access Point (RAP) and Tunneled Node (TN) design
- Syslog, SNMP, NetFlow, and log aggregation
- API automation (basic to intermediate scripting)
- Disaster recovery and business continuity for WLAN infrastructure
