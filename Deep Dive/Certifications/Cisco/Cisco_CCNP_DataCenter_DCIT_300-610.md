## About

The DCIT (Implementing Cisco Data Center Technologies, 300-610) is a concentration exam under the CCNP Data Center track, building on the mandatory core exam (350-601 DCCOR). It validates hands-on expertise in implementing Nexus switches, UCS compute infrastructure, HyperFlex hyper-converged systems, storage networking, and data center orchestration platforms. Ideal for infrastructure engineers and systems integrators who design and deploy Cisco data center solutions. Requires DCCOR as prerequisite; passing DCCOR + DCIT earns the full CCNP Data Center credential.

---

## Domain context — Networking / Data Center

Enterprise data center networking: on-premises and hybrid infrastructure spanning Ethernet switching, compute virtualization, storage fabrics, and management platforms. The DCIT track emphasizes implementation, configuration, and day-to-day operations — distinguishing it from DCID (design) and DCACI (ACI policy). This is the ideal path for engineers deploying, managing, and troubleshooting Cisco DC stacks.

## Topics covered

**Core implementation domains (from official blueprint):**

- **Nexus Switch Configuration (NX-OS)** — VDC, vPC, port-channels, VLAN, STP, QoS, monitoring
- **UCS Compute Infrastructure** — Service profiles, vNIC/vHBA templates, firmware, licensing, power/thermal management
- **UCS Blade & Rack Servers** — B-series blade chassis, C-series rack mount, unified fabric, FI management
- **HyperFlex Hyper-Converged Infrastructure** — Cisco HX clusters, compute, storage, networking integration basics
- **Storage Networking** — Fibre Channel (FC), Fibre Channel over Ethernet (FCoE), iSCSI, NAS in data center environments
- **Cisco MDS SAN Switches** — VSAN configuration, zoning, fabric aliases, Fibre Channel switching
- **Data Center Network Virtualization** — Overlay Transport Virtualization (OTV), LISP for DC, L2 extension, underlay design
- **Cisco Intersight Management Platform** — Dashboard, device monitoring, telemetry, policy management
- **Automation and Programmability** — Ansible playbooks on NX-OS, Python scripting, REST APIs, JSON/YAML configuration
- **Troubleshooting Nexus and UCS** — Diagnostics, log analysis, interface status, fabric errors, performance monitoring

## Study strategy recommendations

1. **Prerequisites (weeks 1–2):** Must hold CCNA or equivalent; must pass 350-601 DCCOR before attempting DCIT.
2. **Core domains (weeks 3–7):** Study Nexus (VDC, vPC) and UCS (service profiles, FI, blades) in depth. These dominate the exam.
3. **Storage and OTV (weeks 6–8):** FC, FCoE, MDS zoning, OTV scenarios. Less weighted than compute/switching but critical for scenarios.
4. **Hands-on labs (weeks 4–9, parallel):** UCS simulator (Cisco Learning Network), Nexus emulation, Intersight dashboard access. Build and troubleshoot a complete stack if possible.
5. **Automation (weeks 8–10):** Ansible playbooks targeting NX-OS. Python REST API calls to UCS. This is heavily tested in scenarios.
6. **Practice exams (weeks 10–11):** Boson and Pearson under timed conditions. Target 75%+ before registration.

**Typical study timeline:** 150–250 hours (assumes DCCOR completion). DCIT is implementation-heavy; expect 50% labs, 50% theory and practice exams.

## Career trajectories post-CCNP DC (DCIT path)

- **Data Center Engineer** → Senior Data Center Engineer → Data Center Operations Manager
- **Infrastructure Support** (Tier 2/3) → DC Systems Engineer → Senior DC Engineer
- **Systems Integrator / Professional Services** — DCIT is preferred for pre-sales and deployment teams
- **Hybrid Cloud Operations** — on-prem DC + cloud gateway management
- **DevOps Engineer** (infrastructure focus) — use DCIT + DCAUTO for full automation credibility

DCIT is the operational path; engineers who want architecture should pursue DCID instead. Engineers planning CCIE Data Center lab often start with DCIT to deepen hands-on knowledge before the advanced lab.

## Industry context

Cisco Nexus and UCS remain the dominant on-premises data center platforms in Fortune 500 and telecom operator environments. The DCIT concentration appeals most to managed service providers (MSPs), systems integrators, and large enterprises with dedicated DC operations teams. Cloud-native organizations (AWS, Azure, Google) have less demand for Cisco DC certs, but hybrid environments (on-prem + cloud) are increasingly valuing engineers with this credential. The rise of Intersight (Cisco's cloud-based management platform) has elevated the importance of API-driven operations and telemetry — reflecting the shift toward software-defined, intent-based infrastructure.

## Renewal and continuing education

CCNP DC (DCCOR + any concentration, including DCIT) is valid for 3 years. Renewal options:
- **CE credits:** Accumulate 120 CE credits over the 3-year period (Cisco training, conferences, published tech content, teaching, or passing another Cisco professional exam).
- **Retake:** Retake the 350-601 DCCOR exam alone (concentration does not need to be renewed if retaking core).

Most engineers find the CE credit path practical, attending annual Cisco Live or pursuing advanced tracks.
