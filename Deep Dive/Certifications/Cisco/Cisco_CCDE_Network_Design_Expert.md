## About

The Cisco Certified Design Expert (CCDE) is an expert-level network architecture and design certification launched in 2007 that validates mastery of high-level network design and the ability to align technology solutions with business requirements. Unlike the CCIE (Cisco Certified Internetwork Expert), which focuses on configuration, deployment, and troubleshooting of operational networks, the CCDE emphasizes architectural thinking: analyzing business needs, designing scalable and resilient solutions, and bridging the gap between business strategy and technical implementation. The certification confers **lifetime status** upon completion, making it a rare credential in the Cisco portfolio—no renewal required. 

The certification path requires two exams: a written exam (400-007, $400) covering core HLD (high-level design) principles, business-driven architecture, and design validation; followed by an 8-hour practical exam (352-001, $1,900) composed of four independent real-world design scenarios that simulate stakeholder constraints, legacy infrastructure, and trade-off analysis. Passing the written exam alone earns "CCDE Expert Specialist" status; the full "Cisco Certified Design Expert" credential requires both exams. 

Optional concentration codes (Service Provider, Enterprise, Large-Scale Networks, AI Infrastructure) allow candidates to tailor the practical exam to their specialization and career focus. CCDE is particularly valued in enterprise and service-provider environments where network architects direct infrastructure evolution across large, complex topologies, and in organizations transitioning to cloud-hybrid models where design decisions must account for both legacy and modern infrastructure paradigms.

## CCDE vs. CCIE: Key distinctions

**CCDE (Design Expert)** and **CCIE (Internetwork Expert)** are both expert-level Cisco credentials, but they measure fundamentally different competencies:

| Aspect | CCDE | CCIE |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | Network *design* and architecture; aligning business goals to infrastructure | Network *implementation*, configuration, and operational troubleshooting |
| **Audience** | Architects, senior design engineers, enterprise/SP infrastructure strategists | Network engineers, systems integrators, operational teams |
| **Career stage** | Mid-to-senior architects; cross-functional leadership roles | Senior engineers moving toward architecture or specialization tracks |
| **Exam format** | Written (design theory) + Practical (scenario-based design challenges with HLDs) | Written + 8-hour hands-on lab (emulated config/troubleshooting) |
| **Validity** | Lifetime (no renewal) | 3-year validity; requires renewal via CE credits or retake |
| **Salary premium** | +15–25% above CCNP in architect roles | +20–35% above CCNP in engineer roles |
| **Common path** | CCNP → CCDE (design specialization) | CCNP → CCIE (deep technical specialization) |

**Why both exist:** A network architect must understand design principles (CCDE), but many architects also pursue CCIE to validate deep technical chops in a specific domain (Enterprise, Data Center, Service Provider, Security). Some roles value both credentials; others prefer one over the other based on organizational structure and career trajectory.

## Topics covered

**Written Exam (400-007) — Weight by domain:**

- **1.0 Business Strategy Design** (15%) — Aligning network design to business goals, ROI, cost-benefit analysis, risk and compliance, organizational constraints
- **2.0 Control, Data, and Management Plane Design** (25%) — Routing protocols (OSPF, ISIS, EIGRP, BGP), traffic engineering, scalability, convergence, operational models, SNMP/telemetry
- **3.0 Network Design** (30%) — High-availability, redundancy, topology design (core, distribution, access), WAN design, multicast, QoS architecture
- **4.0 Service Design** (15%) — VPN architectures (MPLS, IPSec), SD-WAN, segment routing, cloud interconnection, hybrid infrastructure
- **5.0 Security Design** (15%) — Security policy integration, defense-in-depth, DDoS mitigation, threat modeling, identity/access architecture, encryption strategies

**Practical Exam (Eight-hour format):**  
Four independent 2-hour scenario-based design challenges covering mixed business and technical requirements. Candidates document design decisions, create implementation plans, analyze trade-offs, and validate designs against stated constraints. Each scenario stands alone but may include skills such as HLD design, cost modeling, risk assessment, and technical validation.

*Source:* [Cisco CCDE v3.1 Exam Blueprint ↗](https://learningcontent.cisco.com/documents/marketing/exam-topics/CCDE_v3.1_Written_And_Practical_Exam_12132024.pdf)

## Skills validated

_Concrete technologies, methodologies, and design practices this exam demonstrates proficiency with._

**Architectural & Design Methodology:**
- High-level network design (HLD) documentation and visual representation
- Topology architecture for campus, data center, and WAN environments
- Design requirements elicitation and stakeholder alignment
- Trade-off analysis: cost vs. performance, redundancy vs. complexity, flexibility vs. standardization
- Failure domain isolation and blast radius reduction

**Routing & Control-Plane Design:**
- Advanced routing protocol design and selection: OSPF, ISIS, EIGRP, BGP
- Segment routing (SR) and application-based routing architectures
- Routing protocol convergence and stability tuning
- Scalability analysis for large-scale deployments
- Multi-AS and multi-vendor interoperability design

**Data-Plane & Traffic Management:**
- Traffic engineering and QoS architecture (DiffServ, IntServ, policing, LLQ)
- MPLS-based traffic engineering and fast reroute (FRR)
- Multicast architecture and service delivery design
- Load balancing and equal-cost multipath (ECMP) optimization

**WAN & Cloud Connectivity:**
- MPLS VPN and IPSec VPN architecture for hybrid WANs
- SD-WAN design and deployment patterns (centralized, distributed, hybrid)
- Cloud interconnection strategies (direct, VPN, hybrid)
- Business continuity and disaster recovery (BC/DR) design

**Security Architecture:**
- Defense-in-depth strategy and security zoning design
- DDoS mitigation and traffic scrubbing architecture
- Zero-trust network principles and micro-segmentation
- Identity and access management (IAM) integration in network design
- Encryption and integrity validation placement and protocols

**Data Center & Modern Infrastructure:**
- Data center network design (fabric, overlay, underlay)
- Cloud-native design considerations (container networks, serverless)
- Hyperscaler architecture patterns and lessons (CLOS, leaf-spine)
- Capacity planning and growth modeling

**Business Integration:**
- Cost-benefit analysis and ROI calculation for design decisions
- Operational cost modeling (OPEX vs. CAPEX)
- Risk assessment and mitigation in design
- Compliance and regulatory integration (PCI-DSS, HIPAA, etc.)
- Vendor evaluation and technology selection criteria
