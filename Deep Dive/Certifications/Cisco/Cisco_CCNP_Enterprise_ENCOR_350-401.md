## About

ENCOR (Enterprise Core) is the mandatory first exam for the CCNP Enterprise certification track. Launched in November 2019 as part of Cisco's modernized CCNP redesign, it validates broad expertise across enterprise networking domains: architecture, virtualization, infrastructure, network assurance, security, and automation. To earn CCNP Enterprise, candidates must pass ENCOR (350-401) plus one concentration exam (ENARSI routing, ENSDWI SD-WAN, ENSLD design, ENWLSD/ENWLSI wireless, or ENAUTO automation). ENCOR is designed for mid-to-senior network engineers transitioning into enterprise architecture or specialization roles.

## Topics covered

Official exam blueprint (subject weights):

- **Enterprise Architecture (15%)** — Network design principles, modularity, high availability, disaster recovery, business continuity.
- **Virtualization (10%)** — Virtual switching, overlay networks (VXLAN), virtual routing and forwarding (VRF), container networking fundamentals.
- **Infrastructure (30%)** — Switching (VLANs, Spanning Tree, EtherChannel), routing protocols (OSPF, BGP, EIGRP), MP-BGP, QoS, IPv6.
- **Network Assurance (10%)** — Monitoring, telemetry, NetFlow, Cisco DNA Center basics, troubleshooting methodologies.
- **Security (20%)** — Access control lists (ACLs), firewalling, VPN (IPsec, SSL), identity services (ISE), threat defense.
- **Automation (15%)** — APIs (REST, NETCONF/YANG), Python scripting, infrastructure-as-code, Ansible, Cisco DNA Center automation.
