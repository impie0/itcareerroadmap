## About

The CCNP Data Center (core exam 350-601 DCCOR) validates advanced expertise in designing, deploying, and operating data center infrastructure — including Nexus switches, UCS compute, storage networking, automation, and security. Released in 2020, it replaced the legacy CCNP Data Center track and now forms the foundation for specialized concentration exams (DCID, DCIT, DCACI, DCSAN, DCAUTO). Target audience: senior network engineers, data center architects, and infrastructure specialists with 3+ years of hands-on DC experience.

---

## Domain context — Networking / Data Center

Enterprise data center networking: on-premises and hybrid infrastructure spanning Ethernet switching, storage fabrics, compute virtualization, and orchestration. Cisco dominates this market through Nexus (switching), UCS (blade servers), and MDS (SAN). This certification is foundational for Cisco-centric shops and strongly preferred in large enterprises.

## Topics covered

**Core domains (from official blueprint):**

- **Networking (25%)** — VLAN, STP, LAG, QoS, BGP, OSPF, underlay/overlay design
- **Compute (25%)** — UCS architecture, server profiles, vNIC/vHBA, firmware, licensing
- **Storage Networking (20%)** — Fibre Channel, FCoE, NVMe-oF, MDS switches, zoning
- **Automation (15%)** — Ansible, Python, REST APIs, Puppet, Chef, JSON/YAML
- **Security (15%)** — ACLs, port security, 802.1X, RADIUS/TACACS+, encryption

## Concentration exams detail

After passing 350-601 DCCOR, candidates must pass one of five 2-hour concentration exams to earn the full CCNP DC:

| Exam | Code | Focus | Ideal for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DCID** | 300-610 | Data center network and compute design, capacity planning | Architects, design engineers |
| **DCIT** | 300-615 | Troubleshooting, diagnostics, performance analysis | Support engineers, on-call staff |
| **DCACI** | 300-620 | Application-Centric Infrastructure (ACI) policy, tenants, endpoints | ACI specialists, network automation |
| **DCSAN** | 300-625 | Storage networking, Fibre Channel, MDS, SAN zoning | Storage teams, SAN engineers |
| **DCAUTO** | 300-635 | Automation frameworks, Ansible, Python, REST APIs, data center orchestration | DevOps, infrastructure-as-code teams |

*Concentration exams are not optional; earning the full CCNP DC requires DCCOR + 1 concentration.*

## Study strategy recommendations

1. **Foundation (weeks 1–2):** Review CCNA or equivalent. Data center exams assume solid layer 2/3 knowledge.
2. **Core topics (weeks 3–8):** Work through the Pearson official cert guide chapter-by-chapter. Lab heavily with Cisco Learning Network labs or INE bundled labs.
3. **Hands-on labs (weeks 5–10, parallel):** UCS simulator, Nexus emulation, Ansible playbooks on Linux. Boson reports labs are critical.
4. **Concentration track (weeks 9–12):** Choose one concentration based on role and lab the specific topics.
5. **Practice exams (weeks 11–13):** Take Boson and Pearson practice tests under timed conditions. Aim for 80%+ before registering for the real exam.

**Typical study timeline:** 200–300 hours (core + 1 concentration). Data center is hands-on heavy; expect 40% labs, 60% theory and practice exams.

## Industry context

Cisco maintains ~60–70% market share in enterprise data center switching (Nexus) and blade compute (UCS) in North America and Western Europe. Adoption is strongest in Fortune 500, financial services, and telecom operators. The CCNP DC certification is highly valued in these verticals but less common in cloud-native and startup environments (where AWS / Azure certs dominate). The introduction of ACI (Application-Centric Infrastructure) in 2013 and the DCACI concentration exam have shifted modern DC roles toward intent-based networking — making the DCAUTO and DCACI paths increasingly relevant.

## Renewal and continuing education

CCNP DC is valid for 3 years. Renewal options:
- **CE credits:** Accumulate 120 CE credits over the 3-year period (pursuing advanced Cisco courses, attending conferences, publishing technical content, teaching, or passing another Cisco professional-level exam).
- **Retake:** Simply retake the 350-601 exam (no concentration required for renewal).

Most engineers find the CE credit route more practical than retesting.
