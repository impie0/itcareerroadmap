## About

CLCOR (Collaboration Core) is the mandatory first exam for CCNP Collaboration certification. Launched in September 2020, it validates expertise across voice and unified communications infrastructure, protocols, call control, quality-of-service, and collaboration application integration. To earn CCNP Collaboration, candidates must pass CLCOR (350-801) plus one concentration exam: CLICA (UC Applications, 300-810), CLACCM (Call Manager, 300-815), CLCEI (Cloud & Edge, 300-820), or CLAUTO (Collaboration Automation, 300-835). CLCOR targets collaboration engineers and voice architects managing on-premises or hybrid Cisco telephony, Webex, and unified communications platforms.

## Topics covered

Official exam blueprint (subject weights):

- **Infrastructure & Design (20%)** — Collaboration network architecture, device roles, endpoint types, call flows, dial plans, and deployment models (on-premises, hybrid, cloud).
- **Protocols, Codecs & Endpoints (20%)** — VoIP protocols (SIP, H.323, MGCP, SCCP), audio/video codecs (G.711, G.729, H.264), endpoint registration, and capability exchange.
- **Cisco IOS XE Gateways & Media Resources (15%)** — Voice gateway configuration, transcoding, media termination point (MTP) functions, announcements, and PSTN integration.
- **Call Control & Routing (25%)** — Call processing, calling search spaces (CSS), partition models, route patterns, translation rules, and call flow topology.
- **Quality of Service (10%)** — QoS classification, marking (DSCP, Cos), voice traffic prioritization, congestion handling, and bandwidth management for voice/video.
- **Collaboration Applications (10%)** — Webex integration, unified messaging, presence, and integration patterns with call control systems.
