# Cisco Certified Internetwork Expert (CCIE) Wireless

**`CCIE Wireless`** · ● **Active** · **Expert** · _Cisco_

> **CCIE Wireless** is Cisco's expert-level wireless certification. In 2020, Cisco unified CCIE Wireless with the broader CCIE Enterprise track, consolidating around the **350-401 ENCOR** (Implementing and Operating Cisco Enterprise Network Core Technologies) written exam plus an **8-hour proctored lab exam** ($1,900 USD). This is the gateway to senior wireless architecture and enterprise RF engineering roles.

## About

The **CCIE Wireless** certification validates expert-level knowledge in enterprise wireless network design, deployment, operation, and troubleshooting. Historically a standalone certification (CCIE Wireless v2.1), it was consolidated into **CCIE Enterprise** in 2020, meaning the written exam (350-401 ENCOR) now covers enterprise-wide infrastructure, and the lab exam focuses heavily on wireless: 802.11ac/ax/be standards, Catalyst 9800 controller architecture, WLAN security (ISE integration, 802.1X, MFP), RF fundamentals and RRM (Radio Resource Management), mobility, mesh deployments, and DNA Center automation. The lab is the decisive hurdle—a full-day, 8-hour scenario-driven practical that simulates real production troubleshooting and deployment challenges. Estimated 2,500–3,500 hours of study/hands-on lab time recommended; typical exam-to-pass journey: 12–24 months after CCNP Enterprise.

## Topics covered

*From Cisco's official CCIE Enterprise exam blueprint. Wireless topics dominate the lab exam.*

### Written Exam (ENCOR 350-401)
- **Network Fundamentals** (15%) — OSI, routing/switching, IP services
- **Infrastructure** (25%) — VLANs, STP, port security, NTP, SNMP, logging
- **Automation** (15%) — Python, APIs, Ansible, Cisco DNA Center
- **Network Assurance** (15%) — QoS, NetFlow, monitoring, Cisco Catalyst Center
- **Security** (30%) — ACLs, AAA, encryption, IPSec, IKEv2, Zone-Based Firewall, wireless security

### Lab Exam (Wireless Focus)
- **Catalyst 9800 Controller** configuration and operation (central, local, cloud modes)
- **802.11ac/ax/be** standards, channel planning, power settings, band steering
- **Radio Resource Management (RRM)** — dynamic power, channel assignment, load balancing
- **WLAN Security** — 802.1X/EAP, ISE integration, WPA3, Management Frame Protection (MFP)
- **Mobility & Roaming** — inter-controller handoff, Fast Roaming (FT, OKC), 802.11k/v/w
- **Mesh Deployments** — backhaul configuration, bridge roles, DTN (Dynamic Tunneling)
- **DNA Center Wireless** — provisioning, assurance, policy templates, analytics
- **Bluetooth & IoT** — coexistence, interference mitigation, BLE scanning
- **High-Availability & Redundancy** — HSRP, stateful switchover (SSO), redundant uplinks
- **Troubleshooting** — packet captures, log analysis, RF footprinting, roaming failures

## Skills validated

*Technologies and competencies specifically tested in the CCIE Wireless lab exam.*

- **Catalyst 9800 Controller** — multi-site deployment, local/central/cloud modes, high-availability failover
- **802.11 Standards (ac/ax/be)** — PHY/MAC, channel bandwidth, MCS rates, power management
- **Radio Resource Management (RRM)** — power optimization, channel assignment, TPC (Transmit Power Control), DCA (Dynamic Channel Assignment)
- **WLAN Security** — 802.1X authentication flows, EAP variants, ISE Policy Service Node (PSN) integration, certificate-based and username-based auth, WPA3, MFP (Management Frame Protection), TKIP deprecation
- **Cisco Identity Services Engine (ISE)** — WLAN AAA, RADIUS, TACACS+, guest access, network segmentation via SGT (Security Group Tag)
- **DNA Center (Catalyst Center)** — wireless provisioning, network hierarchy, intent-based networking, assurance dashboards, network telemetry
- **Roaming & Mobility** — fast roaming (FT/OKC), 802.11k (Neighbor Report), 802.11v (BSS Transition Management), 802.11w (Management Frame Protection), inter-controller and cross-vendor roaming
- **Mesh Networking** — RAP (Root Access Point) vs. MAP (Mesh Access Point), backhaul protocols, DTN (Dynamic Tunneling)
- **IoT & Bluetooth Coexistence** — BLE scanning, interference detection, location services, RFID integration
- **Wireless Troubleshooting** — Wireshark analysis, controller debugs, log parsing, RF site surveys, capacity planning
- **Advanced QoS** — per-SSID rate limits, traffic shaping, priority queuing in wireless contexts
- **Redundancy & Failover** — HSRP, SSO (Stateful Switchover), stateful session persistence, N+1 architectures
