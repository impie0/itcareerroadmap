## About

The **MikroTik Certified Wireless Engineer** (MTCWE) is a professional-level wireless certification for engineers deploying and managing 802.11 networks on MikroTik RouterOS and RouterBoard wireless hardware. It builds on the MTCNA and covers advanced wireless access point (AP) configuration, client station modes, bridging, repeaters, mesh networking, wireless hotspot systems, and RF spectrum fundamentals. The cert validates hands-on expertise with NV2 proprietary protocol, Capsman (centralized AP management), and RouterOS wireless feature set. The MTCWE is particularly prominent in WISP (Wireless Internet Service Provider) and ISP deployment environments across Eastern Europe, Africa, and Asia, where MikroTik wireless hardware dominates cost-sensitive infrastructure.

## Topics covered

- **802.11 Standards & RF Fundamentals** — 802.11a/b/g/n/ac/ax protocol basics, frequency bands (2.4 GHz / 5 GHz / 6 GHz), channel width and overlapping, TX power, dBm, antenna gain, RF propagation loss, Fresnel zones
- **Wireless Access Point (AP) Configuration** — SSID setup, encryption modes (WPA2/WPA3), channel selection and auto-switching, TX power tuning, bandwidth limiting, isolation and security
- **Station Mode & Client Connections** — Client bridging to AP, roaming between APs, signal strength thresholds, rate limiting, background scan configuration
- **Bridge Modes & Repeaters** — Transparent bridging between wired and wireless, repeater AP setup for coverage extension, bridge isolation and loop prevention
- **Mesh Networking** — Mesh protocol basics, multi-hop relay configuration, routing over wireless, mesh network topology and stability
- **Wireless Hotspot System** — User-based authentication, bandwidth quotas, profile-based access control, captive portal setup, accounting and traffic isolation
- **NV2 Protocol** — MikroTik proprietary wireless protocol for longer-range point-to-point links, frequency bands, modulation, encryption, range optimization
- **Capsman (Central AP Manager)** — Centralized wireless controller architecture, AP provisioning and monitoring, roaming optimization, traffic aggregation
- **RouterOS Wireless Interfaces** — Interface management, wireless drivers, multi-radio configuration, queue configuration on wireless links, traffic shaping
- **Wireless Troubleshooting & Monitoring** — Signal strength measurement, packet capture on wireless, rate negotiation issues, interference detection, active connection logging

*Source:* [MikroTik Wireless Engineer Exam Guide ↗](https://mikrotik.com/training/wireless-engineer)
