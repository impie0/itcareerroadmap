## About

The Omnissa Certified Engineer (OCE-W) credential recognizes advanced expertise in architecting, designing, deploying, troubleshooting, and optimizing Workspace ONE unified endpoint management (UEM) environments at enterprise scale. Launched in 2024 as part of Omnissa's completely redesigned, post-spin-off certification program, the OCE-W exam builds on the OCA foundation and assesses deep, hands-on knowledge of infrastructure planning, device enrollment orchestration, advanced policy management, identity and access control integration, and system optimization.

The OCE-W is the target credential for solutions architects, senior administrators, and enterprise engineers managing multi-platform endpoint deployments (iOS, Android, Windows, macOS) in complex, mission-critical enterprise environments. The exam reflects modern digital workspace realities including cloud-first architectures, zero-trust security models, API-driven automation workflows, and hybrid work infrastructure design patterns.

Unlike earlier VMware VCP-EUC exams, the OCE is built on completely redesigned exam blueprints reflecting 2024–2025 product capabilities, modern threat models, and real-world customer deployment patterns (not legacy VDI-centric architectures). Omnissa removed mandatory training prerequisites as of September 2025, allowing experience-based candidates to test without course attendance.

Enterprise-scale unified endpoint management, virtual desktop infrastructure (VDI), and secure digital workspace platforms serving remote work, hybrid compute, bring-your-own-device (BYOD), and work-from-anywhere use cases. EUC encompasses the full endpoint lifecycle from procurement, provisioning, and configuration through security enforcement, compliance auditing, and retirement. Workspace ONE is the industry leader in unified endpoint management, managing millions of devices globally across Fortune 500 companies, healthcare systems, financial institutions, and government agencies worldwide.

## Topics covered

Based on the official OCE-W exam blueprint, exam domains and their approximate weights include:

**Infrastructure Planning & Deployment (20–25%)**

Design and architect Workspace ONE at enterprise scale. Topics include on-premises, cloud, and hybrid deployment models; capacity planning and resource estimation; high-availability design and load-balancing patterns; disaster recovery (DR) strategies, backup/restore procedures, RTO/RPO definitions; integration patterns with identity providers, directory services, and enterprise systems; VMware Horizon integration for VDI-centric scenarios; geo-distributed deployments; federation strategies for multi-tenant/multi-region scenarios; and network architecture considerations.

**Unified Endpoint Management (20–25%)**

Master device management across all platforms. Topics include device enrollment workflows (Android via Google Play Enterprise and managed profiles; iOS via Apple Business Manager and DEP; Windows via Intune and hybrid join; macOS via DEP); device lifecycle management (provisioning, management, updates, retirement); compliance policies and audit reporting; zero-trust security implementation and implementation patterns; remote management and support capabilities; enterprise vs. consumer device models; platform-specific management nuances; and integration with third-party MDM/EMM solutions.

**Device & Security Policy Management (20–25%)**

Design and enforce comprehensive security policies. Topics include security policy design and enforcement (device encryption, PIN policies, jailbreak/root detection); conditional access and risk-based authentication; data protection strategies (on-device encryption, in-transit protection, at-rest encryption); application whitelisting and AppDefense integration; compliance enforcement frameworks (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, FedRAMP, SOC 2); network segmentation and micro-segmentation via tunnel integration; DLP (data loss prevention) policies; and containerization strategies.

**Identity & Access Control (15–20%)**

Architect identity management and access control. Topics include single sign-on (SSO) architecture and integration patterns; SAML 2.0, OAuth 2.0, and OpenID Connect protocols; LDAP and Active Directory synchronization and federation; certificate-based authentication and public key infrastructure (PKI) integration; multi-factor authentication (MFA) and adaptive authentication; identity federation and external directory synchronization; access control enforcement and role-based access control (RBAC); and password policy enforcement.

**Workspace ONE Intelligence & Analytics (10–15%)**

Operate, monitor, and optimize Workspace ONE. Topics include monitoring, observability, and health dashboards; troubleshooting workflows and log analysis; performance optimization and capacity trending; usage analytics and device inventory reporting; compliance and security reporting; intelligent alerting and automated remediation; custom dashboard and report creation; performance metrics and KPIs; and analytics-driven optimization.

**Integration & Automation (10–15%)**

Automate and integrate Workspace ONE with enterprise systems. Topics include Workspace ONE REST API fundamentals and advanced usage patterns; workflow automation and orchestration via Hub Services; third-party integrations (Slack, Teams, ServiceNow, custom applications); webhook configuration and event-driven automation; Intelligent Hub portal customization and branding; PowerShell and command-line (CLI) scripting; Workspace ONE SDK integration for custom applications; and API authentication/authorization patterns.

## Omnissa certification path (redesigned 2024)

Omnissa launched an entirely redesigned, career-track-based certification program in 2024 following incorporation:

1. **Omnissa Certified Administrator (OCA)** — Entry-to-intermediate. Core administration and operations skills. Two tracks: OCA-W (Workspace ONE) and OCA-H (Horizon). **Entry:** None (no mandatory training as of Sept 2025). **Valid:** 3 years.

2. **Omnissa Certified Engineer (OCE)** — Professional/intermediate. Advanced design and troubleshooting at enterprise scale. Two tracks: OCE-W and OCE-H. **Entry:** OCA in same track or current/unexpired OCP/VCP. **Valid:** 3 years.

3. **Omnissa Certified Design Engineer (OCDE)** — Advanced/senior. Architecture and strategic design mastery. Launching mid-to-late 2025. **Entry:** OCE in same track. **Valid:** 3 years.

4. **Omnissa Certified Platform Engineer (OCPE)** — Expert. Mastery across entire Omnissa platform. **Entry:** OCDE in both Workspace ONE and Horizon. **Valid:** 3 years.

All credentials have a 3-year validity period from issue date. Renewal via exam retake or approved continuing education activities through the Omnissa learning platform.

## Prerequisites and eligibility

Candidates must hold one of the following to be eligible for the OCE-W exam:

- **OCA-W (Omnissa Certified Administrator – Workspace ONE)** — Current credential
- **OCA-H (Omnissa Certified Administrator – Horizon)** — Current credential
- **OCP (Omnissa Certified Professional)** — Current/unexpired VMware OCP credential (legacy)
- **VCP (VMware Certified Professional)** — Current/unexpired VMware VCP credential (legacy)

OCP and VCP credentials remain valid prerequisites through their expiration dates, even after being retired from new exam registration. As of September 2025, no training courses are mandatory for OCE eligibility; candidates may sit based on experience and self-study alone.

## Exam strategy and preparation

**Time management:** The 120-minute exam allows approximately 1.5 minutes per question. Allocate time efficiently: skim all questions first, answer confident questions quickly, then return to difficult ones. Flag ambiguous questions for review in the remaining time.

**Content focus:** Infrastructure planning and UEM topics dominate (40–50% combined exam weight). Ensure mastery of enrollment workflows across all platforms, HA/DR design principles, and policy enforcement. Identity integration and APIs are secondary but important (25–30% combined).

**Hands-on preparation is critical:** Theory alone is insufficient for professional-level exams. Create a home lab or use Omnissa TechZone labs to practice device enrollment, policy creation, API calls, and advanced troubleshooting scenarios. Real-world problem-solving scenarios are heavily tested.

**Weak domain identification:** Use practice exams to identify weak domains early. If API/integration topics score low, dedicate 1–2 weeks to hands-on REST API work and PowerShell scripting before the exam.

**Study timeline:** Plan 4–8 weeks of study for candidates with 2+ years of hands-on Workspace ONE experience; 8–12 weeks for those newer to the platform. Allocate 1–2 weeks focused on weak domains immediately before exam day.

## Exam difficulty and pass rate

The OCE-W is considered a moderately difficult professional-level certification. Candidates with 2+ years of hands-on Workspace ONE experience and strong identity/API knowledge typically report 60–75% first-attempt pass rates. Success rates increase significantly among those who completed official Omnissa training or have deep automation and API experience. Failure is most common in infrastructure planning (HA/DR design) and identity integration (SSO/LDAP) domains.

## Recent changes and updates

**September 2025 milestone:** Omnissa removed mandatory training prerequisites for OCA and OCE exams, allowing experience-based candidates to test without course attendance. This aligns with industry trends toward competency-based certification (similar to AWS and Microsoft) rather than training-gate models.

**Post-spin-off changes:** The OCE program represents a complete redesign from legacy VMware VCP-EUC. The exam blueprint was built ground-up in 2024 to reflect current product capabilities (Workspace ONE 24.x+), modern threat models, zero-trust architecture, and cloud-first deployment patterns. Questions do not test deprecated features or legacy VMware terminology.

**Credential reciprocity:** Omnissa honors all current and unexpired OCP and VCP credentials as prerequisites for OCE exams. However, OCP/VCP credentials do not stack directly toward OCDE or OCPE; candidates must earn OCE in the relevant track first.

## About the vendor — Omnissa

Omnissa is an independent software company formed in May 2024 when private equity firm KKR completed its acquisition of the VMware End-User Computing (EUC) division from Broadcom. The spin-off occurred because Broadcom, which acquired VMware in November 2023, determined that the EUC business was "non-core" to its semiconductor and infrastructure software strategy.

Omnissa retained complete ownership of all VMware EUC intellectual property, including flagship products Workspace ONE (unified endpoint management) and Horizon (virtual desktop infrastructure). The company maintains technical and contractual backward compatibility with VMware certifications (OCP and VCP credentials remain valid as OCE prerequisites), honors existing customer support contracts, and continues active development of both on-premises and cloud-delivered solutions. Omnissa serves Fortune 500 enterprises, government agencies, healthcare systems, and financial institutions globally, managing millions of devices.

Omnissa is headquartered in Wilmington, Delaware; operates globally with offices in Europe, Asia-Pacific, and Americas; maintains a large partner ecosystem of resellers, systems integrators, consultants, and training providers. The company is backed by KKR's capital and strategic support and has retained the majority of the original VMware EUC engineering, product, and support teams, ensuring continuity and innovation.

## Exam registration and logistics

**Registration window:** Candidates can register for the OCE-W exam via Pearson VUE's website up to 24 hours before their desired exam date. Pearson VUE offers flexible scheduling with thousands of testing centers worldwide plus OnVUE remote proctored testing from home/office.

**Testing center vs. remote:** Most candidates choose OnVUE remote testing for convenience, though some prefer in-person testing centers for reduced technical/environmental distractions. Both delivery methods use identical question pools and passing scores.

**Exam day requirements:** Bring valid government-issued ID, arrive 15 minutes early, comply with Pearson VUE's testing environment policies (quiet space, no phones, no notes), and disable screen recording software. For OnVUE remote, a webcam, microphone, and high-speed internet (minimum 10 Mbps) are required.

**Results:** Pass/fail results are displayed immediately upon exam completion. Official score reports are emailed within 24 hours and accessible via the Omnissa certification portal.

**Credential issuance:** Upon passing, digital credentials are issued via Credly and appear in the Omnissa certification registry within 5–7 business days. Printed certificates can be requested at additional cost.
