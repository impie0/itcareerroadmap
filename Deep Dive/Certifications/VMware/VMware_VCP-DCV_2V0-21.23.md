# VMware Certified Professional — Data Center Virtualization

**`2V0-21.23`** · ● **Retired (Dec 15, 2025)** · **Professional** · _VMware (Broadcom)_

> **STATUS:** VCP-DCV (exam code 2V0-21.23) was the current professional-level certification for VMware vSphere system administrators and virtualization engineers. It was **officially retired December 15, 2025** as part of Broadcom's post-acquisition restructuring. For candidates transitioning, Broadcom recommends **VCP-VVF Administrator** (vSphere Foundation) or **VCP-VCF Administrator** (Cloud Foundation) as successor pathways.

## About

VCP-DCV (2V0-21.23) was the flagship professional-level credential for VMware vSphere system administrators and virtualization engineers managing on-premises enterprise data center infrastructure. The certification validated hands-on proficiency in vSphere 8.0 architecture, hypervisor deployment (ESXi), vCenter management, storage design, networking, virtual machine lifecycle, clustering, performance optimization, security hardening, and production troubleshooting. First released in 2021 for vSphere 7.0, the exam was refreshed in 2023 for vSphere 8.0. After Broadcom acquired VMware in November 2023, the company restructured its certification portfolio to emphasize hybrid-cloud and cloud-native platforms (VMware Cloud Foundation, vSphere Foundation). VCP-DCV was officially retired on December 15, 2025, with no new exam registrations accepted after that date. Existing VCP-DCV credentials expire on a rolling 3-year schedule, requiring renewal by passing an alternative Broadcom certification (VCP-VVF, VCP-VCF) rather than retaking VCP-DCV.

## Topics covered

Based on the official Broadcom vSphere 8.0 exam blueprint, 2V0-21.23 assessed knowledge across eight major domains:

- **vSphere Architecture and Components** (15–20%) — ESXi, vCenter, VCSA, licensing models, architecture design patterns, upgrades
- **Storage** (10–15%) — shared storage types, vSAN architecture, storage protocols (iSCSI, NFC, Fibre Channel), VMFS, vVols, storage policies
- **Networking** (15–20%) — vSwitches, distributed switches (DVS), port groups, VLAN tagging, vMotion networks, NSX integration points
- **Virtual Machine Management** (15–20%) — VM creation, configuration, resource allocation, snapshots, cloning, templates, vApp deployment
- **Cluster and High Availability** (10–15%) — DRS (distributed resource scheduling), HA (high availability), vSAN stretched clusters, admission control
- **Performance Tuning and Optimization** (10–15%) — CPU/memory scheduling, resource limits/shares/reservations, capacity planning, latency analysis
- **Security** (5–10%) — ESXi hardening, vSphere Authentication Proxy, encrypted vMotion, VM encryption, RBAC, audit logging
- **Troubleshooting and Support** (10–15%) — PSOD (Purple Screen of Death) analysis, log file interpretation, partition corruption, storage access loss, network isolation

*Source:* [Broadcom VCP-DCV vSphere 8.0 Exam Guide ↗](https://docs.broadcom.com/doc/vcp-dcv-for-vsphere-80-exam-guide-2v0-21x7)

## Why VCP-DCV was retired: The Broadcom transition

**November 2023:** Broadcom completed acquisition of VMware for $61 billion.

**May 2024:** Broadcom announced three major changes:
1. Eliminated mandatory training course prerequisites for all VMware certifications (VCP, VCAP, Specialist)
2. Flattened exam pricing to flat $250 USD for all Broadcom certifications
3. Announced pivot toward hybrid-cloud and cloud-native certification portfolio

**March 2025:** Broadcom mandated that all VMware certifications adopt 3-year expiration with **exam-based renewal only** (eliminated CE credit renewal pathway). This effectively forced certificate holders to retake exams rather than simply accumulating continuing education credits.

**July–November 2025:** Broadcom phased out VCP-DCV exam registrations. New registrations halted. Official retirement announced.

**December 15, 2025:** VCP-DCV exam ceased to be offered. Existing VCP-DCV credentials remain valid on holders' resumes and LinkedIn but expire on rolling 3-year schedule (credentials granted March 2025 expire March 2028, etc.). Renewal requires passing **successor exam** (VCP-VVF or VCP-VCF), not retaking VCP-DCV.

### Strategic rationale

- **Cloud-first positioning:** Broadcom pivoting VMware go-to-market toward multi-cloud and cloud-native buyers. On-premises vSphere-only customers declining market segment.
- **Certification unification:** New VCP-VVF track (vSphere Foundation) and VCP-VCF track (Cloud Foundation) simplify learning progression around modern VMware architectures.
- **Rebranding initiative:** "VMware vSphere Foundation" is new name for vSphere 9.0 Enterprise+ tier. Broadcom consolidating messaging under Broadcom brand, reducing standalone "VMware" identity.
- **Customer pressure:** 74% of IT leaders evaluating non-VMware platforms post-acquisition (Gartner 2025); Broadcom responding with modernized credential portfolio to retain mindshare among remaining customers.

## Migration guidance for VCP-DCV holders (2026)

**If your VCP-DCV credential is expiring or recently expired:**

### Path 1: On-Premises vSphere Focus
→ Pursue **VCP-VVF Administrator (2V0-16.25)** — covers vSphere 9.0 via new "vSphere Foundation" brand, single-cluster focus, targets same job market as classic VCP-DCV. Most direct successor for traditional sysadmin roles.

### Path 2: Hybrid / Multi-Cloud Focus
→ Pursue **VCP-VCF Administrator (2V0-11.25)** or **VCP-VCF Architect (2V0-13.25)** — covers VMware Cloud Foundation, multi-cluster orchestration, software-defined cloud. Better path if organization moving toward cloud-managed infrastructure.

### Path 3: Advanced / Design Specialization
→ After VCP-VVF/VCP-VCF, advance to **VCAP-VVF Deploy (2V0-17.25)** or **VCAP-VCF Design (2V0-14.25)** for advanced certification. Note: VCDX (expert) now requires VCAP as prerequisite; VCIX title was retired.

### Path 4: Diversification (Market-Aware)
→ Given customer exodus to Nutanix (30,000+ migrated FY2025), consider **Nutanix Certified Associate (NCCA)** or **Nutanix Certified Associate – Cloud Services (NCCA-CS)** as parallel credential. Dual certification (VCP + Nutanix) increasingly valued by employers hiring infrastructure teams.

### Path 5: Open-Source Alternative
→ For cost-conscious or EU-centric organizations, **Proxmox VE** certifications or **Linux Foundation Certified Systems Administrator (LFCS)** offered escape from Broadcom licensing dependency.

**Timeline:** Training providers (CBT Nuggets, Pluralsight, Linux Academy, A Cloud Guru) completed migration to VCP-VVF/VCP-VCF materials by March 2026. If you find VCP-DCV courses published after January 2026, verify they're archived legacy materials; live training focuses on successor certs.

- **Nutanix:** ~30,000 customers migrated by fiscal 2025 (Nutanix CEO Rajiv Ramaswami, April 2025 earnings call)

- **Workload migration forecast:** 35% of VMware workloads projected to migrate off platform by 2028 (Gartner)
- **Partner ecosystem collapse:** Authorized VMware resellers/VCSPs reduced from 4,500 globally to 312 (April 2025) — 98% reduction
- **Licensing backlash:** Customers reported 150%–1050% price increases year-over-year; industry average 8X–15X for equivalent coverage
- **EU antitrust inquiry:** European Commission opened formal investigation into Broadcom's licensing amendments (2024–2025)

**Career implication:** VCP-DCV job postings declined ~35–40% year-over-year 2024–2025. Remaining roles concentrated at large enterprises (>500 VMs) with multi-year VMware lock-in (financial services, healthcare, automotive). New VMware hires increasingly specify "VCP preferred, Nutanix/Proxmox/Hyper-V experience valued" — suggesting diversified multi-platform expectation.
