## About

Datadog Fundamentals is the entry-level certification validating foundational knowledge of the Datadog observability and monitoring platform. Released in 2022 as part of Datadog's formal certification program, it tests core skills in Datadog architecture, Agent installation/configuration, infrastructure deployment, data collection, metrics, logs, events, dashboards, monitors, and troubleshooting. Aimed at observability professionals, DevOps engineers, SREs, and IT operations personnel adopting Datadog in production environments. No prerequisites required, though 6+ months hands-on Datadog platform experience is strongly recommended.

## Domain context — Observability/Monitoring

Observability encompasses the three pillars of monitoring: metrics, logs, and distributed traces. The Observability/Monitoring domain includes vendor-specific platforms (Datadog, Prometheus, ELK, Splunk, Dynatrace) and vendor-neutral observability practices. Datadog is the market-leading SaaS platform for observability at scale.

## Topics covered

The exam blueprint covers six major knowledge domains:

- Computer fundamentals (CPUs, RAM, storage, networking basics) (~10-15%)
- Datadog Agent installation, configuration, and troubleshooting (~20-25%)
- Infrastructure monitoring: hosts, containers, tags, service discovery (~15-20%)
- Metrics and custom metrics; DogStatsD (~10-15%)
- Logs, events, and log parsing fundamentals (~10-15%)
- Dashboards, monitors, alerts, SLOs, Synthetics, RUM, and Notebooks (~20-25%)

*Source:* [Datadog Fundamentals Exam Guide ↗](https://www.datadoghq.com/pdf/Datadog_Fundamentals_Exam_Guide.pdf)
