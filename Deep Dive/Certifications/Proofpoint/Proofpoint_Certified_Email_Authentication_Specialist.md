## About

The Proofpoint Certified Email Authentication Specialist (PCEAS) is a professional-level certification that validates deep knowledge of email authentication standards (SPF, DKIM, DMARC, BIMI) and their role in modern email security. The certification covers how email authentication improves deliverability, prevents brand hijacking, protects against phishing and business email compromise, and integrates with Proofpoint's Email Fraud Defense (EFD) and broader email security platform. The program is part of Proofpoint's free Cybersecurity Leadership Specialist initiative and consists of three online sessions (covering email authentication basics, DMARC implementation, and BIMI integration) followed by three 100% passing score exams. Candidates are issued digital badges via Credly upon completion. The program is available to all—no partner or customer restrictions.

## Domain context — Email Security

Email remains the primary attack vector for business email compromise, ransomware, credential theft, and data exfiltration. Email authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) protects against sender impersonation and brand spoofing. Modern email security combines authentication enforcement, threat detection (phishing, malware, zero-day), DLP, encryption, and incident response. Cloud-native solutions like Proofpoint provide centralized protection without on-premises hardware.

## Topics covered

The Certified Email Authentication Specialist exam assesses knowledge across these domains:

- Email authentication fundamentals (SPF, DKIM, DMARC overview)
- Sender Policy Framework (SPF) configuration, syntax, and deployment
- DomainKeys Identified Mail (DKIM) setup, validation, and troubleshooting
- DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance) policy creation, enforcement, and monitoring
- DMARC analyzer configuration and reporting
- Email authentication attack prevention (spoofing, impersonation, phishing)
- Brand Indicators for Message Identification (BIMI) integration and benefits
- Business email compromise (BEC) detection and mitigation
- Integration with Proofpoint Email Fraud Defense (EFD)
- Email deliverability and authentication alignment
- DMARC monitoring tools and reporting dashboards
- Common authentication failures and remediation
- Compliance and industry best practices (GDPR, HIPAA, SOX implications)
- Proofpoint platform-specific EFD configuration
