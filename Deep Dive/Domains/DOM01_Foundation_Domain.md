# Deep Dive: Foundation / IT Support / Helpdesk Domain

**Revision:** A  
**Date:** April 30, 2026  
**Scope:** Entry-level IT certifications, support roles, career pathways, compensation, and learning resources (US, UK, South Africa)

---

## 1. Overview

The Foundation domain is the **primary entry point into IT careers**, encompassing helpdesk tiers (T1/T2), desktop support, field service, and IT support specialists. It covers hands-on troubleshooting of hardware, software, operating systems, mobile devices, networking basics, and customer service skills—the skills required to "turn it off and turn it back on" professionally.

This domain overlaps significantly with:
- **IT Management/Governance** (ITIL 4 Foundation, organizational service management)
- **Networking** (basic connectivity, SOHO troubleshooting, Cisco CCST)
- **Identity/Endpoint** (user provisioning, password resets, device enrollment)
- **Security** (password hygiene, phishing awareness, malware identification)

**Career ceiling (without further reskilling):** IT Manager, Service Delivery Manager. Horizontal pivots into Sysadmin, Network Engineer, or SOC Analyst are the most common exits.

## 2. Certifications in the Foundation Domain

### CompTIA A+ (220-1201 & 220-1202)

**Status:** ACTIVE  
**Official URL:** [https://www.comptia.org/en-us/certifications/a/core-1-and-2-v15/](https://www.comptia.org/en-us/certifications/a/core-1-and-2-v15/)  
**Exam Codes:** 220-1201 (Core 1), 220-1202 (Core 2)  
**Exam Objectives (Core 1):** [https://www.examcompass.com/comptia-certifications/a-plus/core-1/comptia-a-plus-220-1201-exam-objectives.pdf](https://www.examcompass.com/comptia-certifications/a-plus/core-1/comptia-a-plus-220-1201-exam-objectives.pdf)  
**Exam Objectives (Core 2):** [https://www.examcompass.com/comptia-certifications/a-plus/core-2/comptia-a-plus-220-1202-exam-objectives.pdf](https://www.examcompass.com/comptia-certifications/a-plus/core-2/comptia-a-plus-220-1202-exam-objectives.pdf)  
**Exam Fee:** $253 per exam (total $506 for both; two exams required)  
**Launch Date:** March 25, 2025  
**Estimated Retirement:** 2028  
**Study Time:** 6–12 weeks recommended  
**Passing Scores:** Core 1: 675/900; Core 2: 700/900  
**Prerequisites:** 12 months hands-on IT support experience recommended  
**Exam Duration:** 90 minutes each exam  
**Question Types:** Multiple-choice, drag-and-drop, performance-based (PBQs)

**Scope:**
- **Core 1 (220-1201):** Mobile device hardware (keyboards, cameras, RAM, storage, wireless cards, biometric), accessories (USB variants, NFC, Bluetooth, tethering), storage devices, power supplies, printers, virtualization, cloud computing, hardware troubleshooting.
- **Core 2 (220-1202):** OS types (Windows, macOS, Linux), OS installation/upgrades, networking configuration, security settings (Windows Defender, wireless protocols), malware types, workstation hardening, mobile device security, data destruction, browser security.

**Validity:** Lifetime (no renewal required)  
**Industry Recognition:** Tier-1 gold standard for helpdesk; required or preferred by most MSPs, enterprise support, and government contractors.

## 4. Day-in-the-Life: Four Sub-Roles

### Tier 1 Help Desk (L1 Support Specialist)

**Responsibilities:**
- **First contact triage:** Categorize incoming support requests (phone, email, chat, ticketing system)
- **Basic troubleshooting:** Password resets, account unlocks, printer paper jams, "Did you reboot?" diagnostives
- **Documentation:** Detailed ticket notes; escalation flags for L2/L3
- **Soft skills:** Empathy, patience, clear explanation to non-technical users
- **Typical call volume:** 3–5 concurrent chats, 1–2 phone calls simultaneously
- **Resolution rate:** 40–60% of tickets closed at L1 (password resets, simple reboots, account access)
- **Escalation:** Remaining 40–60% escalated to L2 Desktop Support or L3 Sysadmin

**Daily Schedule (8-hour shift):**
- 0800–0815: Arrive, open ticketing system, check queue, review overnight escalations
- 0815–1200: Continuous intake of requests; concurrent ticket handling
- 1200–1300: Lunch (rotating)
- 1300–1700: Continued intake; follow-up on morning escalations
- 1700–1730: Document open cases, transfer to next shift, close station
- **Down time:** Minimal; average handle time (AHT) ~10 min per ticket

**Tools/Systems Used:** Ticketing (ServiceNow, Jira, Zendesk), remote access (TeamViewer, RDP), password manager, antivirus console, knowledge base  
**Typical Issues:** Password resets (30%), software installation (15%), access requests (20%), hardware swaps (10%), escalations (25%)  
**Career entry point:** High school diploma + CompTIA A+ (or equivalent on-the-job training)  
**Salary (US):** $38,000–$55,000/year

**Source:** [CompTIA: A Day in the Life of an IT Pro: Tier 1 Help Desk Specialist](https://www.comptia.org/en-us/blog/a-day-in-the-life-of-an-it-pro-tier-1-help-desk-specialist/)

---

### Tier 2 Desktop Support / Desktop Technician

**Responsibilities:**
- **Complex troubleshooting:** Hardware diagnosis, OS-level issues, driver problems, malware removal
- **Physical onsite work:** Device swaps, hardware replacements, cable management, BIOS updates
- **Software deployment:** Imaging, OS installation, application rollouts via GPO or WSUS
- **Hardware inventory:** Asset tracking, device lifecycle management, e-waste handling
- **Knowledge base:** Document solutions, contribute to runbooks
- **Mentoring:** Guide L1 techs on escalated issues
- **Typical ticket complexity:** Medium to high; 30–40% of escalations from L1
- **Resolution time:** 1–4 hours per ticket (not same-day always)

**Daily Schedule (8-hour shift):**
- 0800–0830: Review overnight escalations, prioritize by SLA
- 0830–1200: Mix of desk-side support, lab troubleshooting, hardware repair
- 1200–1300: Lunch
- 1300–1700: Continued onsite/remote support; documentation; imaging/deployment work
- 1700–1730: Update inventory, close tickets, prioritize next-day tasks
- **Travel:** 20–40% of time onsite at user desks; 60–80% in IT shop or remote

**Tools/Systems Used:** Imaging software (MDT, Acronis), remote access, hardware diagnostics (CPU-Z, GPU-Z, MemTest), malware tools (Malwarebytes, HitmanPro), network monitoring  
**Typical Issues:** Hardware RMA/replacement (25%), OS corruption (20%), malware/virus (15%), driver/firmware updates (15%), software licensing (10%), escalations to L3 (15%)  
**Career entry point:** CompTIA A+, 1–2 years L1 experience
**Salary (US):** $45,000–$65,000/year

**Source:** [Robert Half: A Day in the Life of a Help Desk Analyst](https://www.roberthalf.com/us/en/insights/career-development/a-day-in-the-life-of-a-help-desk-analyst)

### IT Support Specialist (MSP / Enterprise Support)

**Responsibilities:**
- **Generalist remote/hybrid:** Combines L1 & L2 duties; accountable for end-to-end resolution
- **Proactive monitoring:** Watch for alerts, patch management, backup verification
- **Customer relationship:** SLA management, follow-up communication, satisfaction tracking
- **Technical depth:** Can troubleshoot Windows, macOS, Linux; mobile device provisioning; basic networking
- **Documentation:** RCA (Root Cause Analysis) on critical incidents
- **Change management:** Request review, testing, deployment coordination
- **Scope:** Broader than traditional helpdesk; less specialized than Sysadmin

**Daily Schedule:**
- 0800–0830: Triage alerts, run morning health checks, check escalations
- 0830–1200: Support ticket intake; mix of remote and onsite visits
- 1200–1300: Lunch
- 1300–1700: Continued support; proactive maintenance; documentation
- 1700–1730: Close tickets, document trends, handoff to next shift or escalation
- **Flexibility:** Often on-call rotation (evenings, weekends)

**Tools/Systems Used:** RMM (Remote Monitoring & Management: Connectwise, Datto, Kaseya), PSA (Professional Services Automation), imaging, patch management, hypervisor consoles (for monitoring)  
**Typical Issues:** All of L1 & L2 combined; also includes backups, hypervisor issues, SAN monitoring, network device resets  
**Career entry point:** CompTIA A+ + 1–2 years helpdesk OR Google IT Support Certificate
**Salary (US):** $50,000–$70,000/year

---

### Field Service Technician / On-Site Support

**Responsibilities:**
- **Onsite presence:** Visit customer locations for hardware setup, upgrade, repair, decommission
- **Hardware proficiency:** Cable management, device imaging, OS installation, BIOS/UEFI config
- **Logistics:** Manage parts inventory, vehicle maintenance, travel scheduling
- **Customer facing:** Professional appearance, communication, onsite problem-solving
- **Documentation:** Photo evidence, work orders, inventory updates
- **Independence:** Less direct supervision than helpdesk; own schedule planning
- **Scope:** Hardware-centric; limited software troubleshooting

**Daily Schedule:**
- 0800–0830: Check work orders, load vehicle with parts, route planning
- 0830–1700: Multiple onsite visits (2–5 per day); 45–60 min travel per visit
- Travel time: 2–3 hours of 8-hour day
- 1700–1730: Close work orders, inventory reconciliation, return to depot
- **Evening/Weekend:** Rare; mostly M–F business hours

**Tools/Systems Used:** Work order system (mobile app), imaging tool, hardware diagnostics, vehicle GPS, inventory tracking  
**Typical Tasks:** Device deployments (30%), hardware upgrades (25%), network drops/cabling (20%), RMA handling (15%), VIP support (10%)  
**Career entry point:** CompTIA A+ + driver's license; no previous IT required
**Salary (US):** $45,000–$65,000/year + vehicle allowance

## 9. Career Progression Pathways from Foundation

### Most Common Exit: Help Desk → Systems Administrator

**Timeline:** 1–3 years (varies by individual learning pace and opportunity)

**Skill Stack Additions:**
- Linux (LPIC-1 or Red Hat basics)
- Server OS (Windows Server, Linux server deployment)
- Infrastructure basics (servers, storage, backup)
- Scripting (PowerShell, Bash)
- Virtualization (VMware or Hyper-V basics)

**Certifications on Path:**
- CompTIA A+ → **CompTIA Network+** (220-1101/1102) or **Cisco CCNA** (200-301)
- Then: **CompTIA Security+** (SY0-701) or **Linux+** (LX0-605)
- Finally: **CompTIA CySA+** (CS0-003) or vendor-specific (e.g., Microsoft, AWS)

**Sample Timeline:**
- Month 0–3: CompTIA A+ (foundation)
- Month 3–9: Helpdesk L1/L2 role + on-the-job learning
- Month 9–18: CompTIA Network+ + early Sysadmin tasks (user provisioning, backup monitoring)
- Month 18–36: Full Sysadmin role; Security+ for advanced positions

**Salary Jump:** $45k (L2) → $65k–$80k (Junior Sysadmin) → $85k–$120k (Senior Sysadmin, 10+ yrs)

**Source:** [CBT Nuggets: Decoding Your IT SysAdmin Career Pathway](https://www.cbtnuggets.com/blog/career/career-progression/decoding-your-it-sysadmin-career-pathway)

---

### Alt Path 1: Help Desk → Network Engineer

**Timeline:** 2–4 years (longer path due to Network+ → CCNA prerequisites)

**Key Certifications:**
- CompTIA A+ → CompTIA Network+ (220-1101/1102) → **Cisco CCNA** (200-301) → CCNP (advanced)

**Skill Focus:** Routing, switching, VLANs, ACLs, WAN protocols, SD-WAN, network automation (Python)

**Salary Jump:** $45k (L1) → $60k (Network Tech) → $80k–$100k (Network Engineer) → $120k+ (Senior/CCNP)

---

### Alt Path 2: Help Desk → SOC Analyst / Security

**Timeline:** 1–2 years (faster than network if company has SOC)

**Key Certifications:**
- CompTIA A+ → **CompTIA Security+** (SY0-701) → **Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)** or **CompTIA CySA+** (CS0-003) → **CISSP** (advanced)

**Skill Focus:** Threat detection, incident response, log analysis, malware, vulnerability scanning

**Salary Jump:** $45k (L1) → $60k–$70k (SOC Analyst T1) → $80k–$100k (SOC Analyst T2/Incident Response) → $120k+ (Senior SOC/SIEM Engineer)

---

### Alt Path 3: Help Desk → Cloud Engineer (with reskilling)

**Timeline:** 2–3 years + significant self-study

**Key Certifications:**
- CompTIA A+ → CompTIA Cloud+ (CV0-003) → **AWS Solutions Architect Associate** (SAA-C03) or **Azure Administrator** (AZ-104) → advanced vendor certs

**Skill Focus:** Cloud infrastructure (AWS/Azure/GCP), containerization (Docker, Kubernetes), IaC (Terraform)

**Salary Jump:** $45k (L1) → $65k–$75k (Junior Cloud Ops) → $100k–$130k (Cloud Engineer) → $150k+ (Cloud Architect)

---

### Alt Path 4: Help Desk → IT Manager (managerial track)

**Timeline:** 3–5 years + soft-skill emphasis

**Key Certifications:**
- CompTIA A+ → **ITIL 4 Foundation** → **CompTIA Project+** (PK0-005) → **ITIL Master** (advanced, optional)

**Skill Focus:** Team leadership, ITIL processes, project management, SLA management, vendor relationships

**Salary Jump:** $45k (L1) → $55k–$65k (L2) → $75k–$90k (Service Desk Manager) → $100k–$130k (IT Manager) → $150k+ (Director)

## 11. 2026 Updates & Certification Status

### Recently Retired / Deprecated

- **Microsoft 365 Fundamentals (MS-900):** Retired March 31, 2026. No direct successor. Consider **Azure Fundamentals (AZ-900)** or skip Microsoft foundational certs.
- **CompTIA A+ 220-1101/1102:** Retired; replaced by **220-1201/1202 v15** (launched March 2025, estimated retirement March 2028).
- **Cisco CCENT:** Retired years ago; replaced by **CCST IT Support (100-140)** as entry point.

### New & Trending (2024–2026)

- **Cisco CCST IT Support (100-140):** New in 2023; growing adoption; tier-1 alternative to CompTIA A+.
- **Google IT Support Professional Certificate:** Matured; employer recognition increasing; Coursera subscription model stable at $49/mo.
- **CompTIA ITF+:** Repositioned as true entry-level; not widely required but excellent pre-A+ step.
- **ITIL 4 Foundation:** Steady demand; increasingly required for service desk manager roles.

### Exam Objective Updates (2025–2026)

- **CompTIA A+ 220-1201/1202:** Version 15 launched March 2025. Major addition: **AI-impact awareness** (recognizing AI in help desk, bias, data privacy in ML). Practice exams from 2024 still broadly valid but may miss AI-specific questions.
- **Apple ACSP:** No major changes announced; focus on macOS 14+ and iOS 17 features.
- **Microsoft:** No new foundation certs; focus shifted to role-based (Administrator, Developer, Architect).

## 13. Conclusion

The Foundation domain is the **proven on-ramp into IT**. Whether you choose CompTIA A+ (industry gold standard), Google IT Support Certificate (affordable, Google-backed), or Cisco CCST (networking-focused), the path is clear:

1. **Get certified** (6–12 weeks)
2. **Land L1 helpdesk role** (entry: $40k–$50k, no degree required)
3. **Build domain expertise** (choose: Sysadmin, Network, Cloud, Security, Management)
4. **Earn vendor/advanced certs** (2–5 years in role)
5. **Transition to specialization** (5+ yrs: $100k–$150k+)

**Key Success Factors:**
- **Hands-on practice** beats memorization (labs, home lab, practice exams)
- **Communication skills** are as important as technical depth for career progression
- **Specialization after Foundation** is non-negotiable (no one stays T2 support forever unless management track)
- **Global salary variance is real:** ZAR entry (R170k–R200k) vs. USD ($40k–$50k) vs. GBP (£22k–£26k); plan accordingly

**Next Steps:**
- Choose a certification path (A+ recommended for broad portability)
- Enroll in free courses (Professor Messer) or paid platform (CBT Nuggets)
- Target a role in an MSP or enterprise with growth opportunities
- Build a home lab (VM, networking simulator)
- Network in r/CompTIA and r/ITCareerQuestions
