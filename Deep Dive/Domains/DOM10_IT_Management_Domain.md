## Overview

IT Management encompasses leadership, governance, and organizational change across seven interrelated domains:

| Domain | Focus | Key Role |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **Project Management** | Single projects, 8 weeks–18 months | Project Manager, CAPM/PMP |
| **Program Management** | Multiple interdependent projects, 12–36 months | Program Manager, PgMP |
| **Portfolio Management** | Strategic alignment of ALL projects + initiatives | Portfolio Manager, PfMP |
| **Agile / Scrum** | Iterative delivery, cross-functional teams | Scrum Master, Product Owner |
| **Scaled Agile** | Enterprise agile across 50–5000 people | SAFe Scrum Master, Agilist |
| **Service Management (ITIL)** | Incident, change, problem, config management | ITSM Manager, ITIL Foundation+ |
| **Governance & Compliance** | Risk frameworks, audit, GRC tools, board-level reporting | Chief Risk Officer, GRC Manager |

This domain is **high-leverage for career progression**: IC → Team Lead → Manager → Director → CIO is the C-suite ladder for IT leaders. Unlike specialist roles (e.g., Network Engineer → Network Architect), management roles span all 12 IT domains and drive organizational strategy.

## Project Management (PMI)

### Overview

The Project Management Institute (PMI) is the world's largest project management standards body, with 3+ million credential holders globally. PMI defines a project as **a temporary endeavor with a defined start, end, and measurable deliverables**—distinguishing it from ongoing operations.

**URL:** https://www.pmi.org/certifications

### Certification Pathway

#### **1. CAPM (Certified Associate in Project Management)**

- **Entry point:** No prior experience required.
- **Exam:** 150 questions, 3 hours, computer-based.
- **Cost:** ~$250–$300 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewable via 36 PDU hours or exam retake.
- **Use case:** Career-changers, students, first-project coordinators.

#### **2. PMP (Project Management Professional)**

- **July 2026 Exam Launch:** New format, 185 questions, 4 hours, AI + sustainability focus.
- **Eligibility (as of July 2026):** 
  - Bachelor's degree + 36 months project experience, OR
  - High school diploma + 60 months project experience.
- **Exam cost:** ~$555 USD (PMI members), ~$705 (non-members).
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewable via 60 PDU hours or exam retake.
- **Salary impact:** **33% higher median income** vs. non-certified (per PMI 14th Edition Salary Survey); US median **$122,000+** for PMP holders.
- **What changed July 2026:**
  - Predictive (Waterfall) methods: 50% → 40% of exam.
  - Adaptive/Agile + Hybrid: 50% → 60% of exam.
  - New domains: AI-assisted decision-making, value delivery, stakeholder engagement.
  - Deeper scenario blocks + graphic-based items.
  - Same credential, same salary impact, same career recognition before/after July 9, 2026.

**Source:** [PMI PMP 2026 Exam Update](https://www.pmi.org/certifications/project-management-pmp/new-exam)

#### **3. PgMP (Program Management Professional)**

- **Prerequisite:** Minimum 5 years program or project management experience.
- **Focus:** Managing multiple dependent projects, benefits realization, portfolio alignment.
- **Exam:** 170 questions, 3 hours 50 minutes.
- **Cost:** ~$500 USD (members), ~$650 (non-members).
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewable via 60 PDU hours.
- **Target roles:** Program Manager, PMO Director, Delivery Lead (50–500 headcount initiatives).

#### **4. PfMP (Portfolio Management Professional)**

- **Prerequisite:** 4 years portfolio, program, or project management experience.
- **Focus:** Strategic alignment, portfolio optimization, governance, resource allocation across the entire IT portfolio.
- **Exam:** 170 questions, 3 hours 50 minutes.
- **Cost:** ~$500 USD (members), ~$650 (non-members).
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewable via 60 PDU hours.
- **Target role:** Portfolio Manager, Chief PMO, VP Delivery (enterprise-wide steering).

#### **5. PMI-RMP (Risk Management Professional)**

- **Focus:** Qualitative + quantitative risk analysis, risk response planning.
- **Prerequisite:** 3 years risk management or 4 years project management experience.
- **Cost:** ~$450 USD (members), ~$600 (non-members).
- **Use case:** Risk leaders, senior PMs in regulated industries.

#### **6. PMI-ACP (Agile Certified Practitioner)**

- **Focus:** Scrum, Kanban, Lean, XP (Extreme Programming) foundations.
- **Exam:** 120 questions, 3 hours.
- **Cost:** ~$420 USD (members), ~$570 (non-members).
- **Target role:** Technical Project Manager, Scrum Master alternative credential.
- **Note:** Scrum.org PSM I is increasingly preferred; PMI-ACP is valid but declining market share vs. PSM.

#### **7. PMBOK Guide (8th Edition)**

- **Published:** 2024 / January 2025.
- **Format:** 408-page integrated volume (two in one: "The Standard for Project Management" + "A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge").
- **Key updates:**
  - 48,000+ data points from global research.
  - Six core principles (stewardship, value, focus, interconnectedness, engagement, tailored approach).
  - Seven performance domains (stakeholders, team, development approach, planning, work, delivery, measurement).
  - Expanded AI, PMO, and procurement guidance.
  - Non-prescriptive process guidance (flexible vs. rigid waterfall).
- **Audience:** PMP candidates, PM practitioners, organizational PMO standards.
- **Source:** [PMI PMBOK Guide 8th Edition](https://www.pmi.org/standards/pmbok)

### PMI Salary Survey (2026, 14th Edition)

**Study:** 14,628 project professionals across 21 countries, fielded March–April 2025.

| Metric | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| **US PMP median income** | $122,000 USD |
| **Non-PMP median income** | ~$91,500 USD (33% lower) |
| **Salary growth past 12 mo.** | 2/3 of certified respondents reported raises; 3/4 received up to 10% increase |
| **Certification premium** | 17–33% wage increase globally, depending on role + region |

**Source:** [PMI Earning Power Salary Survey 14th Edition](https://www.pmi.org/learning/careers/project-management-salary-survey)

## Agile Frameworks

### Scrum.org (PSM)

Scrum.org, founded by Ken Schwaber (Scrum co-creator) in 2009, publishes the official Scrum Guide and manages the Professional Scrum certifications. These are **exam-only credentials** (no mandatory training required).

**URL:** https://www.scrum.org/professional-scrum-certifications

#### **PSM I (Professional Scrum Master I)**

- **Exam:** 80 questions, 60 minutes, multiple-choice / multiple-answer / true-false.
- **Passing score:** 85%.
- **Cost:** $200 USD per attempt.
- **Validity:** Lifetime (no renewal).
- **Focus:** Scrum framework mastery, understanding roles (SM, PO, Team), events (Sprint, Standup, Review, Retro), artifacts (Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment).
- **Target:** First-time Scrum Masters, career changers (no prerequisite experience).
- **Study:** Scrum Guide (free), practice assessments (free on Scrum.org).

#### **PSM II (Professional Scrum Master II)**

- **Exam:** 30 questions, 90 minutes, multiple-choice / multiple-answer / true-false.
- **Passing score:** 85%.
- **Cost:** $250 USD per attempt.
- **Prerequisite:** Typically 1–2 years practical Scrum team experience.
- **Focus:** Coaching teams, handling organizational dysfunction, scaling Scrum to multi-team situations, advanced servant leadership.
- **Target:** Mid-career Scrum Masters, team leads transitioning to SM role.

#### **PSM III (Professional Scrum Master III)**

- **Exam:** 34 questions, 120 minutes, **majority written-answer (graded by humans)**.
- **Passing score:** 85%.
- **Cost:** $500 USD per attempt.
- **Prerequisite:** 5+ years Scrum leadership; typically PSM I + PSM II holders.
- **Focus:** Thought leadership, implementing Scrum across organizational contexts, complex system-level problem-solving.
- **Target:** Senior Scrum Masters, Agile coaches, enterprise transformation leads.

**Source:** [Scrum.org Professional Scrum Master Certifications](https://www.scrum.org/professional-scrum-certifications/professional-scrum-master-assessments)

#### **PSPO I / II / III (Product Owner)**

- **Equivalent pathway:** PSPO I (foundational product ownership) → PSPO II (coaching teams, stakeholder management) → PSPO III (thought leadership).
- **Exam costs:** $200 (I), $250 (II), $500 (III).
- **Validity:** Lifetime.
- **Focus:** Backlog management, ROI + value delivery, market research, roadmap negotiation.

#### **Other Scrum.org Credentials**

| Credential | Focus | Cost | Validity |
|------------|-------|------|----------|
| **PSK** | Scrum + Kanban (pull systems, WIP limits) | $200 | Lifetime |
| **PSU** | Scrum + UX/design (user research, design sprints) | $200 | Lifetime |
| **PSPBM** | Product Backlog Management (advanced PO skills) | $200 | Lifetime |
| **PAL-EBM** | Agile Leadership (evidence-based management + metrics) | $200 | Lifetime |

**Source:** [Scrum.org Professional Scrum Certifications](https://www.scrum.org/professional-scrum-certifications)

### Scrum Alliance

Scrum Alliance, founded by Mike Cohn and other Agile pioneers in 2001, provides **trainer-led certification pathways** with hands-on Agile coaching emphasis. Unlike Scrum.org's exam-only model, Scrum Alliance requires classroom attendance and coach interaction.

**URL:** https://www.scrumalliance.org/

#### **CSM (Certified ScrumMaster)**

- **Requirement:** 2-day in-person or online training with a **Certified Scrum Trainer (CST)**.
- **Exam:** Pass/fail (administered by trainer); no separate exam fee.
- **Cost:** ~$500–$1200 USD (training course + exam included).
- **Validity:** 2 years.
- **Renewal:** 20 Scrum Education Units (SEUs) + $100 renewal fee per 2-year term.
  - SEU earned via: attending events, webinars, volunteering, reading, conference attendance.
  - 1 hour of continuing education = 1 SEU.
- **Advantage:** Cohort learning, hands-on coaching, networking.
- **Target:** Agile practitioners, career-changers, team leads.

#### **CSPO (Certified Scrum Product Owner)**

- **Requirement:** 2-day CST-led training.
- **Exam:** None (certification awarded upon course completion).
- **Cost:** ~$500–$1200 USD.
- **Validity:** 2 years.
- **Renewal:** 20 SEUs + $100 per 2-year term.
- **Focus:** Product vision, backlog prioritization, stakeholder management, value delivery.
- **Note:** Unlike PSPO (Scrum.org), CSPO does **not** require passing a written exam; completion of the course = certification.

#### **A-CSM (Advanced Certified ScrumMaster) & A-CSPO**

- **Prerequisite:** Hold CSM (or CSPO) from Scrum Alliance; **can be active or expired**.
- **Format:** Advanced 1-day workshop or mentored coaching path.
- **Cost:** ~$400–$600 USD per track.
- **Validity:** 2 years; same 20 SEU renewal.
- **Target:** Experienced Scrum Masters stepping into coaching, mentoring, enterprise roles.

#### **CSP-SM, CSP-PO (Certified Scrum Professional)**

- **Prerequisite:** 2+ years post-CSM/CSPO experience + current active status.
- **Requirement:** Continuing education portfolio (case study, coaching narrative, experience evidence).
- **Cost:** ~$250 USD application fee.
- **Validity:** 2 years; 40 SEUs + $100 renewal.
- **Target:** Senior Scrum Masters, Staff-level product managers, organizational coaches.

**Source:** [Scrum Alliance Certifications](https://www.scrumalliance.org/get-certified), [Scrum Alliance Renewal](https://www.scrumalliance.org/get-certified/renewing-certifications)

### SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)

SAFe, maintained by Scaled Agile, Inc., is the **most widely adopted enterprise-scale Agile framework** with 20,000+ organizations across 120 countries using it. SAFe manages Agile at three levels: Team, Program (Agile Release Train), and Portfolio.

### PRINCE2 & Traditional Project Management

PRINCE2 (Projects In Controlled Environments) is a **process-driven, document-heavy project management methodology** favored in UK government, NHS, and traditional industries (construction, manufacturing). It emphasizes strict governance, stage-gates, and formal controls.

**URL:** https://www.peoplecert.org/ | https://www.axelos.com/

#### **PRINCE2 Version 7 (Current)**

- **Foundation:** 1-day course + 1-hour exam (80 multiple-choice questions, 50% passing score).
  - **Cost:** ~$300–$500 USD.
  - **Validity:** Lifetime (no renewal).
  - **Focus:** PRINCE2 principles, themes (organization, business case, quality, risk, change, progress, plans), processes.
  
- **Practitioner:** 2-day course + 2.5-hour exam (60 scenario-based questions, 50% passing score).
  - **Prerequisite:** PRINCE2 Foundation (or equivalent knowledge).
  - **Cost:** ~$600–$1000 USD.
  - **Validity:** 3 years (requires renewal via CPD or retake).
  - **Focus:** Applying PRINCE2 to real projects, handling exceptions, tailoring to context.

**Version History:**
- **Version 6** (2009–2020): Retired but still held by many practitioners.
- **Version 7** (2021–present): Updated for modern teams, digital projects, emphasizes tailoring and integration with Agile.
- **PRINCE2 Agile** (retiring 2026): Formal bridge between PRINCE2 process rigor and Agile flexibility. Being phased out in favor of PRINCE2 7's native Agile integration.

**Source:** [PRINCE2 Certifications](https://www.peoplecert.org/browse-certifications/project-programme-and-portfolio-management/PRINCE2-2), [PRINCE2 Version 7 Update](https://www.axelos.com/certifications/propath/prince2-project-management/)

## IT Service Management (ITIL)

### Overview

ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library) is a **process framework for IT operations and service delivery**, maintained by AXELOS / PeopleCert. ITIL v4 (2019–present) shifted from prescriptive processes to **flexible practices** and value streams.

**URL:** https://www.peoplecert.org/browse-certifications/it-governance-and-service-management/ITIL-1

### ITIL 4 Certification Pathway

#### **ITIL 4 Foundation**

- **Exam:** 40 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes.
- **Passing score:** 65% (26/40 correct).
- **Cost:** ~$150–$250 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years; must renew via higher-level certification or CPD.
- **Focus:** ITIL 4 guiding principles, service value system, 4 dimensions (organizations/people, information/technology, partners/suppliers, value streams/processes).
- **Target:** IT professionals entering service management, first step for IT Managers and Help Desk supervisors.

#### **ITIL 4 Specialist (5 tracks)**

- **Prerequisite:** ITIL 4 Foundation.
- **Cost:** ~$200–$400 USD per exam.
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewable via higher certification or CPD.
- **Specialist tracks:**
  1. **Create, Deliver & Support** – design, transition, operations.
  2. **High Velocity IT** – continuous delivery, Lean, Agile practices.
  3. **Drive Stakeholder Value** – service strategy, value realization.
  4. **Monitor, Support & Optimize** – incident, problem, optimization practices.
  5. **Plan, Implement & Improve** – change, planning, improvement frameworks.

#### **ITIL 4 Strategic Leader**

- **Prerequisite:** ITIL 4 Foundation + any Specialist.
- **Cost:** ~$300–$500 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years.
- **Exam:** 90-minute essay/short-answer format (graded by ITIL assessors).
- **Focus:** Enterprise strategy, board-level governance, digital transformation, CIO-level thinking.

#### **ITIL 4 Master**

- **Prerequisite:** ITIL 4 Foundation + ITIL 4 Strategic Leader + 2–3 years ITIL practice experience.
- **Cost:** ~$500–$1000 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years.
- **Exam:** Portfolio-based assessment (practical projects, organizational case studies, integration across ITIL practices).
- **Target:** ITIL Consultants, Service Owners, IT Directors.

### 2026 Status: ITIL v4 vs. ITIL v5

- **ITIL v4 Foundation:** Still fully valid, widely taught, no retirement date announced.
- **ITIL Version 5:** Announced February 2026 with AI-native guidance, digital experience management, sustainability focus.
- **Transition path:** ITIL 4 Foundation acceptable as prerequisite for ITIL v5 advanced certifications.
- **Market takeaway:** ITIL Foundation remains "still relevant in the right contexts"—not universal must-have, but valuable for IT Operations, Service Desk, and IT Governance roles.

**Source:** [ITIL 4 Certifications](https://www.peoplecert.org/browse-certifications/it-governance-and-service-management/ITIL-1/itil-4-foundation-2565), [ITIL Status 2026](https://techjacksolutions.com/itil/v4-foundation/itil-v4/)

## Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC)

### Overview

GRC is the **three-pillar framework** for managing organizational integrity:
- **Governance:** Board oversight, strategic alignment, compliance frameworks.
- **Risk:** Quantification, mitigation, monitoring (operational + strategic).
- **Compliance:** Regulatory adherence, audit, policy enforcement.

GRC roles sit at the **intersection of IT, legal, audit, and business strategy**.

### ISACA Credentials (GRC + Audit Focus)

ISACA (Information Systems Audit and Control Association) manages 5+ certifications bridging IT audit, governance, security, and risk.

**URL:** https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/certifications

#### **CISA (Certified Information Systems Auditor)**

- **Exam:** 150 questions, 4 hours, multiple-choice.
- **Cost:** ~$300–$500 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewal via 120 CPE hours or exam retake.
- **Prerequisite:** 5 years IT audit/security experience (or less with advanced degree + ITIL/CISM/CRISC).
- **Focus:** Auditing IT systems, controls, governance, risk management, compliance, incident investigation.
- **Salary impact:** **US $149,000+ average** per ISACA data.
- **Target roles:** IT Auditor, Internal Audit Manager, Compliance Officer, GRC Analyst.
- **Holders:** 151,000+ globally.

#### **CISM (Certified Information Security Manager)**

- **Exam:** 150 questions, 4 hours, multiple-choice.
- **Cost:** ~$300–$500 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewal via 120 CPE hours.
- **Prerequisite:** 5 years security management / 2 years CISM + 3 years related experience.
- **Focus:** Information security governance, program development, incident management, risk management at department level.
- **Key 2026 update:** Exam Content Outline updated **November 3, 2026**; expect new question types reflecting modern security challenges (cloud, mobile, remote work, AI).
- **Target roles:** Information Security Manager, Security Governance Manager, Risk Officer, Chief Security Officer.

#### **CGEIT (Certified in Governance of Enterprise IT)**

- **Exam:** 150 questions, 4 hours, multiple-choice.
- **Cost:** ~$300–$500 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewal via 120 CPE hours.
- **Prerequisite:** 4 years IT governance / risk management experience.
- **Focus:** Assessing, designing, implementing, managing enterprise IT governance systems **aligned with business goals** (framework-agnostic: not ITIL-specific, not COBIT-specific, but principles-based).
- **Salary impact:** **US $141,000+ average** per ISACA; **11–20% cash pay premium growth** (Q3–Q4 2025 data).
- **Target roles:** IT Director, Chief Information Officer, IT Governance Manager, Enterprise Architect.
- **Unique value:** **Only IT governance certification** that is framework-agnostic; teaches assessing + designing custom governance models.

#### **CRISC (Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control)**

- **Exam:** 150 questions, 4 hours, multiple-choice.
- **Cost:** ~$300–$500 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewal via 120 CPE hours.
- **Focus:** IT risk assessment, control selection, risk response, risk monitoring, IT governance integration.
- **Target:** Risk Managers, Control Specialists, IT Risk Analysts, GRC leads.

#### **2026 Market Outlook**

Per Foote Partners' Q4 2025 data, **CISA and CGEIT posted 11–20% cash pay premium growth** over the prior six months, significantly outpacing the 6.5% certification industry average. This signals strong demand for IT audit + governance leaders.

**New 2026:** ISACA launched **Advanced in AI Risk (AAIR) certification**, targeting IT risk professionals implementing AI governance, model risk, prompt engineering controls, and responsible AI frameworks.

**Source:** [ISACA Certifications](https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/certifications), [CISA](https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/cisa), [CISM](https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/cism), [CGEIT](https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/cgeit), [2026 Market Outlook](https://www.isaca.org/resources/news-and-trends/isaca-now-blog/2026/why-cisa-and-cgeit-are-outperforming-the-certification-market-in-2026)

### COBIT Framework (ISACA)

COBIT (Control Objectives for Information Technology) is a **governance + control framework** complementary to ITIL. ITIL manages operations; COBIT manages governance + controls.

#### **COBIT Foundation Certificate**

- **Exam:** Computer-based, continuous registration (no fixed windows).
- **Cost:** ~$250 USD.
- **Validity:** Lifetime (no renewal).
- **Focus:** COBIT 2019 framework, governance principles, governance systems, design methodology.
- **Prerequisite:** None.
- **Target:** IT Managers, GRC Analysts, IT Auditors seeking governance grounding.

#### **COBIT Design & Implementation Certificate**

- **Exam:** Computer-based, continuous registration.
- **Cost:** ~$300 USD.
- **Validity:** Lifetime.
- **Focus:** Designing, implementing COBIT governance models at enterprise scale.
- **Prerequisite:** Recommended (not required): COBIT Foundation.
- **Target:** IT Governance Designers, Enterprise Architects, Transformation leads.

**Source:** [COBIT Foundation](https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/cobit-foundation), [COBIT Design & Implementation](https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/cobit-design-and-implementation)

### Open FAIR (Risk Quantification)

Open FAIR (Factor Analysis of Information Risk), maintained by The Open Group, provides a **quantitative methodology** for risk analysis and communication with executives and boards.

**URL:** https://www.opengroup.org/certifications/openfair

#### **Open FAIR Foundation Certification**

- **Requirement:** 6 Level 1 Learning Units (self-study or instructor-led) + Part 1 exam.
- **Exam:** Delivered by Pearson VUE at test centers.
- **Cost:** ~$300–$500 USD.
- **Validity:** **Lifetime (no renewal, no CPE requirement).**
- **Focus:** FAIR terminology, taxonomy, risk analysis methodology, communicating risk to boards.
- **Target:** Risk Managers, GRC Analysts, Chief Risk Officers, CISO teams.
- **Holders:** 1,700+ certified; growing.
- **Advantage:** Unlike most certifications, **no annual fees or CPE burden**—knowledge is considered durable once validated.

**Source:** [Open FAIR Certification](https://www.opengroup.org/certifications/openfair)

## Internal Audit (IIA)

### CIA (Certified Internal Auditor)

The Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA) manages the **CIA**, the gold standard for internal auditors across all industries (not IT-specific, but widely held by IT audit professionals).

**URL:** https://www.theiia.org/en/certifications/cia/

#### **Overview**

- **Established:** 1974; 220,000+ CIAs from 170 countries.
- **Exam:** 125 questions per section (3 parts, 4 hours each); multiple-choice format.
- **Cost:** ~$500–$700 USD per section.
- **Validity:** 2 years; renewal via 40 CPE hours per year.
- **Prerequisite:** 1 year internal audit or equivalent experience.

#### **IT Audit Specialization: CIA Challenge Exam for Information Systems**

- **For:** CISA holders transitioning to internal audit.
- **Format:** Exam waiving PART 1 (Foundation) if you hold active CISA.
- **2026 Update:** As of **June 1, 2026**, the Information Systems CIA Challenge Exam updated to align with new Global Internal Audit Standards.
- **Cost:** ~$250 USD (discounted from full CIA).
- **Target:** IT Auditors moving into broader internal audit careers.

#### **2026 Updates & New Pathways**

- **Experienced Professional Pathway (PILOT):** For professionals with 10+ years internal audit experience.
  - **Available:** June, September, November 2026 testing windows.
  - **Application window:** April 1–September 30, 2026.
  - **Advantage:** Faster certification for senior practitioners.

- **April 1, 2026 Exam Scoring Update:** Official results now delivered within 3 weeks (previously longer).

**Source:** [IIA CIA Certification](https://www.theiia.org/en/certifications/cia/), [CIA Information Systems Challenge](https://www.theiia.org/en/certifications/cia/challenge-exam/information-systems/), [2026 Updates](https://www.theiia.org/en/certifications/)

## DevOps & Lean Six Sigma

### DASA DevOps Certifications

DASA (DevOps Agile Skills Association) provides **DevOps-specific credentials** focused on culture, practices, and tooling across modern software delivery.

**URL:** https://www.dasa.org/products/talent-products/certification-programs/

#### **DASA DevOps Fundamentals**

- **Exam:** 60 minutes, multiple-choice (65% passing score).
- **Cost:** ~$150–$250 USD.
- **Validity:** **Lifetime (no renewal).**
- **Focus:** DevOps principles, culture, practices, automation, CI/CD, collaboration mindset.
- **Prerequisite:** None; recommended Agile/Scrum/Lean/ITSM awareness.
- **Target:** Engineers, operations staff, tech leads entering DevOps roles.

#### **Advanced DASA Tracks (2026+)**

- **DASA DevAIOps:** AI-driven automation, optimization, decision-making in DevOps pipelines.
- **DASA DevOps Specify & Verify:** Testing integration, security, compliance in delivery pipelines.
- **DASA DevOps Enable & Scale:** Building high-performing teams, cross-team collaboration.
- **Cost:** ~$300–$500 USD per exam.
- **Validity:** Lifetime.

**Source:** [DASA DevOps Fundamentals](https://www.dasa.org/products/talent-products/certification-programs/dasa-devops-fundamentals/)

### Lean Six Sigma (ASQ)

ASQ (American Society for Quality) manages **Six Sigma belt certifications** for process improvement and operational excellence.

**URL:** https://www.asq.org/cert/six-sigma

#### **Yellow Belt**

- **Focus:** Awareness, support on improvement projects.
- **Exam:** 50 questions, 2 hours.
- **Cost:** ~$200 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years.

#### **Green Belt**

- **Focus:** Leading small-scope improvement projects, DMAIC methodology.
- **Exam:** 100 questions, 4 hours.
- **Cost:** ~$300 USD.
- **Validity:** 3 years.

#### **Black Belt (CSSBB)**

- **Exam:** 150 questions, 4.5 hours.
- **Cost:** $585 (ASQ members: $485); retakes $385.
- **Validity:** 3 years; renewal via 30 CPE hours or exam retake.
- **Prerequisite:** 3 years full-time work experience + **minimum 1 completed Six Sigma project** with affidavit, OR 2 projects with signed affidavits.
- **Focus:** Leading large, strategic improvement initiatives, data analysis, statistical tools, leadership.
- **Test windows:** May, August, November (CBT 5/1–5/31, 8/1–8/31, etc.).
- **Credential:** ANSI-accredited (ISO 17024 standard).
- **Target:** Operations Managers, Quality Directors, Process Improvement Leaders, IT operations analysts.

#### **Master Black Belt (MBB)**

- **Prerequisite:** Black Belt + 5 years continuous improvement leadership.
- **Focus:** Strategic improvement programs, mentoring Black Belts, organizational transformation.
- **Cost:** ~$600–$800 USD.

**Source:** [ASQ Six Sigma Certifications](https://www.asq.org/cert/six-sigma-black-belt)

## Career Ladders

### Ladder 1: Individual Contributor → Manager → Director → CIO

```
IC Project Coordinator (CAPM, 0–2 yrs)
  ↓
Project Manager (PMP, 3–8 yrs)
  ↓
Senior PM / Delivery Lead (PgMP, 8–12 yrs)
  ↓
Director of PMO / VP Delivery (PfMP, 12–18 yrs)
  ↓
Chief Information Officer (MBA/Executive cert, 18+ yrs)
```

**Time to C-suite:** 15–20 years from IC. Requires:
- Progression through PMP → PgMP → PfMP (or PRINCE2 Practitioner at enterprise level).
- MBA or Executive Leadership Program (optional but common).
- Board/executive presence (public speaking, stakeholder management).

---

### Ladder 2: Scrum Master / Agile Coach Track

```
Scrum Master (PSM I or CSM, 0–2 yrs)
  ↓
Senior Scrum Master / Team Coach (PSM II or A-CSM, 3–5 yrs)
  ↓
SAFe Release Train Engineer / Program Coach (RTE, 5–8 yrs)
  ↓
SAFe Practice Consultant / Enterprise Agile Coach (SPC, 8+ yrs)
  ↓
Chief Agile Officer / Transformation Officer (Executive, 12+ yrs)
```

**Key inflection:** PSM II / A-CSM signals readiness for coaching. RTE signals program-level coordination. SPC = consulting/influencer tier.

---

### Ladder 3: IT Operations → IT Management → IT Director

```
Help Desk / Support Center Analyst (HDI-SCA, 1–3 yrs)
  ↓
IT Manager / Service Desk Manager (ITIL 4 Foundation, 5–7 yrs)
  ↓
ITSM Manager / Operations Manager (ITIL 4 Specialist or Master, 8–12 yrs)
  ↓
IT Director (ITIL 4 Master + CGEIT, 12–18 yrs)
  ↓
Chief Information Officer (CGEIT + executive cert, 18+ yrs)
```

**Parallel track:** Add CISA or CRISC at Manager level to pivot toward GRC/Audit leadership.

---

### Ladder 4: IT Auditor → Chief Audit Officer

```
IT Auditor / Compliance Analyst (CISA, 5–8 yrs)
  ↓
Senior IT Auditor / IT Audit Manager (CISA + CIA challenge, 8–12 yrs)
  ↓
Director of IT Audit / Chief Audit Executive (CIA + CGEIT, 12–18 yrs)
  ↓
Chief Audit Officer / VP Risk & Compliance (Board-level, 18+ yrs)
```

**Certification sequence:** CISA (IT-specific) → CIA challenge exam (general audit credentials) → CGEIT (governance + risk lens for C-suite).

---

### Ladder 5: Risk Manager → Chief Risk Officer

```
Risk Analyst / GRC Analyst (CRISC, 3–5 yrs)
  ↓
Risk Manager / Compliance Officer (CRISC + CGEIT, 5–10 yrs)
  ↓
Director of Risk & Compliance (CGEIT + Open FAIR, 10–15 yrs)
  ↓
Chief Risk Officer (Executive fellowship, 15+ yrs)
```

**Parallel credentials:** Add CISM for security governance; Open FAIR for quantitative risk communication.

## Common Hard & Soft Skills (IT Management Domain)

### Hard Skills

**Project & Program Management**
- PMBOK processes and practice (10 knowledge areas, 5 process groups or 7 domains for v8)
- Scope, schedule, budget, quality, resource, risk, procurement, stakeholder management
- RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) matrices
- Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) design
- Earned Value Management (EVM)
- Critical Path analysis

**Agile & Iterative**
- Scrum framework (roles, ceremonies, artifacts)
- Agile estimation (story points, Planning Poker, velocity)
- Kanban / Lean principles (WIP limits, flow, continuous improvement)
- SAFe framework (PI planning, ART orchestration, synchronization)
- Sprint planning, backlog refinement, retrospectives

**IT Service Management (ITIL)**
- ITIL value streams (design, transition, operation, improvement, engagement)
- Incident, problem, change, configuration management
- Service Desk operations, escalation, SLA management
- IT asset management (ITAM)
- Demand management

**Governance, Risk & Compliance**
- Risk assessment methodologies (qualitative + quantitative)
- COBIT governance framework (governance systems, design methodology)
- Control objectives and maturity models
- Audit planning and execution
- Regulatory compliance (SOX, HIPAA, GDPR, ISO 27001, CIS Controls)
- Board-level reporting and risk communication

**DevOps & Continuous Improvement**
- CI/CD pipeline design and monitoring
- Lean Six Sigma (DMAIC: Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control)
- Value stream mapping
- Metrics and KPIs (deployment frequency, lead time, MTTR, change failure rate)

### Soft Skills

- Stakeholder engagement and influence (managing up, lateral, and down)
- Executive communication and presentation (C-suite readiness)
- Conflict resolution and difficult conversations
- Team coaching and mentoring
- Change management and organizational psychology
- Negotiation (vendor, budget, scope)
- Problem-solving under ambiguity and constraints
