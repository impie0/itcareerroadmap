# Free University Courses & Academic Resources for IT Professionals

A comprehensive guide to world-class computer science education, organized by domain. All courses listed here are **freely accessible** (either fully free, free audit, or with open materials). Most originate from top universities: MIT, Stanford, UC Berkeley, Carnegie Mellon, Princeton, and others.

---

## FOUNDATION / COMPUTER SCIENCE

### Harvard CS50 - Introduction to Computer Science
- **University:** Harvard University
- **Course Code:** CS50
- **Course Name:** Introduction to Computer Science
- **URL:** https://cs50.harvard.edu/x/
- **Format:** Video lectures + labs + problem sets
- **Access:** Free on edX (paid certificate optional)
- **Estimated Time:** 50-100 hours
- **Difficulty:** Beginner to Intermediate
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Why Take It:** The world's most popular intro CS course. Covers fundamentals across languages (C, Python, SQL, HTML, CSS, JavaScript). Excellent entry point for career changers and professionals entering tech. Strong emphasis on problem-solving and practical coding skills.

---

### MIT 6.0001 - Introduction to Computer Science and Programming in Python
- **University:** MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology)
- **Course Code:** 6.0001
- **Course Name:** Introduction to Computer Science and Programming in Python
- **URL:** https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-0001-introduction-to-computer-science-and-programming-in-python-fall-2016/
- **Format:** Video lectures, problem sets, exams
- **Access:** Fully free on MIT OpenCourseWare
- **Estimated Time:** 60-80 hours (half-semester)

- **Course Code:** CS162

## HOW TO USE THIS GUIDE

### For Career Changers / Self-Taught Engineers
1. **Start with Foundation:** Harvard CS50 or MIT 6.0001
2. **Build Data Structures:** Princeton Algorithms I & II
3. **Systems Core:** Operating Systems (MIT 6.S081) → Networking (Stanford CS144) → Databases (CMU 15-445)
4. **Choose Your Path:**
   - **Systems Engineer:** Add 6.5840 (Distributed Systems) + 6.858 (Security)
   - **Backend Engineer:** Add 15-445 (Databases) + principles of software engineering
   - **ML Engineer:** Add Stanford CS229 + CS231n or CS224n
   - **Security:** Add 6.858 + CS255 (Cryptography) + CS155

### For In-Depth Self-Study
- Follow **OSSU Computer Science** curriculum or **Teach Yourself CS** guidance
- Complete all 9 core subjects
- Time commitment: 1.5-2 years at 20 hrs/week

### For Specialists
- Pick domain-specific courses (ML engineers → CS229 + CS231n + fast.ai)
- Use free textbooks for deep dives
- Supplement with research papers (distributed systems, databases, cryptography)

### For Career Accelerators
- **IT Ops / Systems:** 6.S081, CS144, CMU 15-445, MIT 6.5840
- **Security Engineering:** MIT 6.858, Stanford CS255, Stanford CS155, CMU SEI publications
- **Data & ML:** CS229, CS231n, CS224n, fast.ai
- **Backend / Full-Stack:** CS50, CS61A (Berkeley), MIT 6.005, CMU 15-445, optional CS143

## PRACTICAL LEARNING STRATEGY

1. **Do the Labs:** Every course listed includes hands-on projects. Projects are where learning sticks.
   - CS144: Implement TCP/IP
   - CMU 15-445: Build a database system
   - MIT 6.5840: Implement Raft consensus
   - Stanford CS143: Implement a compiler

2. **Read the Papers:** Distributed systems, databases, and security courses assign research papers. Read them.

3. **Build Things:** After courses, apply knowledge to real projects.

4. **Join Communities:** OSSU has Discord. MIT and Stanford courses have Reddit communities. Engage.

5. **Track Progress:** Create a learning portfolio. Share code, write blog posts, contribute to open-source projects that use course concepts.
